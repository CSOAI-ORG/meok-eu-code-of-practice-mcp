## The Core Insight

**RegGeoInt is not an AI product. It is a pattern.**

The pattern: Every technology that crosses a border needs a compliance map. Every industry with fragmented regulations needs geospatial intelligence. Every deployment to multiple jurisdictions needs automated gap detection.

AI was the first target because it's the fastest-regulating technology in history. But the **engine** — geospatial regulatory mapping + cross-border advisory + cryptographic audit receipts — applies to **any regulated cross-border activity**.

## Ranking Rationale

### #1: Crypto / Web3 (Attack First)
**Why #1:**
- **Fastest regulatory velocity:** Laws change weekly. Maps need constant updates = recurring revenue.
- **Highest customer urgency:** MiCA enforcement, SEC enforcement, exchange collapses = panic buying.
- **Technical reuse:** 90% of AI compliance engine applies. Just swap jurisdiction data.
- **Customer overlap:** Crypto founders are AI-native. Same buyers.
- **Content moat:** Crypto regulations are poorly documented. First to map = permanent reference.

**Revenue potential:** $72.5M/year addressable
**Go-live:** 60 days (Q3 2026)

### #2: Supply Chain ESG (Attack Second)
**Why #2:**
- **Immediate enforcement:** CBAM (2026), EUDR (2024), UFLPA (2022) all enforcing NOW.
- **Massive market:** $25T trade volume. Compliance = cost of doing business.
- **Enterprise buyers:** Procurement officers have budgets. Not startups.
- **Technical reuse:** 85% engine reuse. Tariff codes + ESG rules as data layer.
- **Partnership ready:** Sedex, EcoVadis, Assent already have customers.

**Revenue potential:** $62.5M/year addressable
**Go-live:** 60 days (Q3 2026, parallel with crypto)

### #3: Autonomous Systems (Attack Third)
**Why #3:**
- **Regulatory wave building:** AVs, drones, autonomous ships all approaching commercial scale.
- **3D complexity:** Adds altitude/depth dimension to geospatial map. Unique moat.
- **Defense crossover:** Military drones = same tech, 10x budget.
- **Technical reuse:** 80% engine reuse. Vehicle-type classifications as data layer.
- **Longer timeline:** 90 days to build 3D regulatory map.

**Revenue potential:** $19M/year addressable
**Go-live:** 90 days (Q4 2026)

### #4: Biotech / Genomics (Attack Fourth)
**Why #4:**
- **High value per customer:** Pharma pays $1M/year. Highest ARPU of all gaps.
- **Ethical intensity:** Gene editing = most emotionally regulated tech after nuclear.
- **Technical reuse:** 75% engine reuse. Clinical trial protocols as data layer.
- **Longer sales cycle:** 12-18 months. Requires regulatory affairs relationships.
- **Data complexity:** Gene therapy pathways are poorly documented publicly.

**Revenue potential:** $44.5M/year addressable
**Go-live:** 90 days (Q4 2026)

### #5: Space (Attack Fifth)
**Why #5:**
- **Smallest near-term market:** $27.5M addressable. Long sales cycles.
- **Highest long-term potential:** $500B space economy. Compliance layer = 1-2% = $5-10B.
- **Technical complexity:** ITU coordination + orbital mechanics + spectrum physics.
- **120-day build:** Longest data layer construction.
- **Strategic positioning:** Category creation. "The map of space law."

**Revenue potential:** $27.5M/year addressable (near-term)
**Go-live:** 120 days (Q1 2027)

## Competitive Defense

### The Data Moat
Each vertical adds proprietary data that competitors cannot easily replicate:
- **Crypto:** Exchange licensing status, sanctions lists, hack incident database
- **Supply Chain:** Supplier-origin risk scores, tariff classification mappings
- **Autonomous:** City-level drone ordinances, port authority rules
- **Biotech:** Clinical trial approval timelines, ethics committee contacts
- **Space:** ITU filing status, frequency coordination arcs, orbital slot assignments

### The Network Effect
As more verticals launch, cross-vertical customers emerge:
- A pharmaceutical company uses AI (drug discovery) + biotech (trials) + supply chain (shipping)
- A defense contractor uses autonomous (drones) + space (satellites) + AI (targeting)
- Each cross-vertical customer has 3x LTV of single-vertical customer

### The Brand Moat
"RegGeoInt" becomes the generic term for geospatial regulatory compliance. Like "Google" for search, "Uber" for rideshare, "RegGeoInt" for compliance maps.
