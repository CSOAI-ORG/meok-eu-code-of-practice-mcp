What we learned

  1. Article 50 cliff is real, but awareness is low. 12,000+ UK AI companies in scope. Most have not started. The awareness gap is the biggest problem, not the tooling.
  2. Big-4 quotes are the buyer's biggest objection. £40-80K for 8 weeks feels like the only option. Showing the £4,950 / 4-day alternative in the same sentence removes the objection.
  3. Ed25519 > PDF is a hard sell but a real differentiator. Most buyers don't understand machine-verifiable certificates until they need one (regulator request, RFP answer, supply chain question). The differentiator becomes visible at the moment of pain.
  4. Sovereign UK matters for the buyer persona but not for the buyer's CFO. CTOs care about sovereignty. CFOs care about price. The pitch has to lead with the CFO hook (£4,950 vs £40-80K) and then escalate to the CTO hook (sovereign UK, open source tooling).
  5. CRMs are an expensive distraction. Pipeline is 25 qualified prospects + 95 staged emails. The next 1 customer comes from the next 1 outbound, not the next 1 feature. A CRM would add friction.
  6. Open source is a moat. 32 MCP servers, MIT licensed. The buyer can fork, run, audit the wire format. Big-4 + Holistic AI + OneTrust + Vanta + Drata + Secureframe are all closed. That's the real differentiator.
  7. 100% Nick is the narrative. Not VC-backed. Not acquirable. No exit pressure. The buyer trusts the founder. The founder trusts the math.
