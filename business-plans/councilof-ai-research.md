## Positioning
**DO NOT** compete with OneTrust/IAPP head-on.
**Niche:** Regulation-specific tooling + open MCP ecosystem.
Think "Stripe for AI compliance" not "OneTrust lite."
Dedicated AI governance platforms deliver 3.4x higher effectiveness than bolted-on GRC.

## Revenue Model
1. Certification exams ($650-800)
2. Annual membership ($200-300)
3. Training courses ($900-3,500)
4. Consulting/assessment engagements
5. SaaS platform subscriptions (tiered)
6. Regulatory update content (newsletters/reports behind paywall)

**Missing:** Flagship certification credential + recurring community membership

## SWOT

**Strengths:** 15+ live MCP tools, attestation API, open-source credibility, regulation-specific depth
**Weaknesses:** Zero revenue, no brand recognition, solo operator, no accreditation status
**Opportunities:** EU AI Act deadlines driving panic buying, ISO 42001 surge, SME underserved, MCP network effects
**Threats:** IAPP expanding into AI governance, OneTrust adding AI modules, timeline shifts, free gov guidance

## Key Partnerships
Target: ISO 42001 cert bodies (Palindrome, LRQA), EU AI Act consultancies, NIS2 MSSPs in DACH, AI safety orgs (AISI UK)

## Content Strategy
GRC buyers want technical precision, not marketing. Prioritize:
- Regulation deadline trackers (free lead magnets)
- Compliance gap assessment tools (interactive)
- Case studies showing ROI/time savings
- Regulatory change alerts

Avoid buzzword content. Buyer = DPO/CISO/GRC manager who wants checklists and tooling.
