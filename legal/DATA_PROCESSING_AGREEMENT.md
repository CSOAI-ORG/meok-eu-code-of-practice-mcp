# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) — MEOK AI Labs

**Last Updated:** 2026-05-09
**Incorporated into:** Terms of Service

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between MEOK AI Labs ("Processor," "we," "us") and the customer ("Controller," "you"). This DPA applies where we process personal data on your behalf as part of providing our Services.

## 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined here have the meaning given in the Terms of Service.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (as defined in UK GDPR Art. 4(1)) |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on personal data (as defined in UK GDPR Art. 4(2)) |
| **Sub-Processor** | Any third party engaged by Processor to process personal data on behalf of Controller |
| **Data Subject** | The identified or identifiable natural person to whom personal data relates |
| **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** | The standard contractual clauses for data transfer adopted by the European Commission or UK Government |

## 2. Subject Matter and Duration

### 2.1 Subject Matter
The processing concerns personal data submitted by Controller through the Services for the purpose of providing compliance assessments, MCP server tools, API access, and related services.

### 2.2 Duration
This DPA is effective for the duration of your subscription to the Services and will terminate automatically upon termination of the Terms of Service. Upon termination, we will delete or return all personal data within 90 days.

### 2.3 Nature and Purpose
Processing is for the purpose of providing the Services as described in the Terms of Service and this DPA.

## 3. Types of Personal Data

Controller determines the categories of personal data processed. Typically, this may include:
- Identification data (name, email, job title)
- Professional contact information
- Compliance-related documents and assessments
- Usage and technical data

## 4. Categories of Data Subjects

Controller determines the categories of data subjects. Typically, these include:
- Controller's employees and contractors
- Controller's customers and end-users
- Individuals referenced in compliance assessments

## 5. Processor Obligations

We agree to:

### 5.1 Process Only on Documented Instructions
Process personal data only on your documented instructions, including with regard to transfers of personal data to third countries, unless required by applicable law.

### 5.2 Confidentiality
Ensure persons authorised to process personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

### 5.3 Security
Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures as described in Appendix A (Security Measures).

### 5.4 Sub-Processors
Not engage Sub-Processors without your general written authorisation. We maintain a list of Sub-Processors (Appendix B) and will notify you of any intended changes. You may object to new Sub-Processors within 14 days.

### 5.5 Data Subject Rights
Assist you in responding to data subject requests, to the extent possible, taking into account the nature of the processing.

### 5.6 Breach Notification
Notify you without undue delay (within 48 hours) of any personal data breach affecting your data.

### 5.7 Data Protection Impact Assessments
Assist you in conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) and prior consultations with supervisory authorities, where required.

### 5.8 Deletion or Return
At your choice, delete or return all personal data after the end of the provision of Services, and delete existing copies unless storage is required by applicable law.

### 5.9 Audit Rights
Make available to you all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for audits, including inspections, conducted by you or an independent auditor.

## 6. Controller Obligations

You agree to:

1. Comply with your obligations under applicable data protection laws
2. Have a lawful basis for the processing of personal data you submit
3. Provide necessary notices and obtain required consents from data subjects
4. Ensure instructions to Processor comply with applicable laws
5. Not submit special categories of personal data (UK GDPR Art. 9) unless explicitly agreed in writing

## 7. Sub-Processors

### 7.1 Authorised Sub-Processors
By using our Services, you authorise the Sub-Processors listed in Appendix B.

### 7.2 Sub-Processor Obligations
We enter into written agreements with Sub-Processors imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those in this DPA. We remain liable for Sub-Processor compliance.

### 7.3 Notification of Changes
We will notify you of new Sub-Processors at least 14 days before they begin processing. You may object on reasonable data protection grounds within 14 days of notification.

## 8. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK or EEA, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place through:
- UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)
- EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Adequacy decisions by the UK Government or European Commission

## 9. Liability

Each party's liability arising out of or related to this DPA shall be subject to the limitations and exclusions of liability set out in the Terms of Service. Nothing in this DPA limits either party's liability for breaches of data protection law that cannot be limited by contract.
