## Section 3 — The 3 whitepapers (outlines)

### Paper 1: *Byzantine Quorum Architecture for Multi-Agent AI Decision-Making*

**Abstract (150 words):**
We propose a 33-seat Byzantine Fault Tolerant council architecture for AI agent consensus, drawing from distributed-systems BFT (Castro & Liskov, 1999) and applying it to multi-LLM decision-making. Each "seat" is an independent LLM (heterogeneous: 11 local MLX + 11 local Ollama + 11 remote API). A decision requires ≥22/33 (2/3+1) seats to agree. Disagreement triggers reconciliation rounds where divergent seats share reasoning. All votes are HMAC-signed and persist in a hash-chained audit log. We further show that QAOA-based optimisation and Grover search can verify consensus stability and detect sub-quorum collusion attacks. We benchmark against single-LLM decision-making, single-architecture ensembles, and unweighted majority voting, demonstrating superior robustness on adversarial test sets and equal latency on non-adversarial sets. The architecture is open-source under MIT licence.

**Sections:**
1. Introduction — why single-LLM decision-making is fragile
2. Related work — PBFT, Sybil resistance, LLM ensembles
3. Architecture — 33 seats, voting protocol, reconciliation
4. Security — HMAC signing, audit chain, quantum verification
5. Benchmarks — adversarial vs non-adversarial datasets
6. Implementation — github.com/CSOAI-ORG/...
7. Limitations + future work

**Venue strategy:** arXiv preprint → submit to NeurIPS 2026 Workshop on Trustworthy ML (deadline typically Aug). Effort: 15 hours.

### Paper 2: *Care-Aligned Membrane Gating in Sovereign AI Systems*

**Abstract:** Drawing from McGilchrist's hemispheric model + Vedantic Jagrat/Svapna/Susupti/Turiya + Bowlby attachment theory, we present a "care membrane" — a meta-monitoring module that evaluates AI agent outputs against care-alignment criteria (relational coherence, harm-minimisation, emotional valence). The care loop has *veto authority* over the analytical loop's proposals. We implement on top of FastMCP and evaluate against Anthropic's HH-RLHF benchmarks.

**Venue:** FAccT 2027 (ACM Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency) — deadline Jan 2027. Or AAAI 2027 Workshop on Safety & AI. Effort: 12 hours.

### Paper 3: *HMAC-Anchored Compliance Attestation Chain for AI Audit*

**Abstract:** Compliance audits of AI systems require tamper-evident records. We propose a Merkle-rooted hash chain of compliance attestations, each HMAC-signed by the auditor and anchored periodically to a transparency log (sigstore Rekor). We show this satisfies the EU AI Act Article 12 (automatic logs) + DORA Article 17 (ICT incident logs) + ISO/IEC 42001 clause 9 (monitoring) record-keeping obligations simultaneously. Implementation: meok-attestation-api on Vercel.
