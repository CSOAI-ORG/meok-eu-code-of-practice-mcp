# Acquisition / Acqui-hire Shortlist
**Prepared for:** Nick Templeman, SOV3
**The legitimate version of "weak competitor" intel.** If a player is genuinely undercapitalized, the sovereign move isn't to smear them — it's to absorb the talent, tech, and market access. Legal, durable, and it actually removes a competitor from the board.

> **Reality check before you act on any of this:** the funding/headcount figures below come from Kimi's dossier (sourced to Crunchbase/PitchBook). Treat them as *leads, not facts* — re-verify each via primary sources before any outreach. "Distress" here is inferred from "small raise + no follow-on + tiny team," which is suggestive, not confirmed. And the first contact is a **normal partnership/talent conversation**, not anything adversarial.

## A. The shortlist (ranked by fit, not just weakness)

### 1. Straion — AI coding-agent governance · Linz, Austria
- **Reported:** ~$1.5M raised (€1.1M seed + €280K grants), 12 people, pre-revenue, no follow-on since Feb 2026.
- **Why it fits SOV3:** governs Claude Code / Cursor / Copilot — i.e., a *rules engine for AI coding agents*. That's adjacent to your MCP-governance and enforcement story, and to your own dev workflow.
- **What you'd gain:** a working policy engine for coding agents + an EU (Austria) entity and team — useful for EU AI Act credibility and market access.
- **Indicative cost:** low single-digit millions / acqui-hire. **Re-verify runway before assuming distress.**

### 2. Euno — data-model governance · Sunnyvale
- **Reported:** $6.25M seed (Mar 2024), 19 people, no follow-on in ~18 months.
- **Why it fits:** "shift-left" governance for BI tools (Looker/Tableau/dbt). Complements a data-lineage/audit story.
- **What you'd gain:** data-governance tech + a team that's shipped. Weaker strategic fit than Straion — only pursue if data-lineage is on your roadmap.
- **Indicative cost:** sub-$15M / asset purchase.

### 3. NanoCo — human-in-the-loop agent oversight · SF
- **Reported:** $12M raised, **4 employees**. (The "$63M" figure elsewhere is a data-source discrepancy — *not* evidence of fraud; don't repeat it as such.)
- **Why it fits:** human-in-the-loop oversight complements your automated enforcement (the "human oversight" the EU AI Act Art. 14 demands).
- **What you'd gain:** mostly an acqui-hire — 4 people, early product. Value is the tech concept + the team, not traction.
- **Indicative cost:** acqui-hire territory.

### 4. Holistic AI — AI governance / EU AI Act · Palo Alto *(watch, don't pursue yet)*
- **Reported:** funding undisclosed beyond a Mozilla Ventures strategic check (Mar 2024).
- **Why listed:** strong EU AI Act / ISO 42001 compliance expertise and relationships — genuinely valuable for EU access.
- **Caution:** "undisclosed funding" ≠ "weak." Could be well-resourced and simply private. **Verify before assuming they're available.** Higher risk of misread than the three above.

## C. How to do this legitimately

1. **Re-verify** funding, runway, and headcount via primary sources (Crunchbase, company filings, LinkedIn headcount trend) — the dossier figures are starting points.
2. **Open with partnership, not predation.** "We admire what you've built; is there a world where we combine forces?" Distressed founders respond to a lifeline, not a threat.
3. **Lead with talent + tech, structure as acqui-hire or asset purchase** if traction is thin.
4. **Keep it private.** No public "we're picking off the weak" narrative — that poisons every other founder relationship and your own reputation. Acquisitions are announced *after* they close, as wins.
5. **Budget reality:** even acqui-hires cost real cash/equity. Sequence these *after* launch revenue or alongside any raise — don't let "cheap" reasoning commit capital you need for July 4.

## D. What this replaces from the original dossier

The dossier had a "Strike Priority Ranking" framing competitors as targets to *damage*. Same intel, inverted: these are targets to *acquire or partner with*. One version is a defamation risk and a news cycle. The other removes competitors permanently and grows your team. Do the second one.
