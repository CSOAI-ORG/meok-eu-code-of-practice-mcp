## A. The one-sentence story

> **The EU AI Act's hardest deadline lands August 2, 2026. Most AI governance tools tell you what's wrong. SOV3 stops it from shipping — and it's the first platform priced for the 99% of companies that aren't the Fortune 500.**

## B. Why this narrative wins without a single smear

Three legs, each provable on SOV3's own merits or by public record — none requiring an accusation about a competitor:

1. **The deadline does the work.** Aug 2, 2026 is the statutory enforcement date for high-risk obligations under the EU AI Act. Penalties reach **€35M or 7% of global turnover**, and national authorities can pull a non-compliant system from the EU market entirely. *(Source: [EU AI Act implementation timeline](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/); penalties per Art. 99.)* You don't need to say a rival is bad. You need to say the clock is real. It is.
   - **Honesty guardrail:** amendments to soften/extend high-risk timelines are under active EU discussion ([Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/ai-act-update-eu-resolves-to-change-rules-and-extend-deadlines)). Frame as *"the statutory deadline is Aug 2; don't bet your company on Brussels blinking."* Never claim "no extension is possible."

2. **Enforcement, not assessment.** The honest differentiator: most governance platforms evaluate and report. SOV3 gates deployment at runtime — policy-as-code that can *block* a non-compliant AI system from shipping. "Governance that acts, not just reports." This is a claim about **what SOV3 does**, which you can demo, not a claim about what others can't.

3. **Transparent, mid-market pricing.** Enterprise GRC hides behind "contact sales." SOV3 publishes a price and starts where real companies live (from $99/mo). Transparent pricing *is* the competitive act — it needs no commentary on anyone else.

## C. Dual-audience framing (the genuinely sharp July 4 play)

Same launch, two doors — this part of the original plan is good, keep it:

- **US / mid-market:** *"Declare independence from black-box, six-figure compliance."* Freedom-from-lock-in, transparent pricing, ships in hours not quarters.
- **EU / regulated:** *"58 days to the Aug 2 deadline. One platform to get you there."* Urgency, conformity workflow, enforcement.

July 4 carries the "independence" symbol cleanly **as long as it's independence from lock-in — not a date pinned to anyone's stock ticker.**

## D. What we deliberately cut from the dossier (and why)

| Cut | Reason |
|---|---|
| "CrowdStrike Security Audit" timed to the July 2 stock split | Timing reputational content to a public company's split/retail window = SEC 10b-5 exposure + discoverable malice |
| "Funding Fiction" report (competitors "fabricated" funding) | Crunchbase-vs-self-reported gaps ≠ proof of fraud; stating fraud as fact about a named company = defamation per se |
| OneTrust "review manipulation" / "death spiral" | Inferring fraud from a Trustpilot/G2 gap is not provable; defamation risk |
| "CVE = they can't secure themselves" framing | Sets a standard SOV3 (316 MCP packages) cannot survive being held to |

Everything cut was a liability. Nothing cut was load-bearing for the win.
