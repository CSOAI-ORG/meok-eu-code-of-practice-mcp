## 1. Funnel shape (founder-led, not self-serve)

```
  Cold email/DM (named farm)                       100 sent
        ↓  reply rate (cold B2B niche, founder, specific pain)  ~8–15%
  Replies / opens-to-conversation                   8–15
        ↓  book a 15-min call                       ~40–60% of repliers
  Discovery calls                                   4–9
        ↓  call → paid (concierge onboard, free pilot offer) ~20–35%
  Paying / committed pilots                          1–3
```

**Read this honestly:** from a *single* batch of ~15 named, well-targeted emails, the expected outcome is **1–2 real conversations and 0–1 paying customers.** Customer #1 typically needs **2–4 outreach batches + follow-ups over 4–8 weeks.** Anyone promising "first sale this week from one email blast" in a niche this small is selling hope. The job is to run the cadence relentlessly until the math pays out.

### Why these conversion assumptions (not pulled from air)
- **Reply rate 8–15%:** higher than generic cold email (~1–3%) because (a) named real farms with a real, dated regulatory pain, (b) founder-to-founder framing, (c) specific price + specific product, no "let's hop on a call to explore synergies" vagueness. Lower than warm intro (~30%+) because no existing relationship.
- **Call→paid 20–35%:** elevated by the **free-pilot offer** (12 months free for first 5 pilots, already sanctioned in the partnership doc pending Nick's confirm — Q3). A free pilot converts "will you pay £499" into "will you let me do your RSPCA paperwork for free" — a near-yes. The paid conversion then comes at pilot-end when the value is proven. So **early "wins" may be logos/pilots, not £ — and that is the correct first move** (case studies unlock everything downstream).
