# FOLLOWUP_SEQUENCE — 3-touch cadence

**Reality:** in cold B2B, the first email is usually ignored — not rejected, just buried. **Most replies come from follow-ups 2 and 3, not the opener.** Skipping follow-up is the single biggest reason an outbound push produces £0. Run the full cadence on every non-replier.

**Cadence per target:**

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Touch 1 | Day 0 | Email (from OUTREACH_BATCH_1) | Open the conversation |
| Touch 2 | Day 3–4 | Reply on the same thread | Add one new concrete hook + lower the ask |
| Touch 3 | Day 8–10 | Reply on same thread, OR phone | Final value nudge + clean break-up |
| (Phone) | Anytime Day 2+ | Call the listed number | Farms answer phones more than email — for the no-public-email targets, call IS touch 1 |

Stop after Touch 3. Do not chase past 3 — log as "no response, revisit next quarter." Re-approach in a new batch when there's a new hook (case study, new standard deadline, OATA/BTA deal landing).

**Always reply on the original thread** (keeps context, higher open rate than a fresh subject). Keep follow-ups shorter than the opener.

## Handling the common replies

| Reply | Response |
|---|---|
| "How much / what's the catch?" | Restate price + the free-pilot/cancel-anytime. Send the direct Stripe link or book the call. Don't over-explain. |
| "We use a consultant." | "Keep them for the judgement calls — this just kills the evidence-compiling grind and re-generates when the standard changes. Most farms run both." |
| "Not now / busy season." | "Totally — can I check back after [harvest / the next standard refresh]?" Log the date, actually do it. |
| "Is it accredited / official?" | Honest: it's a tool that structures the *public* standards and produces an assessor-ready pack + a verifiable signed fingerprint; it doesn't replace the RSPCA/ASC assessor, it gets you ready for them. (Only mention the signed fingerprint if attestation-verify is confirmed live — CONVERSION_PLAN Q4.) |
| Silence | Run the full 3-touch cadence, then stop. |

**Log everything** in `~/clawd/revenue/OUTREACH_LOG.md`: date, target, touch number, channel, outcome. The log is what tells you which product/segment is actually moving after 3 batches.
