**The 3 EU AI Act deadlines UK businesses are missing (and one that already passed)**

Hello,

Quick question: do you know which EU AI Act deadline already passed in February?

Most companies don't. They're focused on August 2026 (high-risk AI) and missing the one that's already law.

---

**The deadline that already happened: Article 4 (2 Feb 2025)**

EU AI Act Article 4 requires all companies *deploying* AI systems in the EU to ensure their staff have "sufficient AI literacy." That came into force 16 months ago.

This doesn't mean everyone needs a certification. It means:
- Whoever uses AI tools in your business needs documented awareness of what those tools can and can't do
- Someone in your organisation needs to own this literacy programme
- If a regulator asks, you need to be able to show evidence

What does sufficient look like? A simple staff training log with dates, topics covered, and sign-offs. Unglamorous. Auditable. Takes an afternoon to set up.

[→ Article 4 AI literacy requirements explained](https://meok.ai/eu-ai-act/article-4)

---

**The next three deadlines**

**2 August 2026 — High-risk AI systems (Annex III)**
If you use AI for: credit scoring, CV screening, biometric identification, critical infrastructure, or education assessment — you're likely in-scope. Six weeks to assess whether you are.

**November 2026 — AI-generated content watermarking (Article 50)**
All AI-generated images, audio, and video served in the EU must carry machine-readable watermarks. This applies to your social media content, marketing assets, customer-facing chatbots. November is closer than it looks.

**December 2027 — Annex I high-risk AI (general purpose AI)**
The broadest category. Foundation models, large-scale AI services. If you're building with AI APIs, this timeline matters.

---

**What to do this week**

1. **Check if you're deploying a high-risk AI system.** The [MEOK AI Risk Scorecard](https://meok.ai/scorecard) takes 3 minutes. It tells you whether you're in scope for August 2026 and what that means specifically.

2. **Create an AI literacy log.** One spreadsheet: staff name, training date, topics covered, sign-off. That's Article 4 compliance. [Download the care home version here](https://meok.ai/care-homes) (works for any regulated sector).

3. **Check your AI-generated content pipeline.** If you're using Midjourney, DALL-E, Sora, or any AI for customer-facing content, the watermarking deadline affects you.

---

**One number to know**

€35 million or 7% of global annual turnover — whichever is higher. That's the maximum fine for violating the prohibited AI practices under Article 5.

[Article 5 prohibited AI practices explained](https://meok.ai/eu-ai-act/article-5)
