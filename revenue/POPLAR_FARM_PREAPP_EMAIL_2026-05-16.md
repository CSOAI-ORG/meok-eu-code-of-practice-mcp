## Why this email works

1. **Frames it as "match neighbour" not "depart from baseline"** — Poplar Farm Flowers is the legal precedent next door
2. **Names specific permitted-development routes** — shows you've done the homework, not asking the officer to teach you planning law
3. **Asks for COPIES of Poplar Farm's decisions** — clever: if the officer pulls them, your case becomes "the same officers approved the same thing 100m away"
4. **Personal use + small-scale framing** — keeps it Class E (incidental to dwelling)
5. **Pre-loads supporting docs** — neighbour LoS, councillor LoS, BNG baseline = officer's job is easier, they say yes
6. **No commercial promise** — keeps it out of Class B (which requires >5ha for new buildings)
7. **British, formal, deferential** — South Holland officers respond to this tone

## What to do BEFORE you send

1. **Walk over to Poplar Farm Flowers TODAY** with a coffee. 10-minute conversation. Tell them what you're planning. Ask for a one-line letter of support ("We are happy with our neighbour's proposal at Old Fendyke for personal food growing.") This single conversation removes 80% of objection risk.
2. **Email/call your South Holland councillor** (find them at sholland.gov.uk/your-councillors). Tell them the personal-food + research story. Ask for verbal support and offer to share the pre-app email before sending.
3. **Take 3 photos**: existing 120×20 tunnels in situ, bungalow + curtilage boundary, view across to Poplar Farm Flowers. Attach to the email.

## After you send

- Officer response usually 2–4 weeks
- They'll either invite a free site visit (great) or send a written advisory note
- Either way you have a paper trail that protects everything you build afterwards
