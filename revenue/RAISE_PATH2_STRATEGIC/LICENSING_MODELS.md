# Deal Structures & Value Framing — MEOK AI Labs / CSOAI LTD
**2026-06-06 · For internal strategy + buyer-side discussion · Companion to `ACQUIRER_TARGETS.md` and `IP_TEASER_ONEPAGER.md`**

> **Read this caveat first — it governs every number below.** MEOK is **pre-revenue (£0 MRR)** with **max 2 GitHub stars** and a **sole founder**. There is no revenue multiple to apply, no traction to price off. AI-asset deals at this stage are valued on **IP-asset appraisal, data-moat depth, and talent-per-head** — not ARR ([Petersen Landis](https://petersenlandis.com/ai-ma-valuation-valuing-ai-companies-acqui-hire-deals/)). **Every figure here is an *estimate of a possible range*, not a valuation, not an ask, and not a promise.** The actual number is whatever a specific buyer's build-vs-buy math and competitive urgency produce on the day. Treat these as *negotiation scaffolding*, and anchor to the **comparable deals** (which are real) rather than to any single figure.

> **The honest anchor (use this, not fantasy multiples):** the closest real comparable is **FairNow → AuditBoard (Oct 2025)** — an AI-governance startup that had raised ~$4.15M, acquired for an undisclosed sum to add "38+ frameworks incl. ISO 42001 + EU AI Act." Fairly AI (raised ~$2M) merged with Anch.ai (Jun 2025). These are the *scale of company* MEOK is — and they transacted. That is the proof the path is real; the *price* in those deals was not disclosed, so we do **not** invent one.

## The three structures, ranked by realism for MEOK's actual position

| | Structure | Realism for MEOK now | Founder outcome | Buyer's logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | **IP / Tech License** | ⭐ **Highest** — lowest friction, no cap-table/DD drama, fits £0-revenue | Stays independent; keeps building; recurring or lump-sum cash | "Rent the coverage" — buy time-to-market without an acquisition |
| **B** | **Acqui-hire** (founder + IP) | **Medium-high** — fits the 2025–26 "hire-and-license" wave | Joins buyer; IP transfers; comp + (modest) equity | "Buy the builder + clean IP" — talent-per-head economics |
| **C** | **Full acquisition** of CSOAI LTD IP estate | **Medium** — cleanest for buyer, but pre-revenue caps the price | Exit (cash/stock/earnout) | "Own it outright" — coverage + verifiability + optionality |

**Recommended sequence:** lead conversations with **A (License)** — it's the easiest "yes," requires the least diligence, and *creates the relationship and the valuation reference* that can escalate into B or C. Don't open with "buy my company"; open with "license the coverage your roadmap needs."

## Structure A — IP / Tech License (lead with this)

**What transfers:** a license (non-exclusive, or field/territory-exclusive) to one or more of:
- the **crosswalk corpus** (the highest-value, most license-friendly asset — it's *data/IP*, not a running service);
- the **attestation chain** (license the signed-evidence primitive + the verification method);
- a **bundle of the compliance MCPs** (e.g. the EU-AI-Act / DORA / NIS2 / CRA suite).

**Why it fits MEOK best:** no company sale, no cap-table cleanup blocking the deal, minimal diligence (the IP is *already public and verifiable* — that shrinks DD dramatically), founder keeps the rest of the estate (aquaculture, optometry, robotics R&D) and keeps building. It's the structure most compatible with £0 revenue because the buyer is pricing *IP utility*, not a business.

**Honest value framing (ESTIMATE — wide, caveated):**
- **Non-exclusive license** to the crosswalk corpus or attestation chain: realistically a **low-five-figure to low-six-figure (£) lump sum, or a modest recurring fee**, depending on buyer size and how much it accelerates their roadmap. A specialist (Saidot/Trustible) anchors the low end; a platform (Vanta/OneTrust) the higher end.
- **Field-exclusive license** (e.g. exclusive in GRC, MEOK retains AI-security): commands a premium over non-exclusive — **mid-five to mid-six figures (£)** — because exclusivity has competitive value to the licensee.
- **Structure levers:** upfront + annual maintenance; per-framework or per-server pricing; "most-favoured-licensee" clauses; a buy-out option that credits paid license fees against a later acquisition price (this is the bridge from A → C).

**Heavy caveat:** these ranges assume the buyer concludes the IP saves them *meaningful* engineering/regulatory time. With no usage data to prove demand, expect buyers to discount hard and prefer non-exclusive + modest terms initially. The license's real value is often **strategic (it starts the relationship)** more than the cheque.

## Structure B — Acqui-hire (founder + clean IP)

**What transfers:** the founder (employment) + assignment/license of the IP. This is the **defining deal shape of 2025–26** — often a "reverse acqui-hire / hire-and-license" where the buyer hires the person and *licenses* the IP rather than buying the entity ([Stepmark](https://stepmark.ai/2025/11/03/how-big-tech-is-rewriting-ma-the-license-and-acqui-hire-era/)).

**Why it fits MEOK:** the asset register's #1 honest line is that *the entire estate is one person* — that is precisely what an acqui-hire prices. A buyer gets (a) a builder with demonstrated single-founder velocity, deep regulatory-crosswalk + domain (optometry/aquaculture) expertise, and (b) clean, MIT-licensed, transferable IP with no training-data overhang. Best fit: **Holistic AI** (UK-local, cleanest), **Credo AI**, **Snyk** (as MCP-governance/compliance SME), or **into the FairNow team at AuditBoard**.

**Honest value framing (ESTIMATE):**
- Acqui-hires are priced largely on **talent-per-head + a premium for the IP**, not on company value. For a single founder + a deep-but-pre-revenue IP estate, think in terms of a **competitive senior-hire compensation package (salary + equity/RSUs + sign-on)** *plus* an **IP component** that could add a one-off sum (often structured as retention/earnout).
- The all-in headline can look like a "£X00K+ deal" largely because of multi-year comp + equity vesting, but **the cash-for-IP portion is typically modest** for pre-revenue IP. Don't conflate the two when evaluating offers.
- **Levers:** vesting/retention schedule, IP-assignment vs. license, earnout tied to integrating MEOK's coverage into the buyer's product, scope of role (keep it close to the IP so the founder's value compounds).

**Heavy caveat:** acqui-hire = the founder is *employed*, MEOK-as-independent-company effectively ends (or becomes a shell holding non-transferred assets). That's a real trade-off against Path 1 (revenue bootstrap) and Path 3 (angel raise) — only attractive if the buyer + role are genuinely good and the comp reflects the IP.

## Structure C — Full Acquisition of the CSOAI LTD IP estate

**What transfers:** the IP estate — crosswalk corpus, 271 MCPs, attestation chain, and (optionally) the 5 verticals + domains — via asset purchase or share purchase of CSOAI LTD.

**Why a buyer does it:** owns the coverage + verifiability outright, removes it from competitors, gets the founder for integration. This is the FairNow / Riskey shape.

**Honest value framing (ESTIMATE — the most caveated of all):**
- There is **no defensible single number** for a £0-revenue company. What anchors a range is **build-vs-buy**: what would it cost the buyer to replicate ~18 months of crosswalk work + 271 MCPs + an attestation system? A credible internal estimate is *a small team for 12–18 months* (call it a few hundred £K to low-£M of fully-loaded engineering + regulatory expertise) — and a buyer will pay *some fraction* of that replication cost, discounted for the risk that MEOK's IP needs adaptation and that there's no usage proof.
- Net: a realistic *range to discuss* for a full IP-estate acquisition is plausibly **low-six-figures to low-seven-figures (£)**, **heavily** dependent on (i) which buyer (a $4B platform's urgency ≠ a seed specialist's budget), (ii) whether the founder is included, (iii) competitive tension (two interested buyers move this more than any pitch), and (iv) whether *any* revenue/usage exists by deal time. **Without traction, expect the lower half.**
- **What would move it up the range, concretely:** the IP-assignment deed signed (removes the #1 DD red flag); 1–2 reference customers or real PyPI download stats; a second buyer at the table; a granted/filed patent on the attestation process.
- **What a buyer will discount for:** £0 MRR, 2 stars (no demand signal), single-person key-man risk, code that may need re-architecting to fit their stack.

**Heavy caveat — do NOT put a big number on a deck.** The asset register is explicit: "Do not put a big number on a deck with £0 revenue behind it. That's the fake move." These ranges are for *internal* expectation-setting and to recognise a fair offer — **not** to quote at a buyer. Let the buyer underwrite the value off the verifiable IP; anchor the conversation to the real comparable deals, not to a figure.

## The lever that re-rates ALL three structures
**Paying customers and/or real usage data.** Per the ground-truth asset register, 5–10 real logos (or even credible PyPI download/usage stats) don't just add MRR — they **re-rate the whole company from "GitHub estate" to "revenue business,"** moving every number above up materially and shifting leverage from buyer to founder. The single highest-ROI action *before* serious strategic conversations is therefore the same as Path 1: **get a little real usage/revenue.** Even modest proof of demand can multiply the credible range and turn a "license for a modest fee" into "acquire before a competitor does."

## Pre-deal hygiene (do these regardless of structure — they protect the founder and the price)
1. **Sign the IP-assignment deed** (Nicholas → CSOAI LTD, ~£500). Without it, "the IP is just Nicholas's" — every buyer flags it and discounts. **#1 priority.**
2. **Trademark MEOK + CSOAI** (~£1.5K UK/EU/US) — clean marks reduce DD friction.
3. **Cap table as single source of truth** (founder 100%); never grant equity on a handshake.
4. **Confirm MIT-license cleanliness** across the 271 repos (no GPL contamination, no embedded third-party code that blocks transfer) — buyers verify this; pre-empt it.
5. **Keep a private, verifiable data room** (`DATA_ROOM/` skeleton per the asset register) so a buyer's DD is *fast* — fast DD = higher close probability and less price erosion.

## Bottom line
- **Lead with a License (A)** — easiest yes, creates the relationship + a valuation reference, fits £0 revenue.
- **Acqui-hire (B)** is the most *probable* outcome given the "one-person-estate" reality and the 2025–26 hire-and-license wave — attractive only if the buyer/role/comp are genuinely good.
- **Full acquisition (C)** is the cleanest for a buyer but the price is *capped by pre-revenue status* until there's usage/traction.
- **The numbers above are estimates and negotiation scaffolding, not valuations.** The real, defensible anchors are the **comparable deals** (FairNow, Fairly×Anch.ai, Riskey) and the **build-vs-buy replication cost** of MEOK's verifiable IP. **Get a little real usage first — it moves every range and every lever.**
