## The 5 growth levers (ranked by £ leverage)

### Lever 1: NHS Enhanced Pathways (RECURRING REVENUE, ZERO MARKETING)

Lincolnshire ICB (Integrated Care Board) commissions enhanced eye-care services via Primary Eyecare Services Ltd (PES). Independent opticians can sign up to deliver these pathways:

| Pathway | What it is | Typical fee |
|---|---|---|
| **COATS** (Community Optometry Assessment & Treatment) | Sight tests + treatment + referral filtering for patients who'd otherwise go to hospital | £35-£70 per appointment |
| **MECS** (Minor Eye Conditions Service) | Same-day assessment of red eye, foreign body, etc. | £35-£70 per appointment |
| **Pre-cataract assessment** | Pre-surgery eye health check | £40-£80 |
| **Post-cataract assessment** | Post-surgery follow-up | £40-£80 |
| **Low Vision Service** | Magnifiers + visual aids for partially sighted patients | £50-£100 |
| **Glaucoma referral filtering / IPCAT** | Pre-screening glaucoma referrals | £30-£70 |
| **Domiciliary care home pathway** | Travel + standard GOS uplift | £40.80 + standard GOS fee |

**Realistic recurring revenue if Templeman gets accredited on 4 pathways: £4-£15k/yr per optometrist.**

**Action this week:**
- Call **Lincolnshire LOC** via https://www.loc-online.co.uk/lincolnshire-loc/
- Call **PES Lincolnshire** via https://primaryeyecare.co.uk/your-area/midlands/lincolnshire/
- Ask: "What enhanced pathways are currently commissioned in Lincolnshire and how do we get accredited for each?"
- This is the single highest-£/effort move available.

### Lever 2: Grants4Growth £25,000 (DEADLINE-CRITICAL)

South Holland's UK Shared Prosperity Fund grants programme. Up to £25,000 at 50% match — meaning if you spend £50,000 on qualifying equipment, you get £25,000 back.

**Qualifying uses for Templeman:**
- OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography) — modern eye-imaging
- AI retinal screener (e.g., Optain, Retinai)
- Fundus camera
- Visual field analyser
- Autorefractor
- Patient management system (PMS) upgrade
- Website + booking system
- Energy efficiency (LED lighting, heat pump)
- Practice signage

**Deadline:** programme spend by 31 March 2026 [VERIFY successor framework after that]

**Action:**
- Go to https://grants4growth.sholland.gov.uk/
- Click "Apply"
- Get quotes for OCT (~£25k) + AI retinal screener (~£15k) + PMS upgrade (~£10k)
- Submit application with business case

**Why this is urgent:** the programme is first-come-served and likely to run out of money by Q3-Q4 2026.

### Lever 3: Apprentice hire from 1 October 2026

From 1 Oct 2026, the apprenticeship rules change dramatically in your favour:

| Apprentice level | Subsidies stacked |
|---|---|
| **Optical Assistant (L3)** | 100% training cost + £2,000 SME cash incentive + £3,000 if from Universal Credit = **£5,000+ cash per hire** |
| **Dispensing Optician (L6)** | £23,000 of training fully funded over 3 years + £2,000 cash + £3,000 if from UC |
| **Optometrist (L7 degree apprenticeship)** | ~£27,000 training fully funded + £2,000 cash + £3,000 if from UC |

**Action:**
- DO NOT recruit before 1 Oct 2026 — current rules are worse
- Start the conversation with **ABDO (Association of British Dispensing Opticians)** in summer 2026
- ABDO Dispensing Optician Apprenticeship: https://www.abdo.org.uk/dashboard/examinations/dispensing/dispensing-optician-apprenticeship/
- Bradford School of Optometry (BSO) for L7
- City University London for L7
- Identify candidate now (likely someone already working part-time at the practice or a local school-leaver)

**Why this is strategic:** a dispensing optician apprentice frees Wendy + Fred to focus on enhanced pathways (Lever 1) + domiciliary work (Lever 4). The apprenticeship pays for itself within year 2.

### Lever 4: Care home expansion (domiciliary)

Templeman already does some care home work. There's room to grow this.

**Each care-home visit pays:**
- £24.13 × number of patients (GOS1 fee per resident)
- £40.80 for the 1st patient (domiciliary uplift)
- £40.80 for the 2nd patient (uplift)
- £10.21 for the 3rd patient onwards (uplift)

**Example: 8-patient care-home visit = (£24.13 × 8) + £40.80 + £40.80 + (£10.21 × 6) = £283.10 per visit.**

**Lincolnshire care home market:** 200+ registered care homes in Lincolnshire. If Templeman serves 20 of them on a 2-year recall cycle = 10/year × £283 avg = ~£2.8k/yr passive.

**Action:**
- Lincolnshire CQC care home list: https://www.cqc.org.uk
- Filter by South Holland / Spalding / Holbeach / Long Sutton
- Letter or visit each care home manager
- Offer the standard NHS-funded domiciliary visit (no cost to the home)
- Mention "Templeman" — heritage trust factor matters in care homes

**Why this matters:** care homes refer patient families to opticians. One good relationship = 50 NHS sight tests/year + glasses revenue from families.

### Lever 5: The clinical pilot site angle (Nick's AI compliance MCP suite)

This is the unique-to-Templeman lever. Nick has built 38 AI compliance MCPs used by developers globally. **No other independent UK optician has this in-house.**

**The structure:**
- MEOK AI Labs (Nick) applies for **NHS AAC AI Award** + **MHRA AI Airlock** + **SBRI Healthcare** grants
- Templeman Opticians is the **clinical pilot site** — actual patients, real workflow, real data (consented)
- Grant pool: £100k-£1m to MEOK
- Templeman gets early access to AI tools that no chain has, plus a small data-collection fee + reputation boost

**Action:**
- Nick prepares the grant applications (already on his action list)
- Wendy + Fred sign as clinical site partners
- Patient consent forms updated to opt-in for "AI research partner programme"

**Why this is sleight-of-hand brilliance:** Most NHS AI grants need a clinical partner site. Most clinical sites have to find a tech partner. We have both under one family. **No friction. No commercial conflict of interest. No race to find a partner.**

## The mistakes to avoid

❌ **Trying to compete with Specsavers on price.** Specsavers spend £30m+/year on advertising. We can't out-spend them. We win on TRUST and DEPTH OF CARE — not price.

❌ **Spending the Grants4Growth £25k on the wrong equipment.** OCT is the right primary purchase. AI retinal screener is the right secondary. Don't waste it on furniture.

❌ **Hiring an apprentice before 1 October 2026.** You lose £5k cash + 100% training subsidies if you start under old rules.

❌ **Posting on every social platform.** Pick one. Do it well. Other platforms are noise.

❌ **Trying to do everything yourself.** Nick handles tech. Roythornes handles legal. Saffery handles tax. Lincolnshire LOC handles NHS conversations. You handle patients + relationships.

❌ **Saying no to local press.** Free coverage is gold. Always say yes to an interview, photo, or feature.

❌ **Ignoring the Templeman name.** The family story IS the marketing. Lean into it everywhere — website, signage, press, social.

## Mental model for growth

For a small business, growth comes from **stacking small advantages** that compound over time. Not from one big move.

```
Year 1: Apply 5 grants + 2 enhanced pathways + 1 apprentice
        ↓
Year 2: 10 enhanced pathways + 2 apprentices + 30 care homes
        ↓
Year 3: First AI clinical pilot grant won + 3 apprentices + 50 care homes
        ↓
Year 4: Multi-practice satellite via the apprentices who graduated
        ↓
Year 5: Either independent regional leader OR acquisition target for chain
```

**The big picture:** in 5 years, Templeman could be the **Patagonia of UK independent optometry** — the values-driven, family-run, technically-credible practice that other independents look up to and that chains can't replicate.
