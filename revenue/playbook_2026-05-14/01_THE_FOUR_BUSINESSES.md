# 01 — The Four Businesses (in plain English)

This document explains what each of the Templeman businesses does, who buys what, and how they fit together. **No jargon.**

## 1. Templeman Opticians — the family heritage

**What it is:** An independent UK optometry practice. Wendy and Fred test eyes, prescribe glasses, dispense contact lenses, do domiciliary visits to care homes.

**Who pays:**
- **NHS** for every sight test (£24.13 per GOS1 test in 2025/26)
- **Patients** for glasses, lenses, accessories
- **NHS** for domiciliary visits to care homes (£40.80 each for 1st + 2nd patient, £10.21 for 3rd+)

**The current state (May 2026):**
- Real customers, real cash flow, real reputation
- 30+ years of trading (Templeman name = heritage moat)
- Domiciliary care work — visiting care homes
- Independent — not Specsavers, not Vision Express, not Boots
- Likely on the Lincolnshire ICB local list for some enhanced services (COATS, Low Vision)

**Why this matters strategically:** Independent opticians in the UK are being squeezed by national chains. **But independents have one thing chains cannot copy: trust.** Patients stay with Templeman because they trust Wendy and Fred personally. Chains have to manufacture trust through advertising. We already have it.

**Where the growth is:**
- More enhanced NHS pathways via Lincolnshire ICB
- More care-home domiciliary work
- Apprentice hires (from 1 Oct 2026, £5k cash + 100% training funding for under-25s)
- Equipment upgrade via Grants4Growth (£25k UKSPF grant, deadline March 2026)
- Clinical pilot site for AI tools (worth £100k+ to MEOK via NHS innovation grants)

**The unique angle nobody else has:** *"This is the family opticians where the son builds the AI compliance tools used by Fortune 500 companies. The same tools that help global banks comply with EU AI law are what we use to run our recall system."* That story is worth a feature in the local paper, the trade press, and BBC East. **You don't need to advertise it — you ARE it.**

## 2. MEOK AI Labs — Nick's main business

**What it is:** A software company that builds tools (called "MCPs" — pronounced "em-see-pees") that help other companies obey the new EU AI laws.

**Who pays:**
- **Tech companies** building AI products who need to prove they're compliant
- **Banks + insurers** under DORA + NIS2 (EU finance regulations)
- **Medical device makers** under MDR + FDA SaMD rules
- **Government departments** building AI systems

**What it sells:**
- **Free tier:** developers download the tools to try out (38 free tools live on PyPI right now)
- **£79/month Pro:** signed compliance certificates that auditors trust
- **£499/month Enterprise:** unlimited use + custom branding
- **£5,000 one-off:** "Build me a compliance pack for X"

**The current state (May 2026):**
- **38 published tools** on PyPI (Python package index — like the App Store for developers)
- **0 paying customers yet** — the products work, the checkout works, the world just doesn't know they exist
- **Distribution is the bottleneck** — not product, not pricing, not market

**Why it matters for the family:** This is the **moat business**. Even if the opticians stays a family practice forever, MEOK is the bet on Nick's IP becoming worth millions in grant collateral, R&D tax credits, and eventually equity in a software company that could exit for £10M+ in 5-7 years.

**Where the growth is:**
- 38 MCPs into the Official MCP Registry (in flight, awaiting Nick's authorisation)
- 8 awesome-mcp-server lists with PRs queued (~100K combined GitHub stars exposure)
- AI Assurance Innovation Fund £200-500k from DSIT (Spring 2026)
- NLnet €30-60k grant (deadline 1 June 2026)
- First 10 paying customers via:
  - Show HN launch
  - Cold email outreach (5 OEM emails already sent April 27)
  - Cross-pollination from Templeman Opticians as clinical pilot site

## 4. CobolBridge.ai — the legacy bridge

**What it is:** Software that helps banks, government, and insurance companies convert their old (1970s-1980s) mainframe systems written in COBOL into modern code.

**Who pays:**
- **UK banks** still running COBOL (HSBC, Lloyds, NatWest, Barclays all do)
- **Government departments** (HMRC, DWP, Home Office all run COBOL)
- **Insurance companies** running mainframe
- **System integrators** (DXC, Accenture, IBM) who do the work

**What it sells:**
- **£99/month** for individual developers + small teams
- **£999/month** for enterprises
- **£25,000 one-off** for "convert this specific application"
- **£250,000+** for full-system modernisation contracts

**The current state (May 2026):**
- Domain registered, MCP built, basic version live
- 1 product currently active on Stripe
- The buyer is a small, specific market — but each customer is worth £25k+

**Why it matters:** Less heart-of-the-family business than the opticians, but **financially the biggest potential** in 12-24 months. UK government alone runs an estimated 60 BILLION lines of COBOL. Every government department modernising is a £100k-£10m contract.

**Growth path:**
- Win 1 pilot with a UK bank or insurer (target: £25k pilot in 6 months)
- List on G-Cloud and Digital Marketplace
- DASA defence call submission (£50-300k Phase 1)
