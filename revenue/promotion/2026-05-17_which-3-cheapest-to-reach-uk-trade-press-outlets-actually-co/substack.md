### The 3 Outlets (And How to Pitch Each)  
All three accept unsolicited pitches with no placement fees, and have published relevant EU AI Act SMB content in the last year. Pro tip: *verify tip-line emails directly on their contact pages*—our research found conflicting addresses for some outlets, and you don’t want your pitch bouncing.  

1. **The Register**  
   *Why they’re perfect:* Developer-focused, with a proven love for open-source AI stories (their May 2024 piece on open-source compliance tools is a perfect example of their coverage).  
   *Pitch angle:* Lead with how your 39-MCP solves developer-specific compliance headaches—like automating transparency docs for open-source models, which their audience deals with daily.  
   *Tip line:* `tips@theregister.com` (consistent across sources, but double-check their contact page to be safe).  

2. **Computer Weekly**  
   *Why they’re perfect:* Their “Regulation Watch” series is made for IT decision-makers at SMBs worried about EU AI Act risk. They’ve published multiple pieces on practical compliance for smaller teams.  
   *Pitch angle:* Frame meok.ai as a risk-mitigation tool that cuts compliance costs for resource-strapped IT teams—highlight a UK SMB beta tester who saved 20 hours on documentation, for example.  
   *Tip line:* We saw conflicting addresses (`regulation@computerweekly.com` vs. `aiact@computerweekly.com`), so verify on their submissions page before sending.  

3. **TechRadar Pro**  
   *Why they’re perfect:* Their B2B arm runs tutorial-style SME guides, perfect for step-by-step compliance walkthroughs. They accept expert contributions without fees, and their audience is exactly the SMB leaders you want to reach.  
   *Pitch angle:* Position your tool as a cost-effective, plug-and-play compliance solution—emphasize that their 39-MCP requires no enterprise budget, which resonates with their cash-strapped SMB readers.  
   *Tip line:* `editorial@techradar.com` (only one source mentioned this, so confirm via their contact page).
