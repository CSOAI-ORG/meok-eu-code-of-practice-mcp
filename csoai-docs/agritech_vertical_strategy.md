## 1. Market Overview

The UK AgriTech sector is currently valued at approximately £14 billion and is growing at a compound annual rate above 10%. AI-driven precision agriculture — autonomous vehicles, computer vision for crop health, predictive yield modelling — is the fastest-moving segment. Government investment via the UKRI Farming Innovation Programme and DEFRA's Sustainable Farming Incentive is accelerating adoption at the farm level, creating a pipeline of AI systems that will need regulatory sign-off before commercial deployment at scale.

The EU AI Act is the forcing function. Annex III's high-risk category framework captures AI systems that operate autonomously in physical environments where failure can cause material harm — autonomous tractors operating near workers, livestock monitoring systems feeding automated health interventions, robotic harvesters with unguarded cutting mechanisms. The European AI Office has not yet published definitive sector guidance for agricultural AI, but the direction is clear: systems controlling physical agricultural equipment will be treated as high-risk. UK companies selling into the EU market — which is the primary export market for British AgriTech — will need conformity evidence. MEOK AI Labs certification, built on the UK AISI Inspect framework, is the most credible route to that evidence available in the UK right now.

The key players we need to be talking to are Small Robot Company (per-plant autonomy, direct EU ambition), Muddy Machines (asparagus harvesting robot, commercially live), Burro (autonomous follow-bot, expanding to UK), Verdant Robotics (multi-action field robots, US-origin with EU appetite), and John Deere's AI division (large enterprise, longer sales cycle but enormous validation budget). Below these are 30-50 funded Series A/B UK AgriTech companies building AI systems that will hit regulatory gates within 18 months. That is our immediate addressable market.

## 2. The MEOK LABS Advantage

Every other AI safety certifier in the UK operates from an office. We operate from a 6.5-acre working farm. That is not a marketing line — it is a structural advantage that cannot be replicated quickly, and it changes what we can actually certify.

Agricultural AI fails in ways that simulation does not predict. Soil variability within a single field causes wheel slip and sensor drift that changes model confidence scores unpredictably. Lighting transitions from overcast to direct sun in UK conditions produce false positives in crop detection models at rates two to three times higher than controlled test environments suggest. Crop canopy interference — the physical occlusion of sensors by leaves, stems, and debris — is a known failure mode that almost no lab-based evaluation captures. Livestock proximity introduces unpredictable movement patterns that stress collision avoidance systems in ways that synthetic datasets do not reproduce.

We can test all of this. We can run an autonomous tractor through real headland turns with real soil conditions. We can place a drone monitoring system over real crop rows in real UK weather. We can introduce sheep into proximity with a harvesting robot and watch what happens. No simulation environment and no urban-based certifier can do this.

The result is that our certification carries evidential weight that a desk-based conformity assessment does not. When a customer presents our field test report to a notified body or to a procurement team in a large EU food business, they are presenting data from real conditions. That is worth paying for.

## 3. Service Packages for AgriTech

We offer three tiers. Each is scoped to match where a company is in its regulatory journey.

### AgriTech Quick Scan — £15,000 | 2 weeks

Designed for companies at early-stage regulatory awareness: they know the EU AI Act is coming and want to understand their exposure before it costs them a deal.

Deliverables:
- EU AI Act Annex III gap analysis specific to their system and use case
- Risk classification recommendation (high-risk / limited-risk / exempt)
- 5 field probes using our farm testbed — targeted, not exhaustive: we run the system through the five scenarios most likely to reveal failure modes relevant to their architecture
- Written report suitable for sharing with investors and legal counsel
- 90-minute debrief with findings and prioritised remediation roadmap

This package is a lead-generation engine for Full Certification. It is not a loss leader — it delivers real value — but we price it to be budget-approachable for Series A companies.

### AgriTech Full Certification — £75,000 | 6 weeks

This is the flagship product. Full MEOK AI Labs certification under the UK AISI Inspect framework with a complete field evaluation programme.

Deliverables:
- Full 16-probe evaluation covering all relevant Inspect framework dimensions
- Minimum 10 days of field testing at our farm testbed across multiple crop, soil, and weather conditions
- Incident and near-miss logging with root cause analysis
- Technical audit of training data, model architecture, and deployment constraints
- MEOK AI Labs Certificate of Conformity — our formal certification mark
- Continuous improvement recommendations with re-test scope defined

At £75k this is priced at roughly half what a comparable automotive AI safety evaluation costs. For a company selling into the EU market, this is a rounding error relative to the access it enables.

### Annual Surveillance — £20,000/year

Agricultural AI models update continuously. A certification at point of sale is stale within months. Annual Surveillance is the retention product that keeps our customers compliant as their models evolve.

Deliverables:
- Quarterly re-evaluation against a defined change log
- Two field probe sessions per year at our farm testbed
- Updated certification status letter for each quarter
- Priority access to emergency re-evaluation if a critical update is deployed between cycles

Surveillance clients get first access to new test scenarios as we develop them. This is the right incentive to retain Full Certification graduates.

## 4. Go-to-Market

We do not have the brand to wait for inbound. We go outbound, and we go now.

**Institutional outreach — April to June 2026:**

AHDB (Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board) is the right first call. They fund AgriTech adoption across the UK industry and have direct relationships with every significant UK precision agriculture programme. A meeting with their technology and innovation team puts us in the room with the entire ecosystem at once. Request a briefing, offer to co-produce a regulatory readiness guide for their members.

NFU (National Farmers Union) tech policy team is the second call. They are actively engaging with AI governance and are looking for credible voices. Being quoted in an NFU tech policy document is reach we cannot buy with advertising.

Innovate UK's AgriTech cluster managers hold the relationships with every funded AgriTech startup in the UK. A 20-minute briefing slot at one of their cluster events puts us in front of 40 potential customers. Target the Cambridge and East Midlands clusters first — highest density of relevant companies.

UKRI Farming Innovation Programme grant recipients are a pre-qualified list of companies building exactly what we certify. UKRI publishes grant recipient data. We build the list and work it.

**Conference presence — 2026 calendar:**

Agri-Tech East's REAP conference (November, Cambridge) is the primary event. It is the right audience density and it is a credible platform. We need a speaking slot, not a stand. Submit an abstract on EU AI Act implications for UK AgriTech — this will be accepted because no one else is covering it from a practitioner standpoint.

Future Farm Summit (date TBC, London) is the secondary event. Smaller, more startup-facing, good for direct pipeline conversations.

**Partnership targets:**

NIAB (National Institute of Agricultural Botany, Cambridge) is a natural fit — they run applied crop science trials and have existing relationships with AgriTech companies. A co-validation partnership where our certification includes NIAB's crop science sign-off makes our product more compelling and theirs more commercially relevant.

Rothamsted Research is the most credible agricultural science institution in the UK. An affiliation — even an informal research collaboration — adds weight to our farm testbed credentials. Approach via their AI and robotics research leads.

Harper Adams University has the UK's strongest precision agriculture academic programme and a direct pipeline of AgriTech graduates who go into the industry. A relationship here gives us early access to companies spun out of their programmes and a recruitment pipeline for technical evaluators.

## 5. Pricing Rationale

AgriTech companies can pay these prices, and they know it.

EU market access is the primary lever. A UK AgriTech company selling autonomous equipment into Germany, France, or the Netherlands is looking at a market worth tens of millions over a five-year horizon. A €500,000 deal blocked by regulatory uncertainty — or lost to a competitor who has certification — makes a £75,000 evaluation look trivial. We price against the cost of not having certification, not against the cost of running the evaluation.

Precision agriculture ROI is well-documented. Systems that pass our evaluation are systems that work reliably in field conditions. Customers increasingly understand that safety certification and performance certification overlap — a robot that fails safely is a robot that fails less. The evaluation process itself generates data that improves their system. We are selling product development value as much as compliance value.

DEFRA and AHDB both run subsidy programmes that cover technology adoption costs for farms and AgriTech developers. The Farming Innovation Programme has funded AI safety and testing work explicitly. Our Quick Scan and Annual Surveillance products are positioned to be eligible for these schemes. We should engage a grant advisor to confirm eligibility pathways and communicate this clearly in sales materials.
