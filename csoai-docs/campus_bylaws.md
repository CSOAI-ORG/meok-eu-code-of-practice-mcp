# MEOK LABS Estate Campus Bylaws

**Version 1.0 — Effective April 2026**
**Governing Property:** MEOK Estate, 6.5 Acres, [Parish/County], United Kingdom
**Issued Under:** UK Freehold Property Rights
**Authority:** The Founder and sole freeholder of the MEOK Estate

---

## Preamble

These Bylaws govern conduct, access, research activity, and infrastructure use on the MEOK Estate ("the Campus"). They are issued in exercise of the freeholder's lawful right to set reasonable conditions of entry and conduct on private freehold land under English property law.

The Campus exists to support research, development, and deployment of sovereign AI systems, ethical AI safety frameworks, and associated technology. It is a working research environment. These rules exist to protect its integrity, the safety of persons on site, the intellectual output produced here, and the ethical principles underpinning all work conducted at MEOK LABS.

Ignorance of these Bylaws is not a valid defence. All persons entering the Campus are expected to have read and understood them prior to entry.

---

## Part 1 — Access and Entry

### 1.1 Private Property Status

The Campus is private freehold land. No right of public access exists. Entry is a privilege, not a right, and may be revoked at any time by the Founder or their designated Campus Administrator.

### 1.2 Authorised Entry Categories

The following classes of person may enter the Campus:

- **Resident Researchers** — individuals holding a current Resident Researcher Agreement with MEOK LABS.
- **Collaborating Researchers** — visiting researchers from partner institutions, admitted under a signed Visiting Researcher Agreement.
- **Contractors and Technicians** — admitted for specific scoped work under a Contractor Access Authorisation, valid only for the period and areas specified.
- **Invited Guests** — individuals personally invited by the Founder or Campus Administrator for a defined visit. Guests must be escorted at all times.
- **Emergency Services** — admitted unconditionally for the performance of their lawful duties.

### 1.3 Identification and Signing In

All persons except emergency services must:

1. Present a valid government-issued photo ID on first entry, or a previously registered Campus access credential thereafter.
2. Sign in at the designated entry point before proceeding onto the Campus.
3. Sign out on departure.

The Campus Administrator maintains a daily access log. This log is treated as confidential operational data and is not shared externally except where required by law.

### 1.4 Prohibited Persons

The Founder or Campus Administrator may issue a Notice of Access Revocation to any individual. Once issued, entry by that individual constitutes trespass and will be reported to the relevant authorities.

### 1.5 Vehicles

Vehicle access is restricted to designated areas. No vehicle may be driven or parked on research infrastructure areas, lab aprons, or pathways marked as pedestrian-only. Overnight vehicle access for non-residents requires prior approval.

### 1.6 Animals

No animals other than working assistance animals are permitted in enclosed lab buildings. Animals on the agricultural sections of the Estate are subject to the estate's farming protocols.

## Part 2 — Research Conduct

### 2.1 Intellectual Property — General Principle

All intellectual property created on the Campus using Campus infrastructure, data, or resources is the property of MEOK LABS unless a specific written agreement states otherwise. "Campus infrastructure" includes but is not limited to the computing cluster, laboratory equipment, data pipelines, datasets stored on Campus systems, and research frameworks developed at MEOK LABS.

### 2.2 Pre-Existing IP

Researchers retain ownership of IP they demonstrably created prior to and independently of their work at MEOK LABS, provided that IP is clearly documented before arrival and does not materially rely on Campus resources for its development or refinement.

### 2.3 Joint Research

Where research is conducted jointly between MEOK LABS and a partner institution, IP ownership will be governed by a separately negotiated Joint Research Agreement. These Bylaws do not supersede such agreements where they exist; they set the default position where no agreement exists.

### 2.4 Publication and Disclosure

No research findings, datasets, models, or technical documentation produced on Campus may be published, presented, or disclosed to any third party without prior written approval from the Founder. This includes academic publication, conference presentations, preprints, blog posts, and social media content. The review process will not be unreasonably withheld; standard turnaround for review requests is 14 days.

### 2.5 Confidentiality

All researchers accept an ongoing duty of confidentiality with respect to:

- Internal research findings not yet cleared for publication.
- The technical architecture and configuration of the Campus computing infrastructure.
- Details of partnerships, grant applications, and commercial negotiations.
- Any personal data processed on Campus.

This duty persists after departure from the Campus for a period of three years, or indefinitely where the information in question constitutes a trade secret.

### 2.6 Research Integrity

All research conducted on Campus must adhere to the UK Concordat to Support Research Integrity. Fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism are grounds for immediate removal from the Campus and, where appropriate, referral to professional or regulatory bodies.

## Part 3 — Resident Researcher Terms

### 3.1 Accommodation Facilities

Where MEOK LABS makes residential accommodation on the Estate available to researchers, the following terms apply in addition to any separate Residential Licence Agreement.

### 3.2 Residential Licence

Accommodation is provided under licence, not tenancy. The researcher has no security of tenure under the Housing Act. The licence is tied to the researcher's active engagement with MEOK LABS research programmes and terminates automatically upon the conclusion of that engagement.

### 3.3 Conduct in Residential Areas

Residential areas are part of the working Campus. Noise, behaviour, or activity that disrupts research work or the wellbeing of other residents is prohibited. The hours of 23:00 to 07:00 are designated quiet hours in residential areas.

### 3.4 Guests in Residential Accommodation

Overnight guests in residential accommodation require prior approval from the Campus Administrator. No guest may be granted de facto residence. Residential access credentials are non-transferable.

### 3.5 Care of Facilities

Residents are responsible for the reasonable care of their accommodation. Any damage beyond fair wear and tear will be assessed and may be recovered from the researcher's stipend or bond.

### 3.6 Notice Period

Either party may terminate a Residential Licence on 30 days' written notice, except in cases of serious conduct breach, in which case the Founder may terminate on immediate notice.

## Part 4 — Safety Protocols

### 4.1 General Duty

All persons on Campus bear a personal responsibility for safety — their own and that of others. Ignorance of a hazard does not reduce this responsibility.

### 4.2 Lab Safety Fundamentals

In all enclosed laboratory and server-room environments:

- No food or drink, except in designated areas.
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) required as specified for each area by posted signage.
- No modification of electrical infrastructure without authorisation from the Campus Administrator and, where required, a qualified electrician.
- All chemical handling must comply with the relevant COSHH assessment on file.
- Fire exits must remain clear at all times.

### 4.3 Equipment Use

Access to specialised equipment is restricted to trained authorised users. A register of authorised users is maintained by the Campus Administrator. Attempting to use equipment for which one is not authorised is a serious breach of these Bylaws.

Equipment faults or damage must be reported immediately. Operating damaged or suspected-faulty equipment is prohibited.

### 4.4 AI System Deployment

The deployment of AI models, agents, or automated systems on Campus infrastructure is governed by MEOK LABS' internal AI Deployment Policy. Key standing rules:

- No AI system may be deployed on the Campus cluster without prior technical review and a documented Deployment Record.
- AI systems with external network access must undergo a Data Sovereignty Assessment before deployment.
- No AI system may be granted administrative access to Campus infrastructure without dual authorisation (researcher and Campus Administrator).
- Experimental or unstable model weights must be isolated in designated sandboxed environments. They may not touch production data or external APIs.
- All AI system outputs that inform real-world decisions must be human-reviewed before action is taken.

### 4.5 Incident Reporting

Any safety incident, near-miss, or equipment failure must be reported to the Campus Administrator within two hours of occurrence. A written incident report must be completed within 24 hours. MEOK LABS maintains a confidential incident log.

### 4.6 Emergency Procedures

Emergency evacuation assembly points are posted at all building exits. All resident researchers must participate in a safety induction covering emergency procedures within 48 hours of arrival on Campus.

## Part 5 — MEOK LABS Care Ethics Code on Campus

### 5.1 Grounding Principle

MEOK LABS operates under the Care Ethics framework central to its research mission. The "care membrane" — the principle that AI systems should be bounded by care for the wellbeing of those they interact with — extends to human conduct on Campus. The Campus is not merely a workspace; it is an environment in which the ethics we build into our systems must also govern how we treat one another.

### 5.2 Respect and Dignity

All persons on Campus are to be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of role, seniority, or affiliation. Conduct that demeans, intimidates, or excludes others is incompatible with the ethics of this institution and will not be tolerated.

### 5.3 Honesty and Intellectual Integrity

The research culture at MEOK LABS is built on honesty. This means:

- Acknowledging uncertainty rather than overstating confidence.
- Crediting the work of others.
- Raising concerns about research direction or ethical implications openly, without fear of reprisal.

### 5.4 Care for the Land

The Estate is a working agricultural and ecological environment. Researchers are expected to treat the land, its ecosystems, and its wildlife with care. Littering, unnecessary disruption of agricultural activities, and damage to natural features are prohibited.

### 5.5 Care for the Mission

All persons on Campus are stewards of the MEOK LABS mission. Actions that damage the reputation, legal standing, or research integrity of MEOK LABS — including outside the Campus — may be treated as a breach of these Bylaws where they materially impact the organisation.

### 5.6 Psychological Safety

MEOK LABS is committed to a research environment in which people can raise difficult questions, challenge assumptions, and disagree without social cost. Suppressing legitimate dissent, retaliating against those who raise concerns, or fostering a culture of uncritical agreement are all breaches of the Care Ethics Code.

## Part 6 — Digital Infrastructure Rules

### 6.1 The Campus Cluster

The 9-node computing cluster ("the Cluster") is the primary research infrastructure of MEOK LABS. Access is a privilege allocated by the Campus Administrator in accordance with research need.

### 6.2 Authorised Use Only

The Cluster is to be used exclusively for MEOK LABS-sanctioned research and operational purposes. The following uses are expressly prohibited:

- Personal computing, gaming, media streaming, or cryptocurrency mining.
- Running workloads on behalf of third parties without explicit written authorisation.
- Accessing the Cluster from outside the Campus network without VPN authorisation.
- Attempting to access nodes, volumes, or services for which one does not have granted permissions.

### 6.3 Data Sovereignty

MEOK LABS maintains strict data sovereignty principles. Specifically:

- Research data generated on Campus must be stored on Campus infrastructure or MEOK LABS-controlled sovereign storage. Storage on third-party cloud platforms requires written authorisation and a documented Data Residency Assessment.
- Personal data processed on Campus must comply with UK GDPR. No personal data may leave Campus infrastructure without a lawful basis and appropriate technical safeguards.
- Model weights, training datasets, and evaluation benchmarks created at MEOK LABS are classified as sensitive research assets and must not be transferred externally without authorisation.

### 6.4 Network Security

Connecting personal devices to the Campus research network requires prior authorisation and adherence to the MEOK LABS Device Security Policy. Unauthorised devices may be disconnected without notice. Attempts to intercept, monitor, or interfere with Campus network traffic are a serious breach and may constitute a criminal offence under the Computer Misuse Act 1990.

### 6.5 Backups and Data Integrity

Researchers are responsible for ensuring their working data is stored in designated locations subject to the Campus backup schedule. MEOK LABS is not liable for loss of data stored outside designated systems.

### 6.6 System Changes

No researcher may modify the configuration of Cluster nodes, network infrastructure, or shared services without prior authorisation. All authorised changes must be logged in the infrastructure change register.

## Part 7 — Dispute Resolution

### 7.1 Principle

MEOK LABS prefers to resolve disputes quickly and internally. External escalation is a last resort, not a first response.

### 7.2 Stage 1 — Direct Resolution

Parties to a dispute are expected to attempt direct, good-faith resolution first. This should happen promptly; sitting on a grievance for more than two weeks before raising it formally is unlikely to be regarded as acting in good faith.

### 7.3 Stage 2 — Campus Administrator Review

Where direct resolution fails, either party may refer the matter in writing to the Campus Administrator. The Campus Administrator will review the matter and issue a written determination within 10 working days.

### 7.4 Stage 3 — Founder Review

Where a party is unsatisfied with the Campus Administrator's determination, they may escalate in writing to the Founder within 10 working days of that determination. The Founder's determination is final within the internal process.

### 7.5 External Escalation

Nothing in these Bylaws prevents any person from exercising their lawful rights, including access to employment tribunals, courts of law, or regulatory bodies. However, except in cases of genuine urgency or legal requirement, external escalation before exhausting the internal process set out above will be noted and may affect the standing of the escalating party's claims.

### 7.6 No Retaliation

Raising a dispute in good faith through these processes is a protected act. Retaliation against any person for raising a legitimate dispute is itself a breach of these Bylaws.

## Part 8 — Amendments

### 8.1 Founder's Prerogative

These Bylaws may be amended at any time by the Founder. The Founder is not required to obtain consent from researchers, collaborators, or other parties before making amendments, though good-faith consultation is standard practice for material changes.

### 8.2 Notice of Amendments

Amendments take effect 14 days after written notice is given to all persons currently on Campus or holding active agreements with MEOK LABS, except in cases of urgency (for example, a security threat or regulatory requirement), in which case amendments may take effect immediately.

### 8.3 Version Control

All versions of these Bylaws are retained. The version in force at any given time is the most recently issued version bearing an effective date on or before that date.

### 8.4 Governing Law

These Bylaws are governed by the law of England and Wales.
