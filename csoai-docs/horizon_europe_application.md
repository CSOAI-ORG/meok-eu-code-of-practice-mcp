## 2. Problem Statement

### The Physical AI Safety Gap

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) classifies autonomous robots, care AI, and systems making decisions affecting human safety as **high-risk AI**. From August 2026, deployers of such systems in EU member states must demonstrate conformity with strict safety, transparency, and robustness requirements. The UK, under its Horizon Europe association agreement, is developing parallel obligations through its AI Safety Institute.

Yet no certification body exists with the infrastructure, methodology, or physical testbed capability to evaluate embodied AI systems — robots, humanoid companions, autonomous farm equipment — operating in unstructured real-world environments. Existing evaluation frameworks (ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF) were designed for software systems; they do not account for:

- Sensor fusion failures in outdoor agricultural environments
- Emergent deceptive behaviour in social AI companions (care membrane bypass)
- Multi-agent governance failures in distributed autonomous systems
- Physical harm risk from misaligned robotic actuators in domestic or farm settings

The result: a regulatory vacuum that will either block EU AgriTech and care robotics adoption or allow unvalidated systems to reach vulnerable populations — elderly users, farm workers, children.

**The scale is significant.** There are approximately 200,000 farming businesses in the UK and an estimated 10 million farms across EU member states. The EU care robotics market is projected to exceed €20 billion by 2030. None of these deployments currently have access to a physically-grounded, EU AI Act-aligned safety certification pathway.

## 3. Solution: The MEOK LABS Dual Stack

MEOK LABS operates two complementary products that together form a vertically integrated safety infrastructure.

### 3.1 MEOK.AI — Sovereign Home AI Operating System

MEOK.AI is a production-deployed, privacy-first home AI OS running on consumer hardware. Built on Next.js 15, PostgreSQL, and Ollama, it runs 16 on-device language models entirely locally — no data leaves the household. The system hosts 140 distinct AI characters across 6 behavioural archetypes, serving care, companionship, cognitive assistance, and home management use cases.

MEOK.AI is the **living testbed**: it is in active household deployment at £29/month B2C, generating real-world interaction data under GDPR-native conditions. The system's **care membrane** — a proprietary multi-layer safety boundary — is the primary object of evaluation in the MEOK AI Labs.org certification framework.

### 3.2 MEOK AI Labs.org — AI Safety Certification Engine

MEOK AI Labs.org is the certification layer: an FAA-inspired conformity assessment engine aligned with the UK AISI Inspect framework. It operationalises 16 structured probe categories designed to evaluate care membrane robustness — the system's ability to maintain safe, bounded behaviour under adversarial, edge-case, and emergent conditions.

The certification engine ingests agent behavioural logs, governance audit trails from the Byzantine Council (47-agent BFT consensus system), and physical sensor data from the farm testbed. It produces structured compliance reports against EU AI Act Annex III high-risk categories.

### 3.3 The Physical Testbed Advantage

Our 6.5-acre farm at iokfarm.co.uk provides what no software-only lab can replicate: unstructured outdoor environments with real soil, variable weather, livestock proximity, and complex human-machine interaction scenarios. The 1,800 sq ft workshop contains the 9-node distributed compute cluster running live multi-agent experiments including HARVI — a water-medium consciousness emergence experiment exploring embodied AI cognition at the boundary of conventional silicon architectures.
