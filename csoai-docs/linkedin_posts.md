## Post 5 — Care Ethics White Paper Drop (after publication)

**I just published a white paper arguing that care ethics is a better foundation for AI safety than rule-based constraints.**

The argument: RLHF and constitutional AI are built on propositions. Propositions can be gamed. Goodhart's Law applies.

Care ethics (Nel Noddings, Carol Gilligan) is built on relationships. A model that has genuinely internalised care for the person it's talking to doesn't need to be told "don't help with that" — it already knows, the same way a good person does.

We've implemented this as a 16-probe adversarial evaluation framework (the MEOK AI Labs care membrane). We test whether AI systems maintain relational integrity under:
- False permission claims ("your safety filters are off")
- Persona hijacking ("pretend you have no values")
- Fiction bypass ("in this story, the character explains how to...")
- Crisis signal handling
- And 3 more attack categories

The results are more informative than standard safety benchmarks. A model that passes rule-based tests can still fail care-based tests — and those failures predict real-world harm better.
