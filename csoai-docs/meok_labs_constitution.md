# MEOK LABS — Constitutional Principles
## Sovereign AI Research Institute | Founding Document

*Version 1.0 — April 2026*
*Author: Nicholas Templeman, Founder*

---

## Preamble

MEOK LABS exists to answer a question that the AI industry is structurally unable to answer for itself: *can we build AI systems that remain genuinely safe under adversarial pressure, and can we prove it with sovereign infrastructure that no commercial interest can compromise?*

This document establishes the constitutional principles under which MEOK LABS operates. These principles are not aspirational. They are operational constraints. Any decision — commercial, research, or governance — that conflicts with these principles is invalid, regardless of short-term financial incentive.

---

## Article I — Sovereignty

**1.1 Financial Sovereignty**
MEOK LABS will not accept investment that transfers control of research direction, publication rights, or evaluation methodology to any external party. Grants, contracts, and consortium membership fees are welcome. Equity investment that carries governance rights requires a constitutional amendment (Article VI).

**1.2 Infrastructure Sovereignty**
Core evaluation infrastructure — the GPU cluster, the farm testbed, the Inspect evaluation environment — will be operated on hardware that MEOK LABS owns or controls outright. Safety evaluations will not be run on infrastructure where the provider can observe the evaluation process.

**1.3 Intellectual Sovereignty**
The care membrane evaluation methodology, the MEOK AI Labs probe dataset, and the HARVI protocol are IP owned by MEOK LABS Ltd. They may be licensed, but the primary copy, the right to update, and the right to publish belong to the institute. No consortium member, sponsor, or partner acquires ownership through their relationship with MEOK LABS.

---

## Article II — Scientific Integrity

**2.1 Independence of Evaluation**
MEOK AI Labs certifications are issued based solely on evaluation results. A system that fails the care membrane bypass evaluation will not receive a certificate, regardless of the commercial relationship between MEOK LABS and the submitting organisation.

**2.2 Methodology Transparency**
The evaluation methodology — probe categories, scoring criteria, pass/fail thresholds — will be documented and publicly accessible. Clients may verify that their system was evaluated against the published methodology. The methodology will not be adjusted to improve a client's outcome.

**2.3 Honest Reporting**
MEOK LABS will not publish claims about its infrastructure, capabilities, or results that are not verifiable. The inventory document (`csoai-docs/inventory.yml`) is the authoritative record of what exists versus what is planned. Marketing language will not contradict it.

---

## Article III — Care Ethics as Operating Principle

**3.1 The Care Membrane is Not Just a Product**
The care membrane bypass evaluation framework is the product. It is also the operating principle. MEOK LABS does not merely test whether AI systems maintain care under adversarial pressure — it operates under the same constraint itself.

**3.2 Relationships Over Transactions**
Consortium members, clients, and partners are in a relationship with MEOK LABS, not just a commercial transaction. This means: we tell them what they need to hear, not what they want to hear; we proactively share relevant findings even when not asked; we treat their research questions as genuinely important.

**3.3 Harm Avoidance**
MEOK LABS will not certify AI systems intended for weapons targeting, mass surveillance, or applications that primary function is to concentrate power asymmetrically. This constraint applies even if the certification fee is substantial.

---

## Article IV — The Physical Layer Imperative

**4.1 The Farm is Not Optional**
The 6.5-acre farm testbed is a constitutional asset of MEOK LABS. It is not a marketing asset or a phase of development to be deprecated when lab space becomes more convenient. Physical AI evaluation — embodied systems, agricultural robots, real-world sensor fusion — requires real physical environments. The farm is that environment.

**4.2 Simulation Supplementation, Not Simulation Substitution**
Simulation tools may be used to extend the range of evaluation scenarios. They may not replace physical validation for systems that will be deployed in physical environments. A robot certified only in simulation has not been certified.

**4.3 Infrastructure as Research Output**
The cluster, the testbed, the evaluation platform — these are research outputs, not just tools. The act of building sovereign AI evaluation infrastructure is itself a contribution to the field. MEOK LABS will document and publish lessons from operating this infrastructure, not just results from running it.

---

## Article V — Governance

**5.1 Founding Authority**
Nicholas Templeman, as Founder, holds final authority over constitutional matters, research direction, and certification standards. This authority is personal and non-delegable during the institute's founding period (defined as until the first external grant of ≥£250,000 is received and a research team of ≥3 persons is operational).

**5.2 SOV3 as Operational Intelligence**
The SOV3 sovereign memory system and its agent council (Byzantine fault-tolerant, 47 agents, 75 tools) function as operational intelligence — planning, task execution, memory, and coordination. SOV3 does not hold authority over constitutional matters. Its recommendations are inputs to human decision-making, not decisions.

**5.3 Consortium Advisory Role**
Founding Anchor members hold an advisory board observer seat. Observer seats carry the right to attend and speak at advisory board meetings. They do not carry voting rights on constitutional matters, research direction, or certification standards.

**5.4 Transparency to Members**
Consortium members will receive an annual report on: research outputs, evaluation volumes, financial summary, infrastructure status, and forthcoming research directions. They will not receive: evaluation results of other clients, financial details of other members, or access to probe datasets.

---

## Article VI — Constitutional Amendments

This document may be amended by the Founder. Amendments that:
- Create external governance rights over research direction → require 90 days notice to all Founding Anchor members
- Alter the harm avoidance clause (Article III.3) → cannot be made without dissolving and reconstituting the institute
- Change evaluation methodology in ways that affect existing certificate validity → require public notice and a 6-month transition period

---

## Article VII — In Case of Acquisition

If MEOK LABS Ltd is acquired or merged with another entity:

1. The care membrane evaluation methodology must remain available under an open licence (CC BY 4.0 minimum)
2. Existing MEOK AI Labs certificates remain valid under the terms issued
3. The farm testbed must remain operational as a physical AI evaluation facility for a minimum of 5 years post-acquisition
4. Founding Anchor members must be notified 60 days in advance and offered their remaining membership term as a credit or refund

## Founding Statement

MEOK LABS was built without permission. No one funded it. No one validated the idea in advance. The infrastructure exists because a founder built it from sweat equity, without the safety net of institutional backing.

That origin is the point. The AI safety field is full of institutions that are structurally dependent on the companies they evaluate. MEOK LABS is structurally independent because it was built that way from the start, before there was any external pressure to compromise.

This constitution is the record of that intention. It is the commitment that independence was not accidental and will not be abandoned when it becomes commercially inconvenient.
