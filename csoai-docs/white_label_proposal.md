Every enterprise deploying AI will need documented, repeatable safety evaluation — and they will pay for it from an accredited body they already trust.

Our 16-probe Inspect framework runs structured adversarial tests across refusal behaviour, hallucination rates, jailbreak resistance, bias vectors, and alignment drift. Results are scored, versioned, and stored against a reproducible methodology. We operate a 9-node GPU cluster capable of evaluating frontier and open-weight models at production scale. Our care membrane methodology provides a theoretically grounded, auditable safety posture score that survives regulatory scrutiny.

What we do not have — and do not need to build — is your accreditation, your client relationships, and your brand.

TUV Rheinland, SGS, BSI Group, Bureau Veritas, and UL Solutions collectively certify the largest share of enterprise products on earth. Your clients trust your mark. Your UKAS, DAkkS, ANAB, and ILAC credentials make a certificate legally defensible. Your enterprise sales teams are already inside the procurement cycles where AI safety certificates will be required.

The proposition is direct: you bring the accreditation stamp and the client channel; MEOK AI Labs provides the technical engine under your brand. The certificate reads "BSI AI Safety Certificate" (or your equivalent). The methodology, the probe dataset, the Inspect runner, and the scoring logic are licensed to you — they remain MEOK AI Labs's intellectual property. You do not own the methodology; you licence the right to run it and stamp it.

This is not a long integration project. The white-label package is deployable in under 90 days: your branding applied to the certificate template, your team trained on result interpretation, API access to the evaluation runner. MEOK AI Labs handles all model updates, probe maintenance, and framework evolution as the regulatory landscape shifts.

The commercial terms are clean. A £50,000 annual platform licence covers access rights and support. Each certification processed carries a £500 per-certificate fee — no volume commitment, no minimums. Optional dedicated cluster access for high-throughput clients is available at a separately agreed compute rate. At 200 certifications per year, the arrangement generates £150,000 annually from a single partner. At 1,000 certifications, £550,000.

The window to establish this product before competitors replicate it is 12 to 18 months. First mover advantage in accredited AI safety certification is structurally durable — switching an accreditation relationship is expensive for enterprise clients.
