# MEOK Innovation Campus Registration Strategy

**Version 1.0 — April 2026**
**Property:** MEOK Estate, 6.5 Acres
**Author:** MEOK LABS Strategic Development

---

## Executive Summary

The MEOK Estate has the underlying characteristics of a legitimate research campus: dedicated computing infrastructure, a defined research programme, and a bounded physical site. Formalising this through recognised UK innovation campus and science park frameworks unlocks material financial and operational benefits — most significantly reduced or eliminated business rates, preferential planning treatment, and eligibility for UKRI-backed grant instruments that require a named campus affiliation.

This document maps the principal pathways, their requirements, and the recommended execution sequence.

---

## Part 1 — UK Science Park Association (UKSPA)

### 1.1 What UKSPA Is

The UK Science Park Association is the industry body representing science parks, technology parks, and innovation centres across the UK. Membership is widely recognised by UKRI, Innovate UK, local enterprise partnerships (LEPs), and university research offices as a quality signal for research infrastructure. Many grant instruments reference UKSPA membership explicitly or implicitly as evidence of a serious research environment.

### 1.2 UKSPA Membership Categories

UKSPA has two primary membership categories relevant to MEOK:

**Full Science Park/Innovation Centre Member** — for established science parks with a track record of supporting multiple tenant companies or research groups.

**Associate Member** — available to emerging science and technology organisations, smaller innovation spaces, and organisations at an earlier stage of formalisation. This is the realistic entry point for MEOK at present.

Associate membership fees are in the low hundreds of pounds per annum and are not financially prohibitive.

### 1.3 UKSPA Membership Criteria

UKSPA does not publish a rigid checklist, but consistent membership criteria drawn from their documentation and member profiles include:

- A defined physical site with dedicated research or technology activity.
- Formal organisational structure (company registration, or equivalent).
- Evidence of supporting research, innovation, or technology development — not merely commercial office space.
- A commitment to the UKSPA Code of Practice, which covers community, sustainability, and quality of provision.

MEOK's AI research programme, computing infrastructure, and care ethics framework map well to these criteria. The key gap to address before application is **formal documentation of research activity** — publications, grant applications in progress, or formal collaboration agreements with a university or research institute.

### 1.4 UKSPA Benefits

- Listing in the UKSPA directory (used by researchers seeking affiliated sites and by grant bodies verifying campus claims).
- Access to UKSPA's network of member parks for collaboration, knowledge sharing, and joint bids.
- Eligibility for UKSPA-facilitated programmes and funding routes.
- Credibility in grant applications citing "UKSPA-member campus" as the research base.
- Introductions to LEPs, local authorities, and development agencies that support UKSPA members.

### 1.5 Application Process

1. Review current UKSPA membership criteria on ukspa.org.uk and contact their membership team for a pre-application conversation. UKSPA is approachable and will give honest guidance on fit.
2. Prepare a site summary covering: physical description, research activities, organisational structure, and a brief on MEOK LABS' mission.
3. Submit associate membership application with supporting documentation.
4. Anticipate a 4–8 week review and decision period.

**Realistic timeline: 2–3 months from first contact to membership.**

## Part 2 — Enterprise Zone Status

### 2.1 What Enterprise Zones Are

Enterprise Zones (EZs) are designated geographic areas in England where businesses and organisations benefit from a package of incentives including business rate discounts, simplified planning rules, and (historically) enhanced capital allowances. They are designated by central government, typically on the recommendation of Local Enterprise Partnerships (LEPs) or, since the LEP consolidation, mayoral combined authorities and local authorities.

### 2.2 How EZ Status Is Designated

Enterprise Zones are not applied for by individual landowners. Designation happens at a geographic level — a local authority or combined authority proposes an area, and central government (via the relevant department, currently MHCLG or DBT depending on the instrument) approves the zone. Individual sites within a zone then benefit automatically.

**Therefore: the MEOK Estate cannot apply to become an Enterprise Zone in isolation.** The route is:

1. Determine whether the area surrounding the MEOK Estate is already within a designated Enterprise Zone. Check: enterprisezones.communities.gov.uk or the relevant local authority's economic development team.
2. If not currently in an EZ, engage the local authority's economic development department to make the case for inclusion of the area in any future EZ designation or review.
3. Alternatively, engage the regional mayoral combined authority or LEP successor body to understand whether a new EZ or Investment Zone proposal is under consideration for the area.

### 2.3 Investment Zones — The Current Instrument

The Investment Zones programme (launched 2023, expanded 2024–2025) is the more active current instrument and is more relevant to a research-focused site like MEOK. Investment Zones are designed specifically around clusters of research, technology, and innovation. They offer:

- **Enhanced Capital Allowances** — 100% first-year relief on qualifying plant and machinery.
- **Structures and Buildings Allowance** — accelerated relief on construction costs.
- **Business Rates Relief** — 100% relief for eligible businesses in the zone for a defined period.
- **Reduced Stamp Duty Land Tax** on commercial land transactions within the zone.
- **UKRI research cluster funding** — Investment Zones are intended to anchor knowledge-intensive clusters.

Investment Zones are announced by government in partnership with a lead university or research anchor institution. The most direct route for MEOK is to **position the Estate as a satellite site within an Investment Zone anchored by a nearby university**.

### 2.4 Practical Steps for Enterprise Zone / Investment Zone Access

1. Contact the local authority's economic development team. Ask whether the Estate's postcode falls within any current EZ or Investment Zone designation.
2. If not: identify the nearest designated Investment Zone (check UKRI's Investment Zone programme pages) and contact the programme office about satellite site affiliation.
3. In parallel, identify the relevant university research office and propose a formal research collaboration agreement — this is often the mechanism by which satellite sites gain Investment Zone access.
4. Engage a specialist commercial property advisor with EZ/IZ experience for formal site assessment. This is not optional if the financial benefits are material — the designation criteria are technical and advice is valuable.

## Part 3 — Planning Reclassification

### 3.1 Current Likely Status

Agricultural land in England is classified under the Town and Country Planning Act 1990 and associated Use Class Orders. A 6.5-acre working farm will currently sit in an **agricultural use class** (sui generis for agricultural purposes, or Class B land in older designations). Research and laboratory use is generally Class E (commercial, business, and service) under the current Use Classes Order.

Operating a research campus on agricultural land without the appropriate planning permission is a planning enforcement risk. The scale and visibility of the activity determines the risk level, but it is prudent to regularise this before pursuing formal registration as an innovation campus.

### 3.2 Permitted Development Rights

Some change of use between agricultural buildings and other uses is possible without full planning permission under Permitted Development Rights (PDR), specifically:

- **Class Q** — conversion of agricultural buildings to residential use (not directly relevant here but relevant if adding researcher accommodation).
- **Class R** — conversion of agricultural buildings to a flexible commercial use (Class E), subject to conditions. Maximum 500 square metres per agricultural unit.

Class R PDR is potentially relevant for converting existing farm buildings to research/lab use without full planning permission, subject to a Prior Approval application to the local planning authority (LPA). This is significantly faster and cheaper than a full planning application.

### 3.3 Full Change of Use Application

For the broader site, or for activity exceeding PDR limits, a full planning application will be required to authorise mixed agricultural and research use. Key elements:

- **Planning Statement** — setting out the proposed uses, their compatibility with the development plan, and national planning policy (NPPF).
- **Design and Access Statement** — where new buildings or material changes to buildings are proposed.
- **Heritage, ecology, and highways assessments** — depending on local conditions (proximity to listed buildings, SSSIs, road access).
- Engagement with the LPA's pre-application advice service before formal submission is strongly recommended. Most LPAs offer paid pre-application meetings with planning officers. This shapes the application before submission and significantly reduces refusal risk.

### 3.4 Mixed Use (Agricultural and Research)

There is established precedent for rural research campuses in England operating under mixed agricultural and research use designations. The key planning argument is that the research use is **ancillary to and compatible with** the agricultural use, and that it brings economic and knowledge benefits to the rural area consistent with the NPPF's support for rural enterprise and innovation.

**If the research activity is described as agricultural technology research (agritech), this argument is materially stronger** and may allow the research use to be treated as incidental to agricultural use rather than requiring a separate Class E designation.

### 3.5 Recommended Approach

1. Commission a planning consultant to review current lawful use and advise on the lowest-risk route to formal research use authorisation.
2. Prioritise Class R PDR Prior Approval for any existing agricultural buildings to be used as labs — this can be done quickly.
3. Prepare a full change of use application for the broader site if activities exceed PDR scope, framing the research as agritech/rural innovation.
4. Ensure any planning applications reference the innovation campus strategy and UKSPA membership (once obtained) as evidence of the seriousness of the research use.

**Realistic timeline for PDR Prior Approval: 2–3 months. Full planning application: 6–12 months.**

## Part 4 — Business Rates

### 4.1 The Default Position

Non-domestic properties in England are subject to business rates assessed by the Valuation Office Agency (VOA). Agricultural land and buildings are exempt from business rates. However, once land or buildings are repurposed for non-agricultural use — including research — they become rateable.

### 4.2 Small Business Rate Relief

If the campus operates from a single property with a rateable value below £15,000, 100% Small Business Rate Relief (SBRR) applies. Between £15,000 and £51,000, a tapered relief applies. For a small rural research site, SBRR may eliminate business rates entirely. This relief is applied automatically if MEOK LABS is registered for business rates and meets the rateable value threshold.

### 4.3 Rural Rate Relief

Properties in rural areas (designated rural settlement lists maintained by local authorities) with a rateable value below £8,500 may qualify for Rural Rate Relief of up to 100%. The local authority has discretion to extend this. Worth investigating the Estate's location against the rural settlement list.

### 4.4 Charitable and Research Institution Relief

Where MEOK LABS operates as a charitable organisation or community interest company with a research mission, up to 80% mandatory charitable rate relief applies. Local authorities have discretion to grant an additional 20% on top (making it 100% relief). This is one of the most material potential savings.

**Action: Assess whether MEOK LABS' research mission and structure support a charitable registration or CIC conversion. This is also beneficial for grant eligibility and public credibility.**

### 4.5 Research Facility Exemption — Limited Scope

There is no blanket statutory exemption for research facilities from business rates in England (unlike, for example, some agricultural buildings). However, the Valuation Office Agency's Rating Manual gives guidance on how research and development buildings are valued, and specialist research infrastructure (computing equipment, lab fittings) is generally excluded from the rateable value assessment — only the building structure itself is rated. This can significantly reduce the rateable value compared to a general commercial property.

### 4.6 Process for Business Rates Assessment

1. Contact the local authority's business rates team once the planning position is regularised. Do not trigger a business rates assessment prematurely — only register when the lawful use is confirmed.
2. Obtain the VOA's rateable value assessment for the premises. This can be challenged through the Check, Challenge, Appeal process if the value appears incorrect.
3. Apply simultaneously for Small Business Rate Relief and, if eligible, Rural Rate Relief and/or charitable rate relief.
4. Instruct a specialist rating surveyor to review the VOA assessment — particularly to ensure that research equipment and infrastructure are correctly excluded from the rateable value.

## Part 5 — Step-by-Step Application Sequence

The following sequence is designed to avoid wasted effort (pursuing later steps before earlier ones are secured), manage regulatory risk (not exposing the Campus to enforcement before it is regularised), and maximise the financial benefit of each step.

---

### Step 1 — Internal Preparation (Month 1)

**Objective:** Get the basic documentation and organisational structure in order before approaching any external bodies.

Tasks:
- Confirm MEOK LABS' legal entity status. Verify Companies House registration, SIC code, and registered address. Update SIC code to reflect research activity (SIC 72190 — Other Research and Experimental Development on Natural Sciences and Engineering, or SIC 62090/62099 for AI/software research).
- Draft a one-page research programme summary suitable for external audiences: what is being researched, why it matters, what infrastructure exists, and what outputs are expected.
- Commission a planning consultant for an initial review of the Estate's current lawful use and a written opinion on the lowest-risk route to regularising research use.
- Document existing research activity formally: lab notebooks, code repositories, any publications or presentations, grant applications in progress.

---

### Step 2 — Planning Regularisation (Months 2–5)

**Objective:** Secure the planning basis for research use before making any public claims about innovation campus status.

Tasks:
- Submit Class R Prior Approval application for conversion of existing agricultural buildings to Class E research use (where applicable, within PDR limits).
- If full change of use is needed for broader site activity, instruct planning consultants to prepare and submit a full planning application.
- Frame all applications around agritech/rural innovation where honest to do so — this aligns with national planning policy support for rural enterprise.

**Milestone: Written confirmation from the LPA of lawful research use, either via Prior Approval decision or planning permission.**

---

### Step 3 — UKSPA Associate Membership Application (Months 3–6)

**Objective:** Secure UKSPA membership to establish recognised innovation campus status.

Tasks:
- Contact UKSPA membership team for a pre-application conversation.
- Prepare membership application using the research programme summary and, ideally, the planning confirmation from Step 2.
- Reference any university or research institution relationships.
- Submit application.

**Milestone: UKSPA Associate Member status confirmed. Update all grant applications, website, and correspondence to reference UKSPA membership.**

---

### Step 4 — Business Rates Assessment and Relief (Months 4–6, following planning confirmation)

**Objective:** Register for business rates correctly and secure maximum available relief.

Tasks:
- Once planning regularisation is confirmed, contact the local authority business rates team to declare the property for rating.
- Instruct a specialist rating surveyor to review the VOA assessment.
- Apply for Small Business Rate Relief, Rural Rate Relief (if applicable), and charitable/CIC rate relief if the entity qualifies.

**Milestone: Business rates bill reduced to minimum lawful amount, ideally zero.**

---

### Step 5 — Enterprise Zone / Investment Zone Engagement (Months 6–12)

**Objective:** Secure formal EZ or IZ affiliation or begin the groundwork for a future designation.

Tasks:
- Determine whether the Estate's postcode is within an existing EZ or Investment Zone.
- If yes: contact the zone management team directly about formalising MEOK's participation and accessing the associated tax reliefs.
- If no: engage the local authority's economic development team and the relevant regional body (combined authority, Mayoral Development Corporation, or equivalent) to make the case for including the area in a future designation.
- Identify the nearest Investment Zone anchor university and open a conversation about satellite site affiliation or a formal research collaboration agreement.

**Milestone: Either formal confirmation of existing EZ/IZ inclusion, or a documented engagement plan with the relevant bodies for future inclusion.**

---

### Step 6 — UKRI Campus Status and Grant Eligibility (Months 9–18)

**Objective:** Leverage UKSPA membership and planning status to access UKRI grant instruments requiring a named campus.

Tasks:
- Review current UKRI funding opportunities relevant to MEOK's research areas (AI safety, sovereign AI, agritech, care ethics in AI). Key instruments: UKRI Future Leaders Fellowships, Innovate UK Knowledge Transfer Partnerships (KTPs), Innovate UK Smart Grants, ARIA (if open).
- Ensure that MEOK LABS is registered on the Je-S (Joint Electronic Submissions) system, which is required for UKRI applications.
- For grants requiring institutional affiliation (typically UKRI Research Council instruments), identify a university partner willing to act as the lead institution with MEOK LABS as a named non-academic partner or co-investigator. UKSPA membership significantly supports this conversation.
- For Innovate UK instruments, MEOK LABS can typically apply directly as a company.

**Milestone: At least one active UKRI or Innovate UK application naming the MEOK Estate campus as the research site.**

## Key Professional Advisors to Engage

- **Planning Consultant** — specialising in rural change of use and agritech. Priority: Step 2.
- **Rating Surveyor** — specialising in research/technology properties. Priority: Step 4.
- **Company/Charity Solicitor** — to advise on whether a charitable structure or CIC conversion maximises rate relief and grant eligibility.
- **UKRI / Innovate UK Specialist Consultant** — many organisations employ grant consultants familiar with Je-S and UKRI application requirements. Useful for Step 6, particularly if MEOK LABS lacks prior UKRI application experience.

## Notes on Risk

**Planning enforcement risk** is the primary near-term risk. Operating research activity on agricultural land without planning permission is a live exposure. Step 2 must be treated as a priority, not an optional administrative exercise.

**UKSPA membership does not confer planning permission, tax relief, or UKRI eligibility on its own.** It is a credibility signal that supports all of the above but cannot replace the underlying legal and planning steps.

**Enterprise Zone benefits require geographic designation — this is not within MEOK's unilateral control.** Investment Zone satellite affiliation via a university partner is the more realistic and faster route.
