## 2. The Safety Gap

### The NHS Procurement Problem

NHS trusts procuring robotic systems face a compliance gap that is currently unresolved at a national level. EU AI Act Article 6, read alongside Annex III, places autonomous or semi-autonomous systems operating in healthcare environments — including care robots for elderly patients, surgical assistance systems, and Hospital at Home monitoring robots — in the high-risk AI category. This classification is not discretionary. It carries mandatory requirements: conformity assessment, technical documentation, post-market monitoring, and registration in the EU AI database.

The AI Act applies to systems placed on the UK market. Post-Brexit, the UK government has indicated alignment with the Act's safety principles. Any NHS trust board approving robotic procurement without a conformity pathway is carrying unquantified governance risk.

### The Lab Gap

Existing UK robotic test facilities — including those associated with NHS Innovation hubs and university health institutes — are primarily indoor environments. They test controlled-environment performance: lab floors, flat corridors, static obstacle scenarios. They do not test:

- Outdoor terrain and weather resilience
- AI decision-making under variable lighting and environmental unpredictability
- The behavioural safety properties the EU AI Act actually requires evidence for

More critically, no existing NHS-adjacent facility offers AI safety evaluation aligned to the AISI Inspect framework — the UK government's own standard for AI system evaluation. MEOK AI Labs.org is the only UK certification body operating this standard for applied robotics.

### The Consequence

NHS trusts currently procuring care robots either carry undocumented safety risk or commission multiple separate evaluations — mechanical testing, software audit, AI behavioural review — from different providers, with no integrated compliance report. This is expensive, slow, and produces fragmented evidence that clinical governance committees cannot easily act on.

There is no currently available single-provider pathway from robot procurement to EU AI Act compliance evidence. MEOK LABS fills that gap.

## 3. The MEOK LABS Proposition

### Facility Overview

MEOK LABS is a 6.5-acre private testing facility with a dedicated 1,800 sq ft indoor laboratory, outdoor grassland and variable terrain, and on-site MEOK AI Labs certification capability. The facility was designed for applied AI systems testing — not academic research — and is structured around regulatory evidence generation.

### Three-Phase Testing Protocol

**Phase 1 — Indoor Hospital Ward Simulation (Days 1–3)**

The 1,800 sq ft indoor workshop is configured as a hospital ward mock-up: bed zones, corridor navigation, doorway clearances, patient proximity scenarios, nurse station interaction points, and medical equipment adjacency. Testing covers all relevant spatial zones a care robot would encounter in a real ward environment.

Evaluation includes: obstacle avoidance, patient proximity safety margins, instruction response latency, emergency stop behaviour, and interaction logging for audit trail. All outputs are documented against EU AI Act technical documentation requirements (Article 11).

**Phase 2 — Outdoor Mobility Testing (Days 4–5)**

The farm field adjacent to the indoor facility provides uncontrolled terrain: uneven ground, gradient variations, surface texture changes, weather exposure, and variable ambient lighting. This phase tests the outdoor mobility performance relevant to Hospital at Home robots, grounds maintenance or delivery robots on hospital campuses, and systems that transition between indoor and outdoor environments.

This testing cannot be replicated in any existing NHS test facility. It is a unique capability.

**Phase 3 — MEOK AI Labs Safety Certification (Days 6–7)**

MEOK AI Labs evaluators conduct the AI safety assessment using the AISI Inspect framework: care membrane evaluation (the ethical and behavioural boundaries of the AI system), bias and consistency testing, adversarial scenario review, and full EU AI Act Annex III compliance mapping.

Output: a MEOK AI Labs Certification Report, a completed EU AI Act technical file, and a Post-Market Monitoring template — a full compliance package suitable for submission to clinical governance, procurement panels, and trust boards.

**Total engagement: 7 working days. Fixed fee: £25,000 per robot system.**

The national body responsible for NHS technology adoption strategy, including robotics and AI. The Clinical AI team and the NHSX (now integrated into NHS England) digital transformation function are the correct entry points for a national certification framework conversation.

Fifteen AHSNs operate across NHS England as the regional innovation adoption infrastructure. Each AHSN has an industry partnership function. Targeting the two or three AHSNs in geographic proximity to the MEOK LABS facility is the recommended first engagement step for regional pilot recruitment.

The procurement authority for medical devices and technology at scale. A framework listing requiring MEOK LABS certification as a compliance condition would create mandatory demand across all participating trusts.

## 6. Pilot Proposal

### Six-Month Pilot with One NHS Trust

**Objective:** Demonstrate end-to-end certification of two robot systems against EU AI Act requirements, generate a replicable evidence package, and publish a joint case study for use in national procurement conversations.

**Month 1 — Scoping and Configuration**
Joint working session with the trust's innovation director, procurement lead, and clinical governance representative. Define the robot systems to be certified. Configure the indoor ward simulation to match the trust's actual ward layout and patient environment specifications. Agree the audit trail format required by the trust's clinical governance committee.

**Months 2–3 — Certification Cohort 1**
Full three-phase testing and MEOK AI Labs certification of the first robot system. Deliver completed technical documentation package. Trust team observes testing days in person — this is an explicit feature of the pilot, enabling the trust to build internal capability for future procurement evaluation.

**Months 4–5 — Certification Cohort 2**
Repeat protocol for the second system. Incorporate any lessons from Cohort 1 into the configuration. Begin drafting the joint case study.

**Month 6 — Evaluation and Publication**
Joint evaluation session. Case study finalised and approved by both parties. Presentation delivered at one AHSN innovation event. Pilot report submitted to NHS England Innovation as evidence for national programme consideration.

**Pilot fee: £15,000 (covering both systems). Standard rate applies from the third certification onward.**

The pilot is designed to produce a replicable, trust-owned procurement process — not a dependency on MEOK LABS. That is the appropriate model for public sector partnership.
