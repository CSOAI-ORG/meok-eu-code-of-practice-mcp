### 2.2 Valuation Methodology

Software asset valuation uses three primary approaches [^487^][^488^]:

**1. Cost Approach (Replacement Cost)**
- Estimates cost to reproduce software from scratch
- COCOMO II model: effort based on program size in lines of code [^487^]
- Includes developer costs, QA (30-40% of dev), PM overhead (15-25%)
- Obsolescence adjustments for economic and functional factors
- Typical range: $50-300 per line depending on complexity

**2. Market Approach**
- Compares to similar software packages/transactions
- Limited comparables for unique/internal software
- Public company data: API management trades at 1.6x-4.7x revenue [^517^]
- Private deals average ~1.6x EV/Revenue, median ~0.9x [^517^]

**3. Income Approach**
- Values software by present value of future cash flows
- For SaaS/platforms, most appropriate when revenue attribution is direct
- AI SaaS companies command 18-30x revenue multiples [^515^]

U.S. investment in open source software was estimated at $36.2 billion in 2019 [^495^], demonstrating the massive economic value of code assets.

### 5.2 Valuation Methodology

**Brand Valuation Methods (ISO 10668 Standard):**

**1. Relief-from-Royalty (RFR)** - Most widely accepted [^548^]
- Values brand based on hypothetical royalty payments avoided by owning it
- Technology/software typical royalty rate: 2-5% [^548^]
- Formula: Branded Revenue x Royalty Rate x Discount Factor

**2. Income Premium Method**
- Compares branded vs unbranded business earnings
- Premium through higher prices, greater volume, or both

**3. Cost-Based Method**
- Cost to recreate brand from scratch
- Marketing spend, campaign costs, trademark fees
