## PERSONA 1 — THE SOLO INVENTOR

**Profile:** Independent hardware/physical-product maker, 1–3 people,
pre-filing or provisional-stage, often self-funded. Files 1–4
disclosures per year. Spends nights in the workshop, not in patent
seminars.

**Wound (pain points):**
- Has had an idea "borrowed" before — seen it on Kickstarter or Alibaba
  within 90 days of disclosure
- Cannot afford a $3,000 provisional to "test" an idea's defensibility
- Doesn't trust cloud-only timestamps (Dropbox, Google Drive modified
  dates are easily contested)
- Has been told "just file a provisional" by people who have never
  tried to budget one

**What they need to hear:**
You are not asking them to believe in a blockchain. You are asking them
to spend 9 minutes and $10 to put a sovereign witness between their
idea and the world. The Bitcoin OP_RETURN anchor is the receipt that
holds up when someone else's lawyer calls.

**Ideal CTA:**
*"Run the Free Spark tier (tier 0) on your next idea. Then run OTS
Self-Serve ($10) on the one you actually care about. 9 minutes,
1 PDF, court-admissible."*

**Objections (and how to handle them):**
- **"I have an attorney for this."** → Good. OTS is the pre-attorney
  witness. The PDF walks into the attorney's office already timestamped.
- **"My idea isn't ready for a disclosure."** → The 6 layers capture
  rough drawings. That is the point. The chain records the rough
  drawing, not the finished patent.
- **"What if Bitcoin goes down?"** → Bitcoin going down is a
  macroeconomic event. The OP_RETURN receipt is in the block. The
  block is replicated on 18,000+ nodes. The chain is not a SaaS.

**Right tier to pitch FIRST:** **Tier 0 — Free Spark** (zero-friction
entry). Then upgrade to **Tier 1 — OTS Self-Serve ($10)** once they
feel the 6-layer flow.

**Persona-tone for the sequence:** Quiet, craft-oriented, evidence-first.
No enterprise jargon. No "synergy." Speak like a fellow workshop builder.

## PERSONA 2 — THE INDIE GAME STUDIO

**Profile:** 2–12 person team, ships 1–4 titles per year, mechanic-heavy
and IP-rich. Often the studio is the IP. Founder is technical, the
non-technical co-founder handles "business stuff" (which includes
disclosures, sadly).

**Wound (pain points):**
- Builds are stolen/cloned within days of a Steam demo or TikTok
  preview
- Publisher contracts demand IP chain-of-custody evidence they
  cannot currently produce
- Multiple parallel builds (engine, art, narrative) each need
  their own witness
- Game-jam-style rapid iteration means a single disclosure per
  project is laughable — they need monthly or weekly

**What they need to hear:**
The Atelier tier is the indie studio's drafting table. Weekly anchored
disclosures. Sprint-build chain anchors. The same engine a defense
prime uses, priced for a 4-person team that ships.

**Ideal CTA:**
*"Spin up the Atelier tier ($99/mo) for one sprint cycle. Anchor every
weekly build with a 6-layer disclosure. When the publisher asks for
chain-of-custody, you have it on a 4-minute SLA."*

**Objections (and how to handle them):**
- **"We use Git for this."** → Git records commits. It does not record
  a tamper-evident, court-admissible 6-layer disclosure anchored to
  Bitcoin. The two systems are complementary, not competing.
- **"We can't afford another subscription."** → Atelier ($99/mo) is
  less than one hour of a junior contractor. One stolen build costs
  more than 80 years of the tier.
- **"We're too small to be a target."** → Most theft happens at
  the 3–8 person stage. Bigger studios have legal departments.
  You're in the danger zone.

**Right tier to pitch FIRST:** **Tier 2 — Atelier ($99/mo)**.
This is their tier. The whole product is built for them.

**Persona-tone for the sequence:** Builder-to-builder. Reference
actual game-dev workflows (sprint cycles, demo drops, publisher
pitches). Avoid legalese. Use "build" and "anchor" more than
"disclose" and "file."

## PERSONA 3 — THE IP BOUTIQUE

**Profile:** 5–40-attorney firm, 50–500 active matters, trade-secret
and patent litigation practice. The Managing Partner is the
decision-maker; the paralegal team is the daily user.

**Wound (pain points):**
- Legacy docketing systems (CPI, Anaqua) cost $40,000+/yr and
  don't anchor to a sovereign ledger
- Discovery fights increasingly turn on timestamp authenticity —
  opposing counsel challenges Dropbox/email/SharePoint dates
- Clients (the boutique's actual customers) are demanding
  blockchain-grade evidence
- Partner-track associates burn 6–10 hours per matter on
  chain-of-custody documentation

**What they need to hear:**
The Sovereign tier is the docketing system that finally earns its
own name. PACER integration. USPTO PAIR integration. WIPO integration.
The 6-layer disclosure as a first-class docket entry, anchored to
Bitcoin OP_RETURN, auditable in 4 minutes, not 18 days.

**Ideal CTA:**
*"Let's put the Sovereign tier ($999/mo) against one of your active
matters for 30 days. Measure: chain-of-custody prep hours, timestamp
disputes, motion outcomes. If it doesn't pay for itself in the pilot,
I'll write you a check."*

**Objections (and how to handle them):**
- **"We have CPI/Anaqua already."** → Great. OTS plugs into both.
  We're the chain anchor and the disclosure PDF; they're the
  docket. The Sovereign tier has the connectors.
- **"The bar association will have opinions."** → The bar association
  has opinions about fax machines too. The OP_RETURN anchor is
  fact, not opinion. Show them the Bitcoin block explorer.
- **"Our clients won't pay for this."** → Your clients are already
  paying for it — buried in $250,000 defense bills. The Sovereign
  tier is the line item that ends those bills.

**Right tier to pitch FIRST:** **Tier 3 — Sovereign ($999/mo)**.
Direct pilot offer. No lead-gen nurture. Boutiques want a
business case, not a sequence.

**Persona-tone for the sequence:** Litigator-precise. Cite federal
rules. Reference specific circuits. Avoid "Web3," "crypto," or any
other word that has been diluted by 2017 ICO marketing.

## PERSONA 4 — THE GOV / DEFENSE BUYER

**Profile:** Program office, prime contractor IP team, or tech-transfer
office at a federal lab or major defense integrator. Procurement is
notoriously slow. Compliance is the lingua franca. Authority to buy
lives 4 levels up from the person you email first.

**Wound (pain points):**
- DFARS, ITAR, and EAR compliance requires demonstrable chain-of-custody
  for any controlled technology
- Adversary nation-states target the disclosure-to-filing window
  specifically — that's the time the IP is exposed and undefended
- Legacy gov IP systems (DoD SAFE, Navy Tech Transfer, etc.) don't
  anchor to a public, sovereign ledger
- Insider threat programs need an auditable trail of who knew what
  when — and when they signed the disclosure

**What they need to hear:**
The Founder's Mark tier is built for this. Lifetime chain anchoring.
FIPS-compliant key management. FedRAMP-aligned deployment. The
6-layer disclosure as a first-class artifact in any tech-transfer
or SBIR/STTR pipeline. Bitcoin OP_RETURN as the public witness
even the most restrictive classification regime can verify.

**Ideal CTA:**
*"Schedule a 30-minute classified-or-unclassified session with our
gov lead. We'll walk the Founder's Mark tier ($9,999/yr) against
your tech-transfer workflow, including the SBIR data-rights
implications."*

**Objections (and how to handle them):**
- **"We can't use public blockchains."** → OP_RETURN anchors are
  public-by-default but the 6-layer disclosure bundle (the actual
  content) is encrypted at rest with FIPS-140-3 keys. The chain
  holds the *receipt*, not the idea.
- **"Procurement cycles are 18 months."** → The Founder's Mark tier
  ships against a 12-month delivery; we have GWACs and a sole-source
  justification drafted. Or, more often, the program office pays
  out of ops budget and the contract shows up 18 months later.
- **"We have our own timestamping."** → Show me the verifier URL.
  The OP_RETURN anchor has a public verifier; internal systems
  don't. We'll happily integrate.

**Right tier to pitch FIRST:** **Tier 4 — The Founder's Mark ($9,999/yr)**.
No lead-nurture hand-holding. Gov/defense buyers want specs, the
SOC 2 / FIPS posture, and a contracting path. Give them all three
on the first call.

**Persona-tone for the sequence:** Spec-sheet-grade. FedRAMP, FIPS,
DFARS, ITAR — name them correctly. No marketing language. A
program office that smells "synergy" unsubscribes in 7 seconds.

## PERSONA 5 — THE AI STARTUP

**Profile:** Seed-to-Series-B AI lab or applied-AI company. 3–30
people. Ship models, weights, datasets, evals, and tools. The
founders are ex-researchers. The IP is the model weights and the
training pipeline.

**Wound (pain points):**
- Model weights and fine-tunes are the new trade secret — but the
  legal system still treats them as "data"
- Open-weights releases, employee turnover, and lab spinouts all
  create a 30-day window where IP walks out the door
- The first instinct is "we'll just use git + Notion" — and it
  fails within one quarter
- Investors now ask for IP chain-of-custody in Series A diligence;
  the answer is usually "uh"

**What they need to hear:**
Your model weights, your eval suites, your fine-tunes — these are
all disclosable assets. The 6-layer disclosure captures them
(title = model name, description = architecture summary, drawings
= architecture diagram, prototype = commit hash, contributor hash
= author/committer registry, chain receipt = Bitcoin OP_RETURN).
The Sovereign tier is the AI lab's drafting table.

**Ideal CTA:**
*"Anchor your next major model release with a 6-layer OTS
disclosure — title, architecture summary, eval results, weights
SHA, contributor registry, Bitcoin OP_RETURN. Send the PDF to
your investors. End the IP diligence question in one document."*

**Objections (and how to handle them):**
- **"We open-source everything."** → Great. Anchor the open-source
  release. The chain timestamp is the receipt that *you* released
  it first, before the fork, before the corporate breakaway.
- **"Our weights are the IP — we'd never disclose."** → The 6 layers
  let you disclose the *existence, architecture summary, and
  contributor registry* of the model without disclosing the weights
  themselves. The chain holds the proof, not the artifact.
- **"We're pre-Series A and can't afford it."** → The Atelier tier
  ($99/mo) is built for you. Move up to Sovereign at Series A.
  The chain follows the cap table.

**Right tier to pitch FIRST:** **Tier 2 — Atelier ($99/mo)**
(pre-Series A) or **Tier 3 — Sovereign ($999/mo)** (post-Series A).
Match the tier to the round. Investors love seeing a tiered IP
strategy that scales with the cap table.

**Persona-tone for the sequence:** Researcher-to-researcher. Use
"weights," "fine-tunes," "evals," "model card" — the right words.
Skip "AI-powered" and "next-generation." Anyone who has shipped a
model laughs at those words.

## HOW TO USE THIS MATRIX

1. **In `scripts/send-outreach.py`**, the `--persona` flag must be one
   of: `solo_inventor`, `indie_studio`, `ip_boutique`, `gov_defense`,
   `ai_startup`. Unknown personas default to the **solo_inventor**
   sequence (the lowest-friction path).
2. **Each persona gets a tier-pitch inserted into segments 2, 6, and 9**
   of the OUTREACH-SEQUENCE.md. The insertion is templated; the rest
   of the body is shared across all personas.
3. **The persona tone is enforced by the segment copywriter**
   (parallel_executor prompts in `vault/tasks/`) — not by string
   templating alone. The chain is the message, but the message is
   the messenger.
