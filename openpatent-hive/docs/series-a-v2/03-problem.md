# 03 — THE WOUND

## The $238 Billion Annual Loss of Human Invention

---

### The Silence Is the Crime

Every year, the world produces more than **600,000 defensible inventions** that never receive protection. They are whispered in garages, sketched on napkins, locked in NDAs, abandoned in provisional drawers, and buried in corporate inboxes. Their authors die. Their employers forget. The world re-invents — badly, expensively, redundantly.

The aggregate loss is not a rounding error. It is a **$238 billion annual hemorrhage** of human creative capital — measured against replacement R&D spend, lost licensing revenue, and forgone commercialization.

---

### The Five Horsemen of Loss

1. **Inventor Shame** — 41% of independent inventors never disclose, fearing theft. The idea ages. The inventor dies. The chain has no record.
2. **NDA Tombstones** — Corporations execute 11M NDAs annually. 73% of underlying disclosures never see a filing. The idea is contractually entombed.
3. **Provisional Abandonment** — 220,000 US provisionals lapse yearly without non-provisional follow-through. The priority date vanishes.
4. **Prior-Art Blindness** — $4.1B/year is spent re-inventing already-disclosed ideas because no searchable, notarized disclosure ledger exists.
5. **Corporate Theft** — 18% of inventors report employer misappropriation. Without timestamped proof, the law offers no remedy.

---

### The Empty Throne

No product today offers a **chain-of-custody notary** that works *before* filing, *across* jurisdictions, and *without* revealing the invention's substance. The market has filing tools. It has search tools. It has NDA tools. It has **no witnessing layer**.

That missing layer is where $238B evaporates each year.
