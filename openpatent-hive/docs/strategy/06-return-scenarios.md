## 2. Scenario A — **Conservative** (£2M Y3 portfolio NAV)

**Assumptions:**

- Only **4 of 28** domains are live by Y3 (openpatent.ai, ipcastle.ai, harvi.ai, datamoat.ai).
- The other 24 domains are **reserved but not earning**.
- The .ai TLD re-rates at **2× from today** (no sovereign-substrate premium, just TLD beta).
- No PatentMCP network effect (>1,000 integrations).
- No BFT monopoly (the 12 queens are advisory, not regulatory).
- **MRR at Y3:** £15k (openpatent.ai only)
- **ARR at Y3:** £180k

**Valuation math (Y3):**

- **Portfolio NAV:** £179,800 (acquisition) × 2× TLD beta × 1.0× power-pack premium = **£360k–£2M** (the 4 live assets carry the premium; the 24 reserved are at bare TLD beta).
- **SaaS valuation:** £180k ARR × 5× (early-stage SaaS) = **£900k**
- **IP Castle valuation:** £35M (5 patents + 5 trademarks + 5 grants) — but discounted to **£1.5M** in the conservative case (no enforcement, no PatentMCP).
- **Subtotal:** £360k (domains) + £900k (SaaS) + £1.5M (IP) = **£2.76M**
- **Conservative NAV (with 30% liquidity discount):** **~£2M**

**Investor return (Seed → Y3):**

- Seed investment: £500k @ £4M pre-money = **12.5% ownership** (on a fully-diluted basis)
- Conservative Y3 NAV: £2M
- **Investor share: £250k** (12.5% of £2M)
- **Multiple:** 0.5× (down round) — the conservative case is a **downside-protection scenario, not a return scenario**.

**This is the floor.** If only 4 of 28 domains are live, the portfolio is still worth £2M because the .ai TLD beta alone carries the assets.
