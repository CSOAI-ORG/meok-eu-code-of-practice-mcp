### **0.9 FORCE MAJEURE**

**Definition:**
"Force Majeure" means events beyond reasonable control, including:
- Acts of God (earthquakes, floods, pandemics, etc.)
- War, terrorism, civil unrest
- Government actions (expropriation, embargo, etc.)
- Cyberattacks on critical infrastructure
- Catastrophic AI failures (if widespread, not isolated)

**Effect:**
- Parties excused from performance during Force Majeure
- Must notify other party within 7 days
- Must make reasonable efforts to resume performance
- If Force Majeure continues >6 months, either party may terminate

**Prosperity Fund Distribution:**
- Force Majeure does NOT excuse Prosperity Fund contributions
- Contributions may be deferred (max 12 months) with approval
- Accrued interest on deferred amounts

### **0.10 DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

**Mandatory Process (Must Follow in Order):**

**Step 1: Negotiation (30 days)**
- Parties attempt to resolve informally
- Good faith discussions
- Escalate to senior management if needed

**Step 2: Mediation (60 days)**
- If negotiation fails, proceed to mediation
- Mediator: Mutually agreed, or appointed by CEDR (Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution)
- Location: London, UK (or remote)
- Cost: Split equally
- Non-binding

**Step 3: Arbitration (Binding)**
- If mediation fails, proceed to arbitration
- Rules: ICC Arbitration Rules (International Chamber of Commerce)
- Number of arbitrators: 1 (for disputes <£100K), 3 (for disputes ≥£100K)
- Location: London, UK
- Language: English
- Award is final and binding
- Enforceable in any jurisdiction under New York Convention

**Exceptions (Can Go Straight to Court):**
- Injunctive relief (emergency situations)
- Intellectual property disputes (trademark infringement)
- Fraud or criminal conduct
- Small claims (<£10K in UK Small Claims Court)

**Class Action Waiver:**
- No class actions or consolidated arbitrations
- Each dispute resolved individually
- Exception: Regulators or consumer protection agencies may bring group actions
