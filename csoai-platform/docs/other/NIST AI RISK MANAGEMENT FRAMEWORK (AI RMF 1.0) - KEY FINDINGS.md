# NIST AI RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK (AI RMF 1.0) - KEY FINDINGS

## DOCUMENT OVERVIEW
- **Published:** January 2023
- **Document:** NIST AI 100-1
- **Purpose:** Voluntary framework to manage AI risks and improve trustworthiness
- **Total Pages:** 48
- **Status:** Living document (next review: 2028)

## CORE STRUCTURE: 4 FUNCTIONS

### 1. GOVERN
- Cross-cutting function that informs all other functions
- Establishes organizational AI governance structures
- Categories and subcategories (see Table 1 in document)

### 2. MAP
- Context establishment and risk identification
- Maps AI system characteristics to organizational context
- Categories and subcategories (see Table 2 in document)

### 3. MEASURE
- Analyzes, assesses, benchmarks, and monitors AI risks
- Quantifies and qualifies AI system trustworthiness
- Categories and subcategories (see Table 3 in document)

### 4. MANAGE
- Allocates resources to mapped and measured risks
- Responds to and recovers from incidents
- Categories and subcategories (see Table 4 in document)

## 7 TRUSTWORTHY AI CHARACTERISTICS

1. **Valid & Reliable** - Necessary foundation for all other characteristics
2. **Safe** - Does not cause unacceptable harm
3. **Secure & Resilient** - Protected against threats and can recover
4. **Accountable & Transparent** - Traceable and explainable
5. **Explainable & Interpretable** - Understandable to stakeholders
6. **Privacy-Enhanced** - Protects personal data
7. **Fair – with Harmful Bias Managed** - Equitable outcomes

## KEY CHALLENGES FOR AI RISK MANAGEMENT

### 1. Risk Measurement
- Difficulty quantifying AI risks
- Emergent properties hard to predict
- Context-dependent impacts

### 2. Risk Tolerance
- Varies by organization and use case
- Cultural and societal differences
- Regulatory requirements

### 3. Risk Prioritization
- Multiple competing risks
- Trade-offs between characteristics
- Resource allocation

### 4. Organizational Integration
- Cross-functional coordination needed
- Alignment with existing risk management
- Continuous monitoring required

## HOW AI RISKS DIFFER FROM TRADITIONAL SOFTWARE RISKS (Appendix B)

1. **Opacity** - Less interpretable than traditional software
2. **Data Dependency** - Quality and bias in training data
3. **Emergent Behavior** - Unexpected outputs
4. **Contextual Performance** - Works differently in different contexts
5. **Continuous Learning** - Systems that adapt over time

## HUMAN-AI INTERACTION (Appendix C)

- Emphasizes human oversight and control
- Addresses automation bias
- Considers cognitive load on human operators
- Highlights need for meaningful human review

## ATTRIBUTES OF THE AI RMF (Appendix D)

- **Voluntary** - Not regulatory
- **Risk-based** - Focuses on managing risks
- **Flexible** - Adaptable to different contexts
- **Technology-neutral** - Applies to all AI systems
- **Internationally aligned** - Compatible with global standards

## INTEGRATION WITH OTHER FRAMEWORKS

The AI RMF is designed to:
- Build on existing risk management practices
- Align with international standards (ISO, IEEE, etc.)
- Support compliance with regulations (EU AI Act, etc.)
- Enable crosswalks to other frameworks

## IMPLEMENTATION APPROACH

1. Start with GOVERN function (establish governance)
2. MAP the AI system and context
3. MEASURE risks and trustworthiness
4. MANAGE identified risks
5. Iterate continuously throughout AI lifecycle

## PROFILES

- Organizations can create AI RMF Profiles
- Profiles specify which categories/subcategories apply
- Enables customization for specific use cases
- Supports sector-specific implementations

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR COAI FRAMEWORK

1. **Governance-First Approach** - COAI should start with governance layer
2. **4-Function Structure** - Adopt Govern-Map-Measure-Manage model
3. **7 Trustworthiness Characteristics** - Build these into COAI architecture
4. **Lifecycle Integration** - COAI must work across entire AI lifecycle
5. **Multi-Actor Framework** - COAI serves multiple stakeholders
6. **Continuous Monitoring** - COAI needs real-time risk assessment
7. **Flexibility** - COAI must adapt to different contexts and regulations
