## 1. DOCUMENT STRUCTURE

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) consists of:

- **Preamble:** 180 recitals explaining the rationale
- **Main Text:** 113 articles organized into 13 titles
- **13 Annexes:** Technical specifications and lists

### Complete Structure:

**TITLE I: GENERAL PROVISIONS (Articles 1-2)**
- Article 1: Subject matter and scope
- Article 2: Scope

**TITLE II: PROHIBITED AI PRACTICES (Article 5)**
- Article 5: Prohibited artificial intelligence practices

**TITLE III: HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS (Articles 6-51)**
- Chapter 1: Classification (Articles 6-7)
- Chapter 2: Requirements (Articles 8-15)
- Chapter 3: Obligations (Articles 16-27)
- Chapter 4: Notifying authorities and notified bodies (Articles 28-39)
- Chapter 5: Standards, conformity assessment, certificates, registration (Articles 40-51)

**TITLE IV: TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS (Articles 50-54)**
- Article 50: Transparency obligations for certain AI systems
- Articles 51-54: Additional transparency requirements

**TITLE V: GENERAL-PURPOSE AI MODELS (Articles 51-56)**
- Article 51: Classification of general-purpose AI models
- Articles 52-56: Obligations for providers

**TITLE VI: MEASURES IN SUPPORT OF INNOVATION (Articles 57-62)**
- AI regulatory sandboxes
- Testing in real-world conditions

**TITLE VII: GOVERNANCE (Articles 63-77)**
- European AI Office
- AI Board
- National competent authorities

**TITLE VIII: EU DATABASE FOR HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS (Article 78)**

**TITLE IX: POST-MARKET MONITORING, INFORMATION SHARING, MARKET SURVEILLANCE (Articles 79-89)**

**TITLE X: CODES OF CONDUCT AND GUIDELINES (Articles 90-96)**

**TITLE XI: DELEGATION OF POWER AND COMMITTEE PROCEDURE (Articles 97-98)**

**TITLE XII: PENALTIES (Articles 99-102)**

**TITLE XIII: FINAL PROVISIONS (Articles 103-113)**

## 2. CRITICAL ARTICLES FOR COAI IMPLEMENTATION

### 2.1 Article 5: Prohibited AI Practices

**French Text:**
> "Les pratiques suivantes en matière d'IA sont interdites..."

**Key Prohibitions:**
1. **Subliminal manipulation** (Article 5.1.a)
2. **Exploitation of vulnerabilities** (Article 5.1.b)
3. **Social scoring by public authorities** (Article 5.1.c)
4. **Real-time remote biometric identification** in publicly accessible spaces (Article 5.1.h) - with exceptions

**COAI Implementation:**
- The 33-agent council must be able to detect these prohibited practices
- "The Watchdog" feature must flag any AI system engaging in these practices
- Automated daily testing must check for subliminal manipulation techniques

### 2.2 Article 6: Classification Rules for High-Risk AI Systems

**French Text:**
> "Un système d'IA est considéré comme présentant un risque élevé..."

**High-Risk Categories (Annex III):**
1. Biometric identification and categorization
2. Management and operation of critical infrastructure
3. Education and vocational training
4. Employment, workers management, access to self-employment
5. Access to and enjoyment of essential private and public services
6. Law enforcement
7. Migration, asylum and border control management
8. Administration of justice and democratic processes

**COAI Implementation:**
- Automated risk classification based on Annex III
- The 33-agent council votes on borderline cases
- Real-time classification updates as regulations evolve

### 2.3 Articles 9-15: Technical Requirements for High-Risk Systems

**Article 9: Risk Management System**
- Continuous, iterative process
- Throughout entire lifecycle
- Regular systematic updates

**Article 10: Data and Data Governance**
- Training, validation, testing datasets
- Relevant, representative, error-free
- Appropriate statistical properties

**Article 11: Technical Documentation**
- Detailed system description
- Development process
- Monitoring and control measures

**Article 12: Record-Keeping**
- Automatic logging of events
- Traceability throughout lifecycle

**Article 13: Transparency and Information**
- Clear instructions for use
- Human oversight capabilities
- Expected level of accuracy

**Article 14: Human Oversight**
- Humans can override AI decisions
- Ability to interrupt system operation
- Understand system capabilities/limitations

**Article 15: Accuracy, Robustness, Cybersecurity**
- Appropriate level of accuracy
- Resilient to errors and faults
- Protected against cyber threats

**COAI Implementation:**
- Each requirement becomes a module in COAI
- The 33-agent council evaluates compliance with each article
- Automated documentation generation for Article 11
- Automated logging system for Article 12

### 2.4 Article 52: Transparency Obligations for General-Purpose AI

**Key Requirements:**
- Providers must draw up technical documentation
- Comply with EU copyright law
- Publish detailed summary of training data

**COAI Implementation:**
- "The Watchdog" can audit general-purpose AI models
- Automated daily testing of GPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.
- Public database of model compliance

### 2.5 Article 53: Systemic Risk Models

**Definition:** General-purpose AI models with high impact capabilities

**Additional Obligations:**
- Model evaluation
- Adversarial testing
- Tracking and reporting serious incidents
- Ensuring adequate level of cybersecurity

**COAI Implementation:**
- AGISafe.ai module handles systemic risk assessment
- The 33-agent council evaluates systemic risk level
- Automated incident reporting system

## 4. PENALTIES (Article 99)

**Maximum Fines:**
- **€35M or 7% of global turnover** (whichever is higher) for:
  - Prohibited AI practices (Article 5)
  - Non-compliance with general-purpose AI obligations

- **€15M or 3% of global turnover** for:
  - High-risk AI system violations
  - Notified body violations

- **€7.5M or 1.5% of global turnover** for:
  - Transparency obligation violations
  - Providing incorrect information

**COAI VALUE PROPOSITION:**
- Avoiding a single €35M fine justifies a €500K COAI investment
- Risk mitigation is the primary driver for enterprise sales

## 5. GAPS & LOOPHOLES IDENTIFIED

### 5.1 Gap: Real-Time Monitoring

**Issue:** The EU AI Act requires "post-market monitoring" (Article 72) but doesn't specify HOW to implement it.

**COAI Solution:** 
- Built-in continuous monitoring dashboard
- Real-time alerts for drift, bias, or anomalies
- Automated incident reporting

### 5.2 Gap: Conformity Assessment Process

**Issue:** Article 43 requires conformity assessment but the process is complex and unclear for most companies.

**COAI Solution:**
- Step-by-step conformity assessment wizard
- Automated generation of required documentation
- Pre-assessment to identify gaps before official assessment

### 5.3 Gap: Cross-Border Compliance

**Issue:** Companies operating in multiple jurisdictions need to comply with EU AI Act + NIST + UK + Canada + Australia.

**COAI Solution:**
- Unified compliance dashboard for all jurisdictions
- Single source of truth for multi-jurisdiction compliance
- Automated mapping between different frameworks

### 5.4 Loophole: "AI System" Definition

**Issue:** Article 3(1) defines "AI system" broadly, but there's ambiguity around simple rule-based systems.

**COAI Solution:**
- The 33-agent council can evaluate borderline cases
- Provides defensible classification with justification
- Reduces risk of misclassification

### 5.5 Gap: General-Purpose AI Model Auditing

**Issue:** Article 52 requires providers to publish training data summaries, but there's no independent verification mechanism.

**COAI Solution:**
- "The Watchdog" feature provides independent auditing
- Crowdsourced problem reporting
- Automated daily testing of all major models
