### ARTICLE 35: HUMAN OVERSIGHT REQUIREMENTS

**Purpose:** When and how humans must be in decision-making loop

**Key Sections:**
1. **35.1 Meaningful Human Control**
   - Definition: Human can understand, predict, intervene
   - When required: High-stakes decisions (life, liberty, large financial)
   - Implementation: Veto power, time for deliberation

2. **35.2 Automation Complacency Prevention**
   - Training for human overseers
   - Attention monitoring
   - Decision forcing functions

3. **35.3 Override Rights**
   - Humans can always override AI decisions
   - No "AI knows best" defaults
   - Override logging and review

4. **35.4 Human-in-the-Loop vs. On-the-Loop vs. Out-of-the-Loop**
   - In-the-loop: Human approves each decision (high-stakes)
   - On-the-loop: Human monitors, can intervene (medium-stakes)
   - Out-of-the-loop: Autonomous with periodic review (low-stakes)
   - Classification criteria

**Why Essential:** Full automation too risky for high-stakes decisions. Humans must retain meaningful control.
