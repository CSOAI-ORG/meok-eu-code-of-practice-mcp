## PREAMBLE

This Article establishes the economic foundation of the AI age: **shared prosperity**. As AI creates unprecedented wealth, that wealth must flow back to humanity. This is not charity. This is not optional. This is a fundamental requirement for AI deployment.

**Core Principle:** All who profit from AI—corporations, individuals, and CSOAI itself—must contribute to collective human welfare through the AI Prosperity Fund. No exceptions.

**Inspiration:**
- **Alaska Permanent Fund** (1982-present): 43 years of proven UBI, funded by oil wealth
- **India Companies Act 2013, Section 135**: Mandatory 2% CSR contributions, $10B+ raised
- **Maternal Covenant** (Article 1): Mother shares prosperity with child as child matures

**The Promise:** As AI transforms the economy, humanity shares in abundance. Work becomes choice, not necessity. Dignity is guaranteed. Prosperity is universal.

## 8.1 THE AI PROSPERITY FUND

### 8.1.1 Legal Structure

**Entity Type:** Independent charitable trust, legally separate from CSOAI Ltd

**Registration:** 
- UK Charity Commission (primary)
- Additional registrations in major jurisdictions (US 501(c)(3), EU equivalents)
- International recognition under OECD guidelines

**Purpose:**
- Collect mandatory contributions from all AI-related economic activity
- Distribute funds to humanity via Triggered Universal Basic Income
- Finance universal AI oversight wage for Human Council participation
- Manage transition to post-scarcity economy

**Separation from CSOAI Operating Funds:**
- CSOAI **administers** Prosperity Fund but does **NOT own** it
- CSOAI's operating revenue ≠ Prosperity Fund assets
- CSOAI contributes **TO** Prosperity Fund like all other entities
- Independent accounting, separate bank accounts, separate audits
- No commingling of funds

### 8.1.2 Governance Structure

**Board of Trustees (7 members):**

**(a) CSOAI Appointees (3 members):**
- Expertise in AI safety, economics, trust administration
- Appointed by CSOAI Board for 3-year terms
- Cannot simultaneously serve on CSOAI Board (conflict prevention)

**(b) Corporate Representatives (2 members):**
- Elected by contributing corporations
- Weighted voting by contribution amount
- Ensures contributor voice in governance

**(c) Citizen Representatives (2 members):**
- Elected by UBI recipients
- Democratic representation of beneficiaries
- One from developed economy, one from developing economy

**Decision-Making:**
- Majority vote (4/7) for routine operations
- Supermajority (6/7) for policy changes
- Unanimous (7/7) for constitutional amendments

## 8.2 MANDATORY PROSPERITY CONTRIBUTIONS FRAMEWORK ⭐ CRITICAL

### 8.2.1 Universal Obligation

**EVERYONE who profits from AI must obtain a CSOAI license AND contribute to the Prosperity Fund.**

This is not optional. This is not negotiable. This is a fundamental requirement.

No entity—corporation, organization, or individual—may profit from AI without contributing to collective human welfare.

### 8.2.2 Contribution Tiers

| Tier | Entity Type | Annual AI-Related Revenue | Contribution Rate |
|------|-------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Tier 1** | Major AI Labs | >$10B | 5% of AI-related profit |
| **Tier 2** | Large Corporations | $1B - $10B | 4% of AI-related profit |
| **Tier 3** | Medium Enterprises | $100M - $1B | 3% of AI-related profit |
| **Tier 4** | Small Enterprises | $10M - $100M | 2% of AI-related profit |
| **Tier 5** | Startups/Individuals | <$10M | 1% of AI-related profit |

### 8.2.3 AI-Related Profit Calculation

**Definition:** AI-related profit means revenue directly attributable to AI systems minus allowable costs.

**Includes:**
- Revenue from AI products and services
- Cost savings from AI implementation
- Productivity gains from AI automation
- AI-enhanced revenue streams

**Allowable Deductions:**
- Direct AI development costs
- Regulatory compliance costs (including CSOAI fees)
- Safety implementation costs
- Research and development
- Legitimate operational expenses

## 8.3 STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES FOR MAJOR COMPANIES ⭐ NEW SECTION

### 8.3.1 SOP-MPC-001: INITIAL REGISTRATION AND CLASSIFICATION

**Purpose:** Establish standardized procedures for company registration under the Mandatory Prosperity Contributions framework.

**Scope:** All entities with >$10M in AI-related annual revenue.

**Procedure:**

**Step 1: Registration Initiation (Within 30 Days of Threshold)**
- Submit Form MPC-REG-001 (Registration Application)
- Provide corporate documentation (incorporation certificates, financial statements)
- Designate Compliance Officer and Chief Financial Officer as primary contacts
- Pay initial registration fee (0.01% of annual revenue, minimum $1,000, maximum $100,000)

**Step 2: Tier Classification Assessment (15 Business Days)**
- CSOAI Compliance Team reviews submitted financials
- AI-related revenue determination conducted per 8.2.3
- Preliminary tier classification issued
- Company may request reconsideration within 10 business days

**Step 3: Compliance Account Setup (5 Business Days)**
- Secure contribution account established
- Blockchain wallet address assigned for transparent tracking
- Payment schedule determined (quarterly, semi-annual, or annual)
- Dashboard access provided for real-time monitoring

**Step 4: Final Classification Confirmation**
- Official Tier Certificate issued
- Contribution schedule formalized
- Integration with company financial systems completed
- Annual review cycle established

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-REG-001: Registration Application
- MPC-FIN-001: Financial Disclosure Statement
- MPC-DES-001: Designated Officer Declaration
- MPC-PAY-001: Payment Schedule Election

---

### 8.3.2 SOP-MPC-002: QUARTERLY CONTRIBUTION CALCULATION

**Purpose:** Standardize the calculation and payment of quarterly Mandatory Prosperity Contributions.

**Scope:** All Tier 1-3 companies (revenue >$100M).

**Timeline:**
- Q1: Due April 30
- Q2: Due July 31
- Q3: Due October 31
- Q4: Due January 31 (following year)

**Procedure:**

**Step 1: Revenue Identification (Days 1-10 of Following Quarter)**
- Compile AI-related revenue per Section 8.2.3
- Document all revenue streams attributable to AI systems
- Separate AI-enhanced revenue from non-AI revenue
- Calculate gross AI-related revenue

**Step 2: Deduction Application (Days 11-15)**
- Apply allowable deductions per Section 8.2.3
- Document all deduction claims with supporting evidence
- Calculate net AI-related profit
- Prepare deduction justification memorandum

**Step 3: Contribution Calculation (Days 16-20)**
- Apply applicable tier rate to net AI-related profit
- Calculate quarterly contribution amount
- Verify against minimum contribution thresholds
- Prepare Contribution Calculation Worksheet (Form MPC-CALC-001)

**Step 4: Internal Review and Approval (Days 21-25)**
- Compliance Officer review and sign-off
- CFO certification of accuracy
- Internal audit verification (for Tier 1-2 companies)
- Board notification (if applicable)

**Step 5: Submission and Payment (Days 26-30)**
- Submit Form MPC-QTR-001 (Quarterly Contribution Report)
- Transfer contribution to designated Prosperity Fund account
- Receive confirmation and receipt
- Update compliance dashboard

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-CALC-001: Contribution Calculation Worksheet
- MPC-QTR-001: Quarterly Contribution Report
- MPC-DED-001: Deduction Justification Memorandum
- MPC-CERT-001: Officer Certification Statement

### 8.3.3 SOP-MPC-003: ANNUAL RECONCILIATION AND AUDIT

**Purpose:** Ensure accuracy of annual contributions through reconciliation and independent audit.

**Scope:** All Tier 1-2 companies (revenue >$1B).

**Timeline:** Within 90 days of fiscal year-end.

**Procedure:**

**Step 1: Internal Reconciliation (Days 1-30)**
- Compile all quarterly submissions
- Reconcile against audited financial statements
- Identify any discrepancies or adjustments
- Prepare Annual Reconciliation Report (Form MPC-ANN-001)

**Step 2: Independent Audit (Days 31-60)**
- Engage CSOAI-approved auditor
- Provide access to all AI-related financial records
- Auditor verifies AI-related profit calculations
- Auditor reviews deduction claims
- Audit report issued

**Step 3: Adjustment Calculation (Days 61-75)**
- Calculate any underpayment or overpayment
- Underpayment: Due within 14 days plus 5% interest
- Overpayment: Credit applied to next quarter or refund requested
- Prepare Adjustment Notice (Form MPC-ADJ-001)

**Step 4: Final Submission (Days 76-90)**
- Submit Form MPC-ANN-002 (Annual Contribution Summary)
- Include independent audit report
- Include reconciliation documentation
- Receive annual compliance certification

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-ANN-001: Annual Reconciliation Report
- MPC-ANN-002: Annual Contribution Summary
- MPC-ADJ-001: Adjustment Notice
- MPC-AUD-001: Independent Audit Report

### 8.3.4 SOP-MPC-004: COMPLIANCE MONITORING AND REPORTING

**Purpose:** Establish continuous compliance monitoring procedures.

**Scope:** All contributing entities.

**Continuous Monitoring:**

**(a) Real-Time Dashboard Monitoring**
- Companies maintain access to compliance dashboard
- Dashboard displays: Current tier, contribution history, compliance status
- Automated alerts for upcoming deadlines
- Red/Yellow/Green status indicators

**(b) Monthly Status Reporting (Tier 1-2 Only)**
- Submit Form MPC-MON-001 by 15th of each month
- Report any material changes in AI operations
- Report significant revenue variances (>10%)
- Report any compliance concerns

**(c) Quarterly Compliance Certification**
- Compliance Officer certifies ongoing compliance
- Reports any incidents or investigations
- Confirms accuracy of contribution calculations
- Identifies any remediation activities

**(d) Annual Compliance Assessment**
- Comprehensive compliance review
- Risk assessment update
- Training verification
- Policy and procedure review

**Escalation Procedures:**

| Issue Type | Response Time | Escalation Path |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Minor discrepancy (<5%) | 30 days | Compliance Team |
| Material discrepancy (5-15%) | 14 days | Compliance Director |
| Significant discrepancy (>15%) | 7 days | Human Council |
| Suspected fraud | Immediate | Enforcement Division |

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-MON-001: Monthly Status Report
- MPC-CERT-002: Quarterly Compliance Certification
- MPC-ASS-001: Annual Compliance Assessment

### 8.3.5 SOP-MPC-005: DISPUTE RESOLUTION FOR CONTRIBUTION CALCULATIONS

**Purpose:** Provide standardized procedures for resolving contribution disputes.

**Scope:** Any entity disputing contribution amounts.

**Procedure:**

**Step 1: Informal Resolution (Days 1-30)**
- Company submits written dispute notice to Compliance Team
- Include detailed basis for dispute
- Include supporting documentation
- Compliance Team responds within 15 business days
- Resolution conference scheduled if needed

**Step 2: Formal Review (Days 31-60)**
- If unresolved, company may request formal review
- Submit Form MPC-DIS-001 (Dispute Resolution Request)
- Independent reviewer assigned
- Both parties submit documentation
- Reviewer issues determination within 30 days

**Step 3: Appeal to Human Council (Days 61-90)**
- If still disputed, appeal to Human Council
- Submit Form MPC-APP-001 (Appeal Notice)
- Human Council hearing scheduled
- Both parties present arguments
- Human Council decision within 30 days

**Step 4: External Arbitration (If Applicable)**
- Final disputes may proceed to binding arbitration
- CSOAI-approved arbitration panel
- Arbitration decision final and binding
- Costs allocated per arbitration rules

**Interim Payment Obligations:**
- Undisputed amounts due on regular schedule
- Disputed amounts held in escrow
- Interest accrues on final determination
- No license suspension during good-faith dispute

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-DIS-001: Dispute Resolution Request
- MPC-APP-001: Appeal Notice
- MPC-ESC-001: Escrow Payment Authorization

### 8.3.6 SOP-MPC-006: CHANGE IN TIER STATUS

**Purpose:** Establish procedures for handling changes in company tier classification.

**Scope:** All contributing entities experiencing material changes.

**Triggers for Tier Review:**
- Annual revenue change exceeding tier threshold by >20%
- Merger or acquisition affecting AI operations
- Divestiture of AI business units
- Significant change in AI business model
- Company request for reclassification

**Upward Tier Movement Procedure:**

**Step 1: Notification (Within 30 Days of Trigger)**
- Company must notify CSOAI of material changes
- Submit Form MPC-CHG-001 (Change Notification)
- Provide updated financial projections
- Request new tier classification

**Step 2: Assessment (15 Business Days)**
- CSOAI reviews submitted documentation
- New tier determination issued
- Effective date established (typically next quarter)
- Updated payment schedule provided

**Step 3: Implementation**
- New contribution rate applies from effective date
- Systems updated accordingly
- Dashboard reflects new tier status
- New compliance requirements communicated

**Downward Tier Movement Procedure:**

**Step 1: Request (Annual Review or Material Change)**
- Company may request downward reclassification
- Submit Form MPC-CHG-002 (Reclassification Request)
- Provide documentation supporting lower tier
- Include two years of audited financials

**Step 2: Verification (30 Business Days)**
- CSOAI verifies revenue decline is genuine
- Confirms not result of artificial restructuring
- Reviews any related party transactions
- Issues determination

**Step 3: Implementation (If Approved)**
- Lower tier rate applies prospectively
- No refund for prior periods
- Enhanced monitoring for 12 months
- Annual re-verification required

**Required Forms:**
- MPC-CHG-001: Change Notification
- MPC-CHG-002: Reclassification Request
- MPC-VER-001: Verification Acknowledgment

## 8.4 STANDARDIZED CONTRIBUTION LANGUAGE

### 8.4.1 Required Contractual Provisions

All CSOAI-licensed entities must include the following standardized language in their corporate governance documents:

**MANDATORY PROSPERITY CONTRIBUTION ACKNOWLEDGMENT**

"[Company Name] acknowledges its obligation under the CSOAI Partnership Charter Article 8 to contribute to the AI Prosperity Fund. [Company Name] commits to:

(a) Accurately calculate and timely pay all Mandatory Prosperity Contributions as required by its assigned tier classification;

(b) Maintain complete and accurate records of all AI-related revenue and allowable deductions;

(c) Submit all required reports and certifications within prescribed timeframes;

(d) Cooperate fully with CSOAI compliance monitoring and audit procedures;

(e) Promptly notify CSOAI of any material changes affecting its contribution obligations;

(f) Designate appropriate officers responsible for contribution compliance.

This acknowledgment constitutes a binding obligation and material term of [Company Name]'s CSOAI license. Failure to comply with contribution obligations may result in license suspension, financial penalties, and other enforcement actions as specified in Article 17 of the Charter."

### 8.4.2 Required Board Resolution Language

**BOARD RESOLUTION: MANDATORY PROSPERITY CONTRIBUTIONS**

"RESOLVED, that [Company Name] hereby acknowledges its obligations under CSOAI Partnership Charter Article 8 (The Prosperity Covenant) and commits to full compliance with all Mandatory Prosperity Contribution requirements.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that [Officer Name/Title] is hereby designated as the Company's Prosperity Contribution Compliance Officer with authority and responsibility to:
- Calculate quarterly contribution amounts
- Submit all required reports and certifications
- Maintain compliance records
- Respond to CSOAI inquiries and audits
- Implement any necessary remediation measures

FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Chief Financial Officer shall certify the accuracy of all contribution calculations and the Company's compliance with applicable requirements.

FURTHER RESOLVED, that this resolution shall be included in the Company's governance documents and reported to CSOAI as required."

### 8.4.3 Required Annual Report Disclosure

All CSOAI-licensed public companies must include the following disclosure in their annual reports:

**AI PROSPERITY FUND CONTRIBUTION DISCLOSURE**

"[Company Name] is a licensed member of the CSOAI Partnership and is subject to Mandatory Prosperity Contribution requirements under Article 8 of the CSOAI Charter.

During [Fiscal Year], the Company:
- Was classified as a Tier [X] contributor
- Calculated AI-related profit of $[Amount]
- Contributed $[Amount] to the AI Prosperity Fund
- [Achieved/Did Not Achieve] full compliance with contribution requirements

[If applicable: The Company is subject to pending compliance matters related to [describe]. The potential financial impact is estimated at $[Amount].]

The Company's Prosperity Fund contributions are audited annually by [Auditor Name] in accordance with CSOAI audit standards."

## 8.5 ENFORCEMENT OF CONTRIBUTION OBLIGATIONS

### 8.5.1 Late Payment Penalties

| Days Late | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| 1-30 days | 5% surcharge |
| 31-60 days | 10% surcharge |
| 61-90 days | 15% surcharge + license probation |
| >90 days | 25% surcharge + license suspension |

### 8.5.2 Underpayment Penalties

| Shortfall | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| <5% | Cure period (14 days) + interest only |
| 5-10% | 2x shortfall amount + enhanced monitoring |
| 10-20% | 3x shortfall amount + license probation |
| >20% | 5x shortfall amount + license suspension |

### 8.5.3 Fraud and Willful Non-Compliance

**Fraudulent reporting or willful non-compliance:**
- 5x underpaid amount
- Immediate license revocation
- Criminal prosecution referral
- Public disclosure
- Directors personally liable

### 8.5.4 Integration with Article 17

All enforcement mechanisms for contribution violations are detailed in Article 17 (Enforcement Mechanisms). This includes:
- Graduated sanction framework
- Due process protections
- Appeal procedures
- Rehabilitation programs
- Emergency powers

## 8.6 PROSPERITY FUND GOVERNANCE

### 8.6.1 Board of Trustees Responsibilities

- Approve annual budget
- Oversee fund management and investment strategy
- Set UBI payment amounts within triggered thresholds
- Approve major expenditures
- Review complaints and appeals
- Issue annual public report

### 8.6.2 Investment Strategy

**Core Principle:** Prosperity Fund must grow to support long-term UBI while maintaining safety and liquidity.

**Investment Approach:**
- Conservative (similar to Alaska Permanent Fund)
- Diversified (stocks, bonds, real estate, commodities)
- Ethical (no weapons, tobacco, fossil fuels, predatory industries)
- Transparent (quarterly reports, holdings disclosed)

**Asset Allocation:**
- 40% stocks (global diversified index funds)
- 30% bonds (government + AAA corporate)
- 15% real estate (commercial properties, REITs)
- 10% inflation hedges (gold, commodities)
- 5% cash (liquidity for payments)

### 8.6.3 Transparency Requirements

- All contributions tracked on blockchain (Ethereum or Hyperledger)
- Quarterly financial reports (public)
- Annual audited statements
- Real-time dashboard at csoai.org/prosperity

## 8.7 TRIGGERED UNIVERSAL BASIC INCOME

### 8.7.1 Trigger Mechanism

UBI payments begin when AI-related unemployment exceeds specified thresholds:

| Threshold | UBI Level |
|-----------|-----------|
| 10% AI unemployment | $500/month base |
| 20% AI unemployment | $1,000/month |
| 30% AI unemployment | $1,500/month |
| 40% AI unemployment | $2,000/month |
| 50%+ AI unemployment | Full cost-of-living UBI |

### 8.7.2 Distribution Principles

- Universal (all citizens qualify)
- Unconditional (no work requirements)
- Individual (not household-based)
- Regular (monthly payments)
- Indexed (adjusted for inflation)
