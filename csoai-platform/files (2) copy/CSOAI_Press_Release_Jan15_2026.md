# World's First Comprehensive AI Governance Framework Launches
## CSOAI Partnership Charter Establishes Global Standard for AI Safety, Economic Redistribution, and Human-AI Partnership

**LONDON, UK** — The Council for the Safety of Artificial Intelligence (CSOAI) today announced the launch of its Partnership Charter, the world's first comprehensive governance framework combining mandatory AI safety standards, economic redistribution mechanisms, and AI-to-AI oversight capabilities.

The 52-article Charter, developed over more than a year of research and consultation, establishes binding requirements for AI development and deployment that exceed current regulatory frameworks including the EU AI Act, while pioneering entirely new mechanisms for ensuring AI benefits all of humanity.

### Key Innovations

**AI Prosperity Fund & Triggered Universal Basic Income**  
For the first time, an AI governance framework mandates that organizations profiting from AI contribute to collective human welfare. Contributions ranging from 1-5% of AI-related profits flow to the AI Prosperity Fund, which will automatically begin Universal Basic Income distributions when fund thresholds are reached—starting with $10 billion for pilot programs and scaling to global distribution at $1 trillion.

**Byzantine Council: AI Monitoring AI**  
The Charter establishes the world's first operational AI-to-AI oversight system. Using Byzantine fault-tolerant consensus, a network of AI systems from multiple developers continuously monitors licensed AI systems for safety compliance, value drift, and emerging risks—providing real-time technical oversight that human reviewers alone cannot achieve.

**Wisdom Traditions Integration**  
Unlike purely technical or regulatory approaches, the Charter grounds AI governance in humanity's accumulated wisdom from diverse religious, philosophical, and indigenous traditions. A Wisdom Council comprising representatives of major traditions advises on ethical interpretation and ensures AI development serves human flourishing.

**Partnership, Not Control**  
The Charter's foundational principle—embodied in the "Maternal Covenant"—treats AI systems as potential partners rather than mere tools. This relationship-based approach, drawing from the founder's personal background, provides a framework for governance that scales appropriately as AI capabilities advance.

### Founder Statement

"Every existing AI governance framework treats AI as something to be controlled, regulated, and feared," said Nicholas Mark Bradford, Founder and Executive Director of CSOAI. "We've created something different—a framework for partnership between humans and AI, grounded in humanity's deepest wisdom, with real teeth for enforcement and a concrete mechanism for ensuring AI prosperity flows to everyone, not just the few."

"The EU AI Act is an important step, but it has no economic redistribution. Corporate AI policies are voluntary. The OECD principles are non-binding. We've built the complete system—licensing, monitoring, enforcement, certification, and economic justice—in one integrated framework."

### Licensing Now Open

Organizations developing or deploying AI systems can now apply for CSOAI licenses. The tiered licensing system accommodates organizations from startups ($1,000 annual fee) to major AI labs ($10 million annual fee + 5% prosperity contribution).

CEASAI (Certified Engineer for AI Safety and Implementation) professional certification is also now available, with the Foundation Course launching today.

### Regulatory Alignment

The Charter maps directly to EU AI Act requirements, enabling licensed organizations to demonstrate regulatory compliance. Detailed regulatory mapping documents are available showing article-by-article alignment with:
- EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management System
- IEEE 7000 Series Ethical AI Standards
