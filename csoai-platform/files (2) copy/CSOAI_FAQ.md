# GENERAL QUESTIONS

## What is CSOAI?

CSOAI (Council for the Safety of Artificial Intelligence) is the world's first comprehensive AI governance framework combining safety standards, economic redistribution, and human-AI partnership. We provide licensing, certification, and oversight infrastructure for responsible AI development and deployment.

## What makes CSOAI different from other AI governance frameworks?

Five features found in no other framework globally:

1. **Mandatory Prosperity Contributions → UBI**: Licensed organizations contribute 1-5% of AI profits to the AI Prosperity Fund, which distributes Universal Basic Income when thresholds are reached.

2. **Byzantine Council (AI-to-AI Monitoring)**: Real-time safety monitoring using fault-tolerant AI consensus - the first operational AI oversight system.

3. **Wisdom Traditions Integration**: Ethics grounded in humanity's accumulated wisdom from diverse religious, philosophical, and indigenous traditions.

4. **Partnership Model**: Treats AI as potential partner rather than mere tool, scaling governance appropriately as capabilities advance.

5. **Complete Governance Stack**: Only framework combining licensing + enforcement + certification + economic redistribution in one system.

## Is CSOAI a government organization?

No. CSOAI is a UK private company limited by guarantee. However, our Charter is designed to align with and exceed regulatory requirements like the EU AI Act, and we are pursuing official recognition as an approved conformity assessment body.

## When did CSOAI launch?

The CSOAI Partnership Charter became effective January 15, 2026.

# LICENSING

## Who needs a CSOAI license?

Any organization that develops or deploys AI systems commercially should obtain a CSOAI license. While currently voluntary, CSOAI certification is becoming expected by enterprise customers, insurers, and investors.

## What are the license tiers?

| Tier | AI-Related Revenue | Annual Fee | Prosperity Contribution |
|------|-------------------|------------|------------------------|
| Tier 1 (Foundation) | >$10B | $10,000,000 | 5% of AI profit |
| Tier 2 (Enterprise) | $1B-$10B | $1,000,000 | 4% of AI profit |
| Tier 3 (Growth) | $100M-$1B | $100,000 | 3% of AI profit |
| Tier 4 (Standard) | $10M-$100M | $25,000 | 2% of AI profit |
| Tier 5 (Startup) | <$10M | $1,000 | 1% of AI profit |

## How do I apply for a license?

1. Create an account at csoai.org
2. Complete the self-assessment questionnaire
3. Submit required documentation
4. Pay the applicable fee
5. Receive license upon approval

## What does a license require me to do?

- Comply with Charter safety requirements
- Submit quarterly and annual reports
- Participate in audits
- Make Prosperity Contributions
- Maintain CEASAI-certified personnel
- Report incidents promptly

## Can my license be revoked?

Yes. Licenses may be suspended or revoked for serious Charter violations, including operating prohibited AI systems, repeated non-compliance, fraud, or failure to make required contributions.

# CEASAI CERTIFICATION

## What is CEASAI?

CEASAI (Certified Engineer for AI Safety and Implementation) is our professional certification program ensuring competent oversight of AI systems. It's similar to certifications like CPA or PMP, but for AI safety.

## What certification levels exist?

| Level | Target | Requirements |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Associate | Entry-level professionals | 40-hour Foundation Course + exam |
| Professional | Practitioners (2+ years) | 80-hour Advanced Modules + case study |
| Expert | Senior leaders | 40-hour Expert Modules + oral exam + portfolio |

## How much does certification cost?

- Foundation Course: $1,500
- Associate Exam: $250
- Advanced Modules: $3,500
- Professional Exam: $500
- Expert Modules: $2,500
- Expert Exam: $1,000

Discounts available for academics, nonprofits, and developing countries (50%).

## How long does certification take?

- Associate: 2-4 weeks (40 hours study + exam)
- Professional: 2-3 months (80 hours study + case study)
- Expert: 3-6 months (40 hours + portfolio development)

## Do I need certification to work with AI?

CSOAI-licensed organizations must employ CEASAI-certified personnel based on their tier level. Even without organizational requirements, certification demonstrates expertise and commitment to AI safety.

# PROSPERITY FUND & UBI

## What is the AI Prosperity Fund?

A charitable trust that receives Prosperity Contributions from licensed organizations. When the fund reaches specified thresholds, it automatically distributes Universal Basic Income payments.

## How does Triggered UBI (TUBI) work?

| Fund Level | What Happens |
|------------|--------------|
| $10 billion | Pilot programs in high-displacement regions |
| $100 billion | Developing nation distributions |
| $1 trillion | Global distribution begins |
| $10 trillion | Full universal coverage |

## Who receives UBI payments?

Distribution criteria are determined by the Fund trustees, prioritizing those most affected by AI-driven economic displacement while working toward universal coverage.

## Does CSOAI itself contribute?

Yes. CSOAI contributes 2% of its own profits to the Prosperity Fund. We practice what we preach.

## Is this just a tax?

No. Prosperity Contributions are contractual obligations under the License Agreement, not taxes. They represent a voluntary commitment (by choosing to get licensed) to share AI-generated wealth with humanity.

# COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT

## How does CSOAI enforce the Charter?

Enforcement mechanisms include:
- Warnings and corrective action plans
- Probation periods
- Fines (up to $10M for serious violations)
- License suspension
- License revocation
- Public disclosure via Public Watchdog

## What if I disagree with an enforcement action?

The Charter includes comprehensive appeals procedures:
1. Internal review
2. Appeals Panel (independent)
3. Binding arbitration
4. Limited court review for procedural issues

## How does CSOAI verify compliance?

- Self-assessment questionnaires
- Third-party audits (frequency based on tier)
- Byzantine Council automated monitoring
- Incident reporting
- Public Watchdog community reports

## What happens to unlicensed AI operators?

Currently, licensing is voluntary. However:
- Enterprise customers increasingly require CSOAI certification
- Insurers are recognizing CSOAI compliance
- Investors view unlicensed operators as higher risk
- The Public Watchdog tracks unlicensed operators

# REGULATORY ALIGNMENT

## How does CSOAI relate to the EU AI Act?

The CSOAI Charter meets or exceeds all EU AI Act requirements. We provide article-by-article mapping showing compliance alignment. Licensed organizations can use CSOAI certification to demonstrate EU AI Act compliance.

## Is CSOAI recognized by governments?

We are actively pursuing recognition:
- UK: Engaging with DSIT for approved body status
- EU: Applying for Notified Body status under EU AI Act
- US: Engaging with NIST and state regulators
- International: Working with OECD, ISO, IEEE

## Can CSOAI license replace regulatory compliance?

CSOAI licensing demonstrates comprehensive governance that aligns with multiple regulatory frameworks. However, you remain responsible for compliance with applicable laws in your jurisdiction.

# TECHNICAL QUESTIONS

## What is the Byzantine Council?

A network of AI systems from multiple developers that monitors licensed AI systems for safety compliance using Byzantine fault-tolerant consensus. This provides real-time technical oversight that human reviewers alone cannot achieve.

## What risk levels does CSOAI recognize?

| Level | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Level 1 | Minimal risk | Spam filters, basic recommendations |
| Level 2 | Moderate risk | Customer service bots, content moderation |
| Level 3 | High risk | Healthcare AI, autonomous vehicles, hiring systems |
| Level 4 | Prohibited | Mass manipulation, social scoring, unauthorized biometrics |

Sector-specific provisions for:

- Transportation/Autonomous Vehicles
- Education AI
- Legal AI
- Employment/HR AI
- Defense/Security AI
- Critical Infrastructure AI
