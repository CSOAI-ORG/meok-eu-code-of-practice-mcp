# CEASAI STUDY GUIDE
## Preparation Guide for Certification Examinations

---

# INTRODUCTION

This study guide will help you prepare for CEASAI certification at all three levels: Associate, Professional, and Expert. Use it alongside the official course materials to maximize your chances of success.

---

# EXAMINATION OVERVIEW

## Associate Examination
- **Format:** 100 multiple-choice questions
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Passing Score:** 70% (70 correct)
- **Topics:** All five Foundation Course modules

## Professional Examination
- **Part A:** 50 MCQ + 5 short-answer (2 hours)
- **Part B:** Case study analysis (2 hours)
- **Passing Score:** 70% overall

## Expert Examination
- **Part A:** Oral examination (1 hour)
- **Part B:** Portfolio review
- **Evaluation:** Panel of 3 Expert examiners

# ASSOCIATE LEVEL STUDY GUIDE

## Module 1: Introduction to AI Systems (20% of exam)

### Key Concepts to Master

**AI Fundamentals:**
- [ ] Define AI, ML, deep learning, neural networks
- [ ] Explain supervised vs. unsupervised vs. reinforcement learning
- [ ] Describe training vs. inference
- [ ] Understand model architectures: Transformers, CNNs, RNNs

**AI Capabilities:**
- [ ] Current capabilities in language, vision, reasoning
- [ ] Known limitations: hallucination, bias, brittleness
- [ ] Emergent behaviors and their implications

**AI Ecosystem:**
- [ ] Major AI labs and their approaches
- [ ] Open vs. closed models
- [ ] Deployment platforms and tools

### Study Tips
- Review AI incident databases for real-world examples
- Understand the difference between capability and safety
- Be able to classify AI systems by type and risk

### Common Exam Topics
- Architecture comparisons
- Capability assessment
- Ecosystem mapping

---

## Module 2: AI Safety Fundamentals (20% of exam)

### Key Concepts to Master

**Alignment:**
- [ ] Define the alignment problem
- [ ] Explain inner vs. outer alignment
- [ ] Understand reward hacking and specification gaming
- [ ] Describe deceptive alignment risks

**Technical Safety:**
- [ ] Robustness: adversarial examples, distribution shift
- [ ] Interpretability: mechanistic, post-hoc, inherent
- [ ] Uncertainty quantification
- [ ] Defense mechanisms

**Operational Safety:**
- [ ] Monitoring and logging requirements
- [ ] Human oversight models (HITL, HOTL, OOTL)
- [ ] Incident response procedures
- [ ] Fail-safe design principles

### Study Tips
- Memorize the different types of AI failures
- Understand the technical-operational safety distinction
- Practice identifying safety issues in scenarios

### Common Exam Topics
- Alignment problem definitions
- Robustness testing methods
- Human oversight models

- [ ] Provable Safety requirements
- [ ] Value Uncertainty approach
- [ ] Prosperity Covenant and contribution rates

**Governance Structure (Articles 9-15):**
- [ ] Licensing tiers and requirements
- [ ] Byzantine Council: composition, function, consensus

- [ ] Public Watchdog: transparency, reporting
- [ ] Democratic participation mechanisms

**Technical Standards:**
- [ ] Risk classification (Levels 1-4)
- [ ] Requirements by risk level
- [ ] Sector-specific provisions
- [ ] Compliance assessment process

**Rights and Responsibilities:**
- [ ] Developer obligations
- [ ] Deployer obligations
- [ ] Enforcement mechanisms
- [ ] Appeals process

## Module 4: Ethics in AI (20% of exam)

### Key Concepts to Master

**Ethical Frameworks:**
- [ ] Deontological ethics (Kant, rules)
- [ ] Consequentialism (outcomes)
- [ ] Virtue ethics (character)
- [ ] Care ethics (relationships)
- [ ] How each applies to AI decisions

**AI-Specific Issues:**
- [ ] Bias: sources, types, mitigation
- [ ] Fairness: definitions and tradeoffs
- [ ] Privacy: collection, inference, surveillance
- [ ] Autonomy: manipulation, nudging
- [ ] Labor: displacement, dignity

**Wisdom Traditions:**
- [ ] Hermetic principles
- [ ] Buddhist ethics
- [ ] Christian ethics
- [ ] Islamic ethics
- [ ] Convergent insights

### Study Tips
- Be able to apply each framework to scenarios
- Understand trade-offs between fairness definitions
- Know which wisdom traditions are in Charter

### Common Exam Topics
- Framework application
- Bias identification
- Ethical dilemma analysis

- [ ] Other sectors as relevant

## Case Study Preparation

**Framework for Analysis:**
1. Identify the facts
2. Identify Charter violations
3. Assess risk level
4. Propose remediation steps
5. Consider stakeholder impacts
6. Document your reasoning

**Practice Cases:**
- Biased hiring AI
- Healthcare AI failure
- Autonomous vehicle incident
- Financial AI manipulation
- Data privacy breach

# EXAM DAY TIPS

## Before the Exam
- [ ] Get adequate sleep
- [ ] Review key numbers and frameworks
- [ ] Arrive early / test technology
- [ ] Bring required identification

## During the Exam
- [ ] Read questions carefully
- [ ] Manage time (don't get stuck)
- [ ] Answer what you know first
- [ ] Review flagged questions

## For Case Studies (Professional)
- [ ] Read scenario twice
- [ ] Outline before writing
- [ ] Address all parts of question
- [ ] Use Charter references

## For Oral Exam (Expert)
- [ ] Practice articulating your experience
- [ ] Prepare examples for key competencies
- [ ] Be ready to defend your portfolio
- [ ] Ask for clarification if needed
