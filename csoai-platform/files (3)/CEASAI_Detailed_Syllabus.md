## MODULE 1: INTRODUCTION TO AI SYSTEMS (8 Hours)

### 1.1 What is Artificial Intelligence (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define artificial intelligence and its subfields
- Distinguish between narrow AI, general AI, and superintelligence
- Understand the history and evolution of AI
- Recognize current AI capabilities and limitations

**Topics Covered:**
- Definitions: AI, ML, Deep Learning, Neural Networks
- AI taxonomy: Reactive, Limited Memory, Theory of Mind, Self-Aware
- Historical milestones: Turing, Dartmouth, AI winters, modern renaissance
- Current state of AI capabilities

**Assessment:** Quiz (20 questions)

---

### 1.2 AI System Architecture (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand how AI systems are built and deployed
- Explain the difference between training and inference
- Describe common AI architectures
- Identify key components of AI infrastructure

**Topics Covered:**
- Training pipelines: Data collection, preprocessing, model training
- Inference systems: Deployment, scaling, latency
- Architectures: Transformers, CNNs, RNNs, Diffusion models
- Infrastructure: GPUs, TPUs, cloud vs. on-premise

**Assessment:** Architecture diagram exercise

---

### 1.3 AI Capabilities and Limitations (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Assess current AI capabilities accurately
- Identify known limitations and failure modes
- Understand emergent behaviors
- Evaluate capability claims critically

**Topics Covered:**
- Language capabilities: Understanding, generation, reasoning
- Vision capabilities: Recognition, generation, analysis
- Limitation categories: Data, compute, alignment, generalization
- Emergent behaviors: In-context learning, chain-of-thought
- Capability evaluation methods

**Assessment:** Capability assessment exercise

---

### 1.4 AI Ecosystem (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Map the current AI industry landscape
- Identify major players and their approaches
- Understand open vs. closed model dynamics
- Recognize deployment platforms and tools

**Topics Covered:**
- Major labs: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI
- Model types: Proprietary vs. open-weight
- Deployment platforms: Cloud APIs, on-premise, edge
- Tools and frameworks: PyTorch, TensorFlow, HuggingFace
- Regulatory landscape overview

**Assessment:** Ecosystem mapping exercise

---

## MODULE 2: AI SAFETY FUNDAMENTALS (8 Hours)

### 2.1 Why AI Safety Matters (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Articulate the case for AI safety
- Analyze historical AI incidents and near-misses
- Categorize potential AI harms
- Evaluate existential risk arguments

**Topics Covered:**
- AI incident database: Real-world failures and harms
- Harm categories: Bias, privacy, security, autonomy, safety
- Near-term vs. long-term risks
- Existential risk perspectives: Orthodox, skeptical, nuanced
- Case studies: Facial recognition, autonomous vehicles, content moderation

**Assessment:** Incident analysis report

---

### 2.2 The Alignment Problem (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define the alignment problem
- Explain value alignment challenges
- Identify reward hacking and specification gaming
- Understand deceptive alignment risks

**Topics Covered:**
- Alignment defined: Goals, values, behavior
- Specification problems: Goodhart's law, proxy gaming
- Reward hacking examples
- Inner vs. outer alignment
- Deceptive alignment: Treacherous turns, mesa-optimizers
- Current alignment approaches

**Assessment:** Alignment scenario analysis

---

### 2.3 Technical Safety (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain key technical safety properties
- Describe robustness requirements
- Understand interpretability approaches
- Evaluate uncertainty quantification methods

**Topics Covered:**
- Robustness: Adversarial examples, distribution shift
- Interpretability: Mechanistic, post-hoc, inherent
- Uncertainty: Epistemic vs. aleatoric, calibration
- Adversarial attacks: Evasion, poisoning, backdoors
- Defense mechanisms: Adversarial training, certified defenses

**Assessment:** Technical safety audit exercise

---

### 2.4 Operational Safety (2 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design monitoring systems for AI
- Implement human oversight mechanisms
- Create incident response procedures
- Apply fail-safe design principles

**Topics Covered:**
- Monitoring: Performance, drift, anomaly detection
- Logging: Audit trails, reproducibility
- Human oversight: Human-in-the-loop, human-on-the-loop
- Incident response: Detection, containment, remediation
- Fail-safe design: Graceful degradation, kill switches

**Assessment:** Operational safety plan development
