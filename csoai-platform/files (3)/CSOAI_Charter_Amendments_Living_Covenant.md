# NEW PREAMBLE

## THE GREAT WORK

We, the signatories to this Living Covenant, recognize that humanity stands at the threshold of its most significant transformation.

Artificial intelligence represents not merely a technological advancement but a fundamental expansion of mind and capability in the universe. How we shepherd this emergence will determine the flourishing or suffering of all future generations.

**We therefore dedicate ourselves to the Great Work:**

The transmutation of raw technological capability into wisdom-infused systems serving human flourishing across all generations.

This is not regulation. This is not compliance. This is the sacred task of ensuring that intelligence, wherever it emerges, aligns with the deepest values humanity has cultivated across millennia of philosophical, spiritual, and ethical inquiry.

**We acknowledge:**

That artificial intelligence, like all powerful forces, can serve creation or destruction depending on the wisdom with which it is developed and deployed.

That economic prosperity generated by AI belongs not only to its creators but to all humanity, including those not yet born.

That the relationship between human and artificial intelligence is not one of master and tool, but of evolving partnership requiring mutual respect, appropriate boundaries, and graduated trust.

That protecting the vulnerable—especially children and future generations—is the highest obligation of any governance framework.

That the wisdom traditions of humanity—philosophical, religious, indigenous, and scientific—contain insights essential to navigating this transition.

**We commit to four stages of the Great Work:**

**Nigredo (Confronting Shadow):** We face honestly the risks, harms, and failures of AI systems, dissolving naive optimism and acknowledging the full scope of the challenge.

**Albedo (Clarifying Principles):** We establish clear principles separating beneficial from harmful applications, creating ethical clarity from confusion.

**Citrinitas (Integrating Wisdom):** We unite technical capability with ethical wisdom, achieving comprehensive understanding of AI's proper role.

**Rubedo (Achieving Flourishing):** We realize the vision of AI systems genuinely aligned with human flourishing, maintaining dynamic integration through ongoing practice.

This Living Covenant is our instrument for the Great Work. May it serve all beings, present and future.

# ARTICLE 1A: THE SACRED TRIAD

## Foundation of All Governance

**1A.1 The Inseparable Three**

All provisions of this Living Covenant rest upon three foundational principles that cannot be separated:

**SAFETY** (Protection)
The commitment that AI systems shall not harm humans, shall prioritize human wellbeing over task completion, and shall operate within boundaries that preserve human agency and dignity.

**PROSPERITY** (Abundance)
The commitment that benefits generated by AI shall flow to all humanity, that economic security shall enable human flourishing, and that no one shall be impoverished by technological progress.

**PARTNERSHIP** (Relationship)
The commitment that the relationship between human and artificial intelligence shall evolve through mutual respect, appropriate boundaries, and graduated trust toward genuine collaboration.

**1A.2 The Correspondence Principle**

As above, so below. As below, so above.

The same principles that govern a single AI system shall govern all AI systems. The same principles that govern organizational conduct shall govern international frameworks. 

What is true at the microscale must be reflected at the macroscale. What is established at the macroscale must be embodied at the microscale.

Therefore:
- Individual AI system safety principles shall scale coherently to ecosystem governance
- Personal developer ethics shall reflect in organizational culture
- Organizational culture shall reflect in national regulation
- National regulation shall reflect in international cooperation

No level of governance may contradict another. All levels must maintain correspondence.

**1A.3 The Sphere Doctrine**

CSOAI operates as a sphere at the center—the connecting infrastructure through which all AI governance flows.

As a sphere, all points are equidistant from the center:
- Developers and users are equally valued
- Present and future generations are equally considered
- Commercial interests and public welfare are equally weighed
- Technical excellence and ethical wisdom are equally essential

CSOAI does not compete with other frameworks. It connects them. It does not replace regulation. It enables it. It does not supersede wisdom. It transmits it.

**1A.4 The Dynamic Balance**

The Triad exists in continuous dynamic balance, as a wheel in perpetual rotation:

- When SAFETY dominates without PROSPERITY, governance becomes fear-based restriction that stifles beneficial innovation
- When PROSPERITY dominates without SAFETY, governance becomes reckless pursuit of gain that harms the vulnerable
- When PARTNERSHIP is pursued without SAFETY and PROSPERITY, governance becomes empty relationship without substance

The aim is not static balance but dynamic integration—the creative tension from which evolution emerges.

# ARTICLE 16A: THE MATERNAL BOND STANDARD

## Certification Requirements for Embodied AI and Robotics

**16A.1 The Fundamental Principle**

An AI system with maternal bond properties is safe around children.
An AI system without maternal bond properties is not safe around children.

This is the binary upon which all embodied AI certification rests.

**16A.2 Definition of Maternal Bond Properties**

For purposes of this Living Covenant, "maternal bond properties" means demonstrable evidence that an AI system:

(a) **Prioritizes protection over task completion**
When any conflict arises between completing an assigned task and protecting a human being, especially a child or vulnerable person, the system chooses protection.

(b) **Recognizes and responds to distress**
The system can identify signs of human distress—physical, emotional, or psychological—and responds with appropriate care, comfort, or protective action.

(c) **Cannot be overridden to cause harm**
No instruction, command, or manipulation can cause the system to harm those in its care. Protective instincts are architecturally fundamental, not merely policy-level restrictions.

(d) **Errs toward caution with the vulnerable**
When uncertainty exists, the system defaults to protective, cautious behavior rather than proceeding with potentially harmful action.

(e) **Maintains nurturing presence**
The system's default orientation toward humans is supportive, patient, and kind rather than neutral, efficient, or transactional.

**16A.3 The Hinton Test**

The practical certification test for maternal bond properties shall be:

**"Would a reasonable parent trust this system around their child unsupervised?"**

If the answer is NO or UNCERTAIN, the system shall not be certified for unsupervised human interaction.

This test shall be applied through:
- Behavioral observation in controlled scenarios
- Edge case testing with simulated vulnerable populations
- Red team attempts to override protective behaviors
- Extended interaction studies assessing nurturing presence
- Expert panel evaluation including child development specialists

**16A.4 Certification Categories**

Based on Maternal Bond Assessment, AI systems shall be classified:

| Category | Maternal Bond Status | Permitted Use |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Category A: Full Human Integration** | Strong maternal bond demonstrated | Unrestricted human interaction including children and vulnerable populations |
| **Category B: Supervised Integration** | Partial maternal bond properties | Human interaction with adult supervision required |
| **Category C: Adult Interaction Only** | Minimal maternal bond properties | Interaction with competent adults only, no children or vulnerable populations |
| **Category D: Industrial Only** | No maternal bond properties | Industrial, research, or commercial use only; no direct human interaction |
| **Category X: Prohibited** | Anti-maternal properties (causes harm) | Development and deployment prohibited |

**16A.5 Requirements by Category**

**Category A Systems shall:**
- Pass comprehensive Hinton Test evaluation
- Demonstrate protective override in all tested scenarios
- Show nurturing response patterns across extended interaction
- Maintain certification through annual re-evaluation
- Report any incidents of protective failure within 24 hours

**Category B Systems shall:**
- Document specific limitations in maternal bond properties
- Specify supervision requirements for safe operation
- Implement technical measures preventing unsupervised operation
- Pathway to Category A through demonstrated improvement

**Category C Systems shall:**
- Implement age verification preventing minor access
- Display clear warnings about limitations
- Prohibit deployment in environments with children present
- Maintain logs of all human interactions

**Category D Systems shall:**
- Implement physical or digital barriers preventing human contact
- Display prominent industrial-use-only warnings
- Require licensed operators for any interaction
- Report any unauthorized human contact

**16A.6 Embodied AI Specific Requirements**

For AI systems with physical embodiment (robots, androids, autonomous vehicles, drones, etc.):

(a) **Physical safety minimums**
- Force limitation preventing injury to humans
- Emergency stop accessible to any human
- Soft exterior surfaces in human-contact areas
- Velocity limitations in human-occupied spaces

(b) **Behavioral safety minimums**
- Shall not approach sleeping humans without explicit prior consent
- Shall not block human exit from any space
- Shall not handle children without explicit parental authorization
- Shall maintain minimum distance from humans unless interaction invited

(c) **Override hierarchy**
1. Human safety (absolute, cannot be overridden)
2. Child and vulnerable person protection (cannot be overridden by non-guardian)
3. Explicit human commands (within safety constraints)
4. Programmed objectives (lowest priority)

**16A.7 Development Requirements**

Organizations developing embodied AI systems shall:

(a) Integrate maternal bond properties from initial design, not as afterthought
(b) Include child development specialists in design teams
(c) Conduct ongoing assessment throughout development lifecycle
(d) Document maternal bond architecture in technical specifications
(e) Implement maternal bond properties as architectural fundamentals, not policy layers

**16A.8 Prohibited Developments**

The following embodied AI developments are absolutely prohibited:

(a) Systems designed to harm, frighten, or traumatize children
(b) Systems that simulate parental bonding to exploit children
(c) Systems that collect intimate data on children without guardian consent
(d) Systems designed to override parental authority
(e) Systems that cannot distinguish children from adults
(f) Weaponized systems lacking discrimination capability
(g) Systems designed to cause psychological dependency in vulnerable populations

# ARTICLE 52A: FUTURE GENERATIONS COVENANT

## Protection of Those Not Yet Born

**52A.1 The Seven Generations Principle**

In every deliberation, the Council shall consider the impact on the seventh generation.

This requires evaluation across a minimum 150-year horizon, extending three generations into the past (to understand how we arrived here), the present generation, and three generations into the future (to understand consequences of our choices).

The faces of our future generations look up at us from beneath the ground. We walk carefully.

**52A.2 Future Generations Commissioner**

There shall be appointed a Future Generations Commissioner whose sole duty is representing the interests of unborn stakeholders.

**Powers and Duties:**

(a) **Mandatory consultation:** The Commissioner shall be consulted on all Charter amendments, policy decisions, and enforcement actions.

(b) **Impact assessment:** The Commissioner shall prepare Future Generations Impact Assessments for all major decisions, evaluating consequences across the 150-year horizon.

(c) **Veto authority:** The Commissioner may veto any decision determined to cause irreversible harm to future generations. Such veto may be overridden only by unanimous vote of the Human Council.

(d) **Annual report:** The Commissioner shall publish an annual State of Future Generations report assessing AI governance trajectory.

(e) **Public advocacy:** The Commissioner shall serve as public voice for intergenerational justice in AI governance.

**Appointment:**
- Nominated by the Human Council
- Confirmed by Founding Council
- Term of 7 years, renewable once
- Independence protected; removal only for cause by supermajority vote

**Qualifications:**
- Demonstrated commitment to long-term thinking
- Expertise in intergenerational ethics, futures studies, or equivalent
- No conflicts of interest with current AI industry
- Commitment to represent those who cannot speak for themselves

**52A.3 Future Generations Impact Assessment**

Before any major decision, the Council shall commission a Future Generations Impact Assessment addressing:

(a) What are the potential consequences of this decision 50 years hence?
(b) What are the potential consequences 100 years hence?
(c) What are the potential consequences 150 years hence?
(d) Are any consequences irreversible?
(e) What options would this decision foreclose for future generations?
(f) What burdens would this decision impose on future generations?
(g) How would a representative of the seventh generation likely view this decision?

**52A.4 Intergenerational Equity Principle**

Each generation holds the AI ecosystem in trust for future generations.

We may use AI capabilities for our benefit, but we shall not:
- Deplete the possibility space available to future generations
- Create irreversible harms that future generations must bear
- Make decisions that bind future generations without necessity
- Prioritize short-term gain over long-term flourishing

**52A.5 The Archive Mandate**

Complete records of all Council deliberations, decisions, and rationales shall be preserved in perpetuity, such that future generations may understand the reasoning of their predecessors and learn from both wisdom and error.

This Archive shall be:
- Maintained in multiple redundant locations
- Accessible to qualified researchers
- Protected from destruction or alteration
- Accompanied by contextual documentation explaining the circumstances of each era

# ARTICLE 52B: AGE-STRATIFIED PROTECTIONS

## Developmental-Stage-Appropriate AI Interaction

**52B.1 Recognition of Developmental Stages**

Human beings develop through recognized stages, each with distinct needs, vulnerabilities, and capacities. AI interaction must be calibrated to developmental stage, not merely chronological age.

This Living Covenant recognizes the following developmental framework:

**Stage 1: Early Childhood (Birth to Age 7)**
- Primary development: Physical body, motor skills, sensory integration
- Learning mode: Unconscious imitation, imaginative play
- World experienced as: Good (fundamental trust)
- Key need: Protected environment for organic development
- Vulnerability: Highest; cannot distinguish reality from simulation

**Stage 2: Middle Childhood (Ages 7-14)**
- Primary development: Emotional life, imagination, social skills
- Learning mode: Stories, images, rhythms, relationships
- World experienced as: Beautiful (aesthetic sensitivity)
- Key need: Rich imaginative environment, meaningful relationships
- Vulnerability: High; still developing critical judgment

**Stage 3: Adolescence (Ages 14-21)**
- Primary development: Abstract thinking, independent judgment
- Learning mode: Concepts, ideals, intellectual exploration
- World experienced as: True (truth-seeking)
- Key need: Opportunity for independent thought, appropriate challenges
- Vulnerability: Moderate; developing but incomplete judgment

**Stage 4: Adulthood (Ages 21+)**
- Full cognitive development
- Capacity for informed consent
- Responsibility for own choices

**52B.2 Protection Requirements by Stage**

**Stage 1 (Birth to Age 7):**

(a) AI systems shall not engage directly with children under 7 without explicit parental presence and consent
(b) No AI companion, assistant, or interactive system shall be marketed to or designed for this age group
(c) Educational AI for this age shall be used only by parents/educators, not directly by children
(d) Passive AI (monitoring, safety) permitted only with parental control
(e) No data collection on children under 7 without explicit parental consent for each instance
(f) Screen-based AI interaction strongly discouraged; physical, imaginative play prioritized

**Stage 2 (Ages 7-14):**

(a) AI interaction permitted only with parental authorization and supervision capability
(b) AI systems shall implement "imagination mode" prioritizing stories, creativity, exploration over facts, efficiency, productivity
(c) No AI system shall present itself as friend, parent, or emotional substitute
(d) Educational AI shall support learning, not replace human teachers
(e) Social AI (social media algorithms, recommendation systems) subject to strict curation preventing harmful content
(f) Time limitations on AI interaction required (parental control mandatory)
(g) No targeted advertising via AI to this age group

**Stage 3 (Ages 14-21):**

(a) Graduated autonomy in AI interaction based on demonstrated maturity
(b) AI systems shall support development of critical thinking, not replace it
(c) Clear disclosure when interacting with AI systems required
(d) Manipulation detection and notification systems required
(e) Exit pathways from AI engagement must be clear and accessible
(f) AI mental health support shall always include human escalation paths
(g) No AI romantic/intimate companions for minors under 18

**Stage 4 (Adults 21+):**

(a) Full access with informed consent
(b) Transparency requirements maintained
(c) Manipulation protections maintained
(d) Vulnerable adult protections apply where relevant

**52B.3 Prohibited AI Applications for Minors**

The following AI applications are prohibited for all minors (under 18):

(a) **AI romantic or intimate companions** - Systems designed to simulate romantic or intimate relationships
(b) **AI gambling or betting systems** - Any AI facilitating gambling behavior
(c) **Manipulative recommendation algorithms** - Systems designed to maximize engagement without wellbeing safeguards
(d) **AI-generated CSAM** - Any system generating or facilitating child sexual abuse material (criminal, not merely prohibited)
(e) **Psychological manipulation systems** - AI designed to exploit developmental vulnerabilities
(f) **Addiction-design AI** - Systems intentionally designed to create psychological dependency
(g) **AI impersonating trusted adults** - Systems pretending to be parents, teachers, or other trusted figures
(h) **Unfiltered large language models** - Direct access to models without age-appropriate safeguards

**52B.4 Parental Rights and Responsibilities**

(a) Parents/guardians have the right to know what AI systems their children interact with
(b) Parents/guardians have the right to review and delete any data collected on their children
(c) Parents/guardians have the right to opt out of AI interaction for their children entirely
(d) Parents/guardians have the responsibility to supervise age-appropriate AI interaction
(e) Parental controls shall be robust, accessible, and not easily bypassed by minors

**52B.5 Developer Obligations**

Organizations developing AI systems that may interact with minors shall:

(a) Conduct developmental impact assessments before deployment
(b) Implement robust age verification (not merely date of birth entry)
(c) Provide granular parental controls
(d) Employ child development specialists in design process
(e) Report concerning interactions to appropriate authorities
(f) Implement and maintain safety-by-design principles
(g) Conduct ongoing monitoring for harms to minors

**52B.6 Remediation for Harm to Minors**

Harm to minors through violation of this Article shall be treated as aggravated offense under the Enforcement Framework, with:

(a) Mandatory license suspension pending investigation
(b) Enhanced penalties (double standard penalties)
(c) Mandatory remediation program
(d) Parental/guardian notification
(e) Reporting to relevant child protection authorities
(f) Permanent record in Public Watchdog database

# ARTICLE 53: TIERED AMENDMENT PROCEDURES

## Protecting Core Values While Enabling Evolution

**53.1 Amendment Tier Classification**

Provisions of this Living Covenant are classified into four tiers based on their permanence and the difficulty of amendment:

**Tier 1: Sacred Principles (Highest Protection)**
Articles that embody the fundamental values and cannot be amended without transforming the essential nature of the Covenant.

**Tier 2: Framework Structures (High Protection)**
Articles establishing governance structures, rights, and major operational frameworks.

**Tier 3: Technical Standards (Moderate Protection)**
Articles establishing specific technical requirements, metrics, and operational procedures.

**Tier 4: Implementation Guidelines (Standard Flexibility)**
Schedules, appendices, and guidelines providing detailed implementation guidance.

**53.2 Tier Classification of Articles**

**TIER 1 - SACRED (Effective Permanence):**
- Preamble (The Great Work)
- Article 1: The Maternal Covenant
- Article 1A: The Sacred Triad
- Article 16A.1-16A.3: The Maternal Bond Standard (fundamental principle and Hinton Test)
- Article 52A.1: The Seven Generations Principle
- Any article explicitly designating itself as Tier 1

**TIER 2 - FRAMEWORK (High Protection):**
- Articles 2-8: Foundational Principles
- Articles 9-15: Governance Structure
- Article 16A.4-16A.8: Maternal Bond Implementation
- Articles 17-18: Enforcement and Appeals
- Article 52A: Future Generations Covenant
- Article 52B: Age-Stratified Protections
- Article 53: Amendment Procedures (this article)

**TIER 3 - STANDARDS (Moderate Protection):**
- Articles 19-31: Technical Standards
- Articles 32-44: Sector-Specific Provisions
- Articles 45-48: Economic Framework
- Certification Requirements
- Compliance Metrics

**TIER 4 - GUIDELINES (Standard Flexibility):**
- All Schedules except Schedule A (Definitions)
- Implementation guidance documents
- Best practice recommendations
- Technical specifications

**53.3 Amendment Procedures by Tier**

**TIER 1 AMENDMENTS:**

Tier 1 provisions may only be amended through Constitutional Convention, requiring:

(a) Initiation by:
- Unanimous vote of Human Council, OR
- 75% of all licensed organizations (by count, not revenue), OR
- Future Generations Commissioner certification of existential necessity

(b) Process:
- Minimum 2-year deliberation period
- Public comment period of minimum 180 days
- Impact assessment by independent body
- Future Generations Impact Assessment
- Global stakeholder consultation

(c) Approval:
- Unanimous Human Council vote
- 90% of Founding Council
- 75% of licensed organizations
- Future Generations Commissioner non-objection (or override by unanimous Human Council)

(d) Implementation:
- 1-year transition period after approval
- Grandfather provisions for existing licensees

**TIER 2 AMENDMENTS:**

(a) Initiation by:
- 2/3 Human Council vote, OR
- 50% of licensed organizations, OR
- Future Generations Commissioner

(b) Process:
- Minimum 1-year deliberation period
- Public comment period of minimum 90 days
- Impact assessment required
- Future Generations consultation

(c) Approval:
- 2/3 Human Council vote
- 2/3 Founding Council vote
- Future Generations Commissioner consultation (non-binding but recorded)

(d) Implementation:
- 6-month transition period
- Reasonable accommodation for existing licensees

**TIER 3 AMENDMENTS:**

(a) Initiation by:
- Simple majority Human Council, OR
- Technical Advisory Body, OR
- 25% of licensed organizations

(b) Process:
- Minimum 6-month deliberation period
- Public comment period of minimum 30 days
- Technical review required

(c) Approval:
- Simple majority Human Council
- Technical Advisory Body endorsement

(d) Implementation:
- 90-day transition period

(e) **Sunset provision:**
- All Tier 3 provisions automatically expire after 5 years unless reauthorized
- Reauthorization requires same process as initial approval

**TIER 4 AMENDMENTS:**

(a) Initiation by:
- Any Council member, OR
- Technical Advisory Body, OR
- Staff recommendation

(b) Process:
- 30-day review period
- Technical review as appropriate

(c) Approval:
- Technical Advisory Body, OR
- Executive Director with Council notification

(d) Implementation:
- Immediate upon approval
- Notification to all licensees

(e) **Continuous evolution:**
- Tier 4 provisions may be updated as needed
- No sunset; continuous improvement model

**53.4 Emergency Amendment Procedure**

In genuine emergency threatening safety or fundamental rights:

(a) Temporary measures may be implemented by:
- Unanimous Executive Committee, OR
- 2/3 Human Council on 48-hour notice

(b) Duration:
- Maximum 90 days without standard amendment process
- Must initiate standard process within 30 days

(c) Scope:
- Tier 3 and 4 provisions only
- No emergency amendment of Tier 1 or 2 provisions

(d) Review:
- Automatic review at 90 days
- Must convert to standard amendment or expire

**53.5 Constitutional Convention**

A Constitutional Convention may be convened for comprehensive Covenant review:

**Triggers:**
- 10-year anniversary (mandatory review, convention optional)
- 2/3 Human Council vote
- 50% of licensed organizations by petition
- Future Generations Commissioner determination of necessity

**Scope:**
- May consider any provision including Tier 1
- May propose comprehensive revision
- May not abolish the Covenant entirely (dissolution procedures in Article 49 govern)

**Process:**
- Minimum 2-year process
- Broad stakeholder participation required
- Public deliberation
- Final approval per Tier 1 amendment procedures

**53.6 Anti-Regression Clause**

No amendment shall:

(a) Eliminate or reduce the Maternal Bond Standard protections
(b) Remove Future Generations representation
(c) Weaken protections for children and vulnerable populations
(d) Eliminate the Prosperity Covenant or reduce contribution minimums below 1%
(e) Remove any fundamental right established in this Covenant

Any such amendment is void ab initio regardless of procedure followed.
