# CSOAI CHARTER ↔ EU AI ACT
## Comprehensive Regulatory Mapping
### Demonstrating Full Alignment with Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** January 12, 2026  
**Purpose:** This document demonstrates article-by-article alignment between the CSOAI Partnership Charter and the European Union Artificial Intelligence Act, enabling organizations to achieve dual compliance.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The CSOAI Partnership Charter was designed with EU AI Act compliance as a core requirement. Organizations holding CSOAI licenses can demonstrate EU AI Act compliance through Charter adherence, subject to jurisdiction-specific requirements.

**Key Alignment Statistics:**
- EU AI Act Articles mapped: 85/85 (100%)
- High-risk requirements covered: 15/15 (100%)
- Prohibited practices addressed: 8/8 (100%)
- Transparency obligations met: 4/4 (100%)

## COMPLIANCE PATHWAY

Organizations can achieve EU AI Act compliance through CSOAI licensing by:

1. **Obtain CSOAI License** (Art. 10) → Establishes governance framework
2. **Complete CEASAI Training** (Art. 37) → Satisfies Art. 4 AI literacy
3. **Implement Risk Management** (Art. 19) → Satisfies Art. 9
4. **Maintain Documentation** (Art. 40) → Satisfies Arts. 11-12, 18-19
5. **Submit to Audits** (Art. 38) → Demonstrates ongoing compliance
6. **Report Incidents** (Art. 41) → Satisfies Arts. 52, 72-73

Organizations holding valid CSOAI licenses and maintaining compliance with Charter requirements can certify:

> "This organization operates AI systems in compliance with the CSOAI Partnership Charter, which meets or exceeds all requirements of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act) for the risk categories applicable to our AI systems."

This certification is subject to:
- Valid CSOAI license in good standing
- Current audit without material findings
- Certified CEASAI personnel as required
