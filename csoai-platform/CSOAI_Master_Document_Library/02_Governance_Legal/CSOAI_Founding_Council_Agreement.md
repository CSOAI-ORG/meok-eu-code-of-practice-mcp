## 2. FOUNDING COUNCIL ROLE

### 2.1 Purpose
The Founding Council provides strategic guidance, expertise, and governance oversight during CSOAI's establishment phase and ongoing operations.

### 2.2 Founding Council Composition
- Maximum 50 Founding Members
- Diverse representation: Industry, Academia, Policy, Civil Society
- Geographic diversity encouraged

---

## 3. RIGHTS & PRIVILEGES

### 3.1 As a Founding Council Member, you receive:

a) **Voting Rights**
   - Vote on Charter amendments (Article 45)
   - Vote on major governance decisions
   - Elect Human Council representatives

b) **Permanent Recognition**
   - Listed as Founding Member in perpetuity
   - Recognition on CSOAI website and materials
   - "CSOAI Founding Member" designation for personal/organizational use

c) **Fee Benefits**
   - Lifetime 50% discount on membership fees
   - Free access to all CSOAI training programs
   - Priority access to new initiatives

d) **Governance Access**
   - Direct input on Charter development
   - Early review of policy proposals
   - Access to Founding Council communications

---

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 As a Founding Council Member, you agree to:

a) **Active Participation**
   - Attend minimum 50% of Founding Council meetings (virtual acceptable)
   - Respond to governance votes within designated timeframes
   - Contribute expertise when requested

b) **Advocacy**
   - Support CSOAI's mission publicly where appropriate
   - Represent CSOAI professionally
   - Refer potential members and partners

c) **Confidentiality**
   - Protect confidential CSOAI information
   - Not disclose deliberations without consent
   - Maintain security of shared materials

d) **Conflicts of Interest**
   - Disclose any conflicts promptly
   - Recuse from decisions where conflicted
   - Act in CSOAI's best interest

---

## 5. TIME COMMITMENT

### 5.1 Expected Commitment
- Quarterly Founding Council meetings (2 hours each)
- Annual strategic planning session (half day)
- Ad-hoc consultations as needed
- Estimated total: 15-25 hours/year

### 5.2 This is a Voluntary Role
- No compensation provided
- Travel expenses for required in-person meetings covered by CSOAI (when funds permit)

---

## 6. TERM

### 6.1 Duration
- Founding Council status is permanent (lifetime)
- Active participation expectations apply for first 3 years
- After 3 years: Emeritus status available (reduced obligations, retained recognition)

### 6.2 Resignation
- Member may resign at any time with written notice
- Founding Member recognition retained unless revoked for cause

### 6.3 Removal
CSOAI may remove a Founding Council Member for:
- Conduct bringing CSOAI into disrepute
- Material breach of this Agreement
- Failure to participate for 12+ consecutive months (without approved leave)

Removal requires 2/3 vote of remaining Founding Council.

---

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 7.1 Contributions
Any contributions to CSOAI Charter, frameworks, or materials:
- Become property of CSOAI
- May be used by CSOAI without restriction
- Will be attributed where appropriate

### 7.2 Use of CSOAI IP
Founding Members may:
- Reference CSOAI membership in professional materials
- Use "CSOAI Founding Member" designation
- Share public CSOAI materials

Founding Members may NOT:
- Modify CSOAI materials without permission
- Represent personal views as CSOAI positions
- License CSOAI IP to third parties

---

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 8.1 Confidential Information Includes:
- Founding Council deliberations
- Unpublished Charter drafts
- Member information
- Financial information
- Strategic plans

### 8.2 Confidentiality Period
Obligations continue for 5 years after end of active membership.

### 8.3 Permitted Disclosures
- Public information
- Information required by law
- With CSOAI's written consent

## 9. LIABILITY

### 9.1 Good Faith Protection
Founding Council Members acting in good faith are indemnified by CSOAI against claims arising from their CSOAI role.

### 9.2 Exclusions
Indemnification does not cover:
- Willful misconduct
- Gross negligence
- Breach of this Agreement
- Criminal acts
