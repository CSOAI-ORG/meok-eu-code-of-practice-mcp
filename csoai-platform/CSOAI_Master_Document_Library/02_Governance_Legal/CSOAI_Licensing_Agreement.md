## 2. DEFINITIONS

**"AI System"** - Any artificial intelligence system submitted for CSOAI licensing.

**"License"** - Authorization to operate AI systems under CSOAI governance.

**"License Tier"** - Classification based on AI system risk level (Articles 6-9 of Charter).

**"Byzantine Council"** - CSOAI's automated monitoring infrastructure.

**"Charter"** - The CSOAI 52-article Partnership Charter.

---

## 3. LICENSE TIERS

### 3.1 Tier Classification (per Charter Articles 6-9)

| Tier | Risk Level | Examples | Review Frequency |
|------|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Tier 1** | Minimal | Spam filters, basic recommendation | Annual self-assessment |
| **Tier 2** | Limited | Customer service bots, content moderation | Annual + random audits |
| **Tier 3** | High | Healthcare AI, financial decisions, hiring | Quarterly review |
| **Tier 4** | Critical | Autonomous vehicles, infrastructure, weapons | Continuous monitoring |

### 3.2 Tier Determination
- CSOAI determines tier based on submitted documentation
- Licensee may appeal classification within 30 days
- Tier may be upgraded if risk profile changes

---

## 4. LICENSE GRANT

### 4.1 Grant of License
Upon successful assessment, CSOAI grants Licensee a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to:

a) Operate specified AI systems under CSOAI governance

b) Display CSOAI certification marks for licensed systems

c) Reference CSOAI compliance in marketing materials

d) Access CSOAI member resources and tools

### 4.2 Scope
License covers only:
- AI systems specifically assessed and approved
- Use cases disclosed in application
- Jurisdictions specified

### 4.3 New Systems
New AI systems require separate licensing application.

---

## 5. LICENSE CONDITIONS

### 5.1 Ongoing Compliance

Licensee must:

a) **Maintain Charter Compliance**
   - Adhere to all 52 articles of the CSOAI Charter
   - Implement required safety measures for tier

b) **Submit to Monitoring**
   - Tier 3-4: Connect to Byzantine Council monitoring
   - Tier 1-2: Provide access for random audits
   - All tiers: Incident reporting as required

c) **Documentation**
   - Maintain current AI system documentation
   - Update CSOAI within 30 days of material changes
   - Preserve audit trails per retention requirements

d) **Incident Reporting**
   - Critical incidents: 1 hour
   - High-risk incidents: 24 hours
   - Medium-risk incidents: 7 days
   - Low-risk incidents: 30 days

e) **Remediation**
   - Address identified issues within specified timeframes
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Report remediation completion

## 8. ASSESSMENT PROCESS

### 8.1 Initial Assessment

1. **Application Submission**
   - Complete application form
   - Technical documentation
   - Risk assessment
   - Use case descriptions

2. **Document Review**
   - CSOAI reviews within 30 days
   - May request additional information

3. **Technical Assessment**
   - Tier 1-2: Document-based
   - Tier 3-4: Technical inspection required

4. **Decision**
   - Approve (with/without conditions)
   - Defer (pending remediation)
   - Reject (with reasons)

### 8.2 Ongoing Assessment
- Scheduled reviews per tier
- Random audits
- Incident-triggered reviews

---

## 9. SUSPENSION & REVOCATION

### 9.1 Suspension
CSOAI may suspend a license for:
- Non-payment (after 60-day cure period)
- Safety incident under investigation
- Material non-compliance discovered
- Failure to remediate within timeframes

Suspension is temporary pending resolution.

### 9.2 Revocation
CSOAI may revoke a license for:
- Persistent non-compliance
- Serious safety incident with inadequate response
- Fraud or misrepresentation
- Misuse of certification marks

Revocation is permanent (reapplication may be permitted after 12 months).

### 9.3 Appeals
- Appeal within 30 days of decision
- Heard by independent Appeals Panel
- Decision is final
