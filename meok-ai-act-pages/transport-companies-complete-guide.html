EU AI Act Compliance for Transport Companies: The Complete Guide

The EU AI Act enforcement deadline of December 2, 2027 for high-risk systems (general provisions: August 2, 2026) affects every transport companies business using AI systems. This guide covers everything you need to know to achieve compliance.

What the EU AI Act Means for Transport Companies

If your transport companies business uses any AI systems — from automated scheduling to predictive analytics — you have legal obligations under the EU AI Act. The regulation classifies AI systems into four risk levels, and transport companies businesses commonly use systems that fall into the high-risk category.

Key Requirements for Transport Companies

  • AI Inventory: Document every AI system your business uses
  • Risk Classification: Classify each system as unacceptable, high-risk, limited-risk, or minimal-risk
  • Transparency: Disclose AI usage to customers and users
  • Human Oversight: Implement human review for high-risk AI decisions
  • Data Governance: Ensure training data is documented and legally sourced
  • Registration: Register high-risk AI systems in the EU database

Common AI Systems in Transport Companies

Typical transport companies businesses use AI for: AI fleet management, route optimisation, safety systems. Each of these may trigger compliance obligations.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Fines for EU AI Act violations reach up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher. For transport companies businesses, even moderate fines could be devastating.
